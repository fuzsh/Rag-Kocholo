What is roundel shape?
A roundel is a circular disc used as a symbol. The term is used in heraldry, but also commonly used to refer to a type of national insignia used on military aircraft, generally circular in shape and usually comprising concentric rings of different colours. Other symbols also often use round shapes.

Why do air forces use roundels?
The use of roundels as aircraft markings has its roots in the early days of aviation. During World War I, when aerial combat was still in its infancy, the need for quick identification between friend and foe became paramount. Simple, circular designs featuring national colors were adopted as a solution.
