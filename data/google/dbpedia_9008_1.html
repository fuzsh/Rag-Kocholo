What's the point of a pipe bomb?
A pipe bomb is an improvised explosive device (IED) that uses a tightly sealed section of pipe filled with an explosive material. The containment provided by the pipe means that simple low explosives can be used to produce a relatively large explosion due to the containment causing increased pressure .

Are pipe bombs used in war?
Pipe bombs are very easily made using gunpowder, iron, steel, aluminium or copper pipes. They are sometimes wrapped with nails to cause even more harm. This improvised weapon - first used by the Russian resistance against German tanks in the Second World War - is used by terrorists world-wide .
