What are the teachings of Jodo Shinshu?
Jodo Shinshu (also known as Shin Buddhism in the West) is a school of Japanese Pure Land Buddhism founded by Shinran Shonin (1173-1262). This tradition emphasizes salvation through faith alone rather than relying on one's own efforts to attain enlightenment .

What are the Sutras in Shin Buddhism?
Its foundation is based on three sutras (recorded sermons of Shakyamuni Buddha), the Larger Sutra of Immeasurable Life, the Smaller Sutra of Immeasurable Life (also referred to as the Amida Sutra) and the Meditation Sutra . These are the principle teachings of what is called Pure Land Buddhism.

What is the Dharma name in Jodo Shinshu?
