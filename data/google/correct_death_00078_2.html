He was the dominant figure of Canadian Confederation, and had a political career that spanned almost half a century.

When did John A. Macdonald step down?
In April 1873, Prime Minister Sir John A. Macdonald and senior members of his Conservative cabinet were accused of accepting election funds from shipping magnate Sir Hugh Allan in exchange for the contract to build the Canadian Pacific Railway. The affair forced Macdonald to resign as prime minister in November 1873 .
