What does McLeod mean in Scottish?
MacLeod, McLeod and Macleod (/məˈklaʊd/ mə-KLOWD) are surnames in the English language. The names are anglicised forms of the Scottish Gaelic MacLeòid, meaning " son of Leòd ", derived from the Old Norse Liótr ("ugly").

What is the difference between McLeod and MacLeod?
McLeod and MacLeod are the same . The Mc is an abbreviation for Mac, the Gaelic word for son. When Mac is used in combination with another name, it means “son of” that other name. MacLeod/McLeod means “Son of Leod” in the same manner that Johnson means “son of John”.
