Where are the Italian kings buried?
Upon the death of King Vittorio Emanuele II in 1878, it was decided that The Pantheon should be the burial site for the Italian kings. Vittorio Emanuele II, his son, Umberto I, and Umberto's wife Margherita, are all buried there. King Vittorio Emanuele II was the first King of Italy, reigning from 1861 until 1878.

Where are Dutch monarchs buried?
Since 1815, all the Dutch monarchs and consorts have been buried at the Nieuwe Kerk (The New Church) in Delft, located in South Holland (Zuid-Holland), the Netherlands . The Nieuwe Kerk was built in the 14th and 15th centuries and was originally a Roman Catholic Church dedicated to the Virgin Mary and Saint Ursula.
