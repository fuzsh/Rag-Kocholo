What is a wagonmaster?
A wagon master was the person hired to oversee the transportation of a group of wagons . On the American frontier, the term usually applies to the person responsible for assisting groups of immigrants or pioneers from the eastern US to the western US. Wagon masters were also hired to oversee shipments of cargo or mail.

How much did a wagon master get paid?
The wagonmaster was almost invariably a powerful man who rose from the ranks of bullwhacker on the basis of his leadership qualities. His word was law on the trail, comparable to that of a ship's captain, and his pay of $125 a month ranked him near that of an Ohio River steamboat skipper.
