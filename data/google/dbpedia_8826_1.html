What is the symbol of Barcelona?
The panot , the city's best-known symbol One of the Barcelona symbols you'll see almost everywhere is the "panot". It's a flower-shaped design, often found on the streets of the Eixample district. Its name "panot" simply means "slab" or "cobble" in Catalan, and its Spanish translation is "baldosa".

What is the original name of Barcelona?
The name Barcelona comes from the ancient Iberian Barkeno, in Ancient Greek sources as Βαρκινών, Barkinṓn; and in Latin as Barcino, Barcilonum and Barcenona. During the Middle Ages, the city was variously known as Barchinona, Barçalona, Barchelonaa, and Barchenona .

What is the logo of Barcelona?
The crest. The current crest dates from 1910, when it was chosen by competition, and since then it has only changed aesthetically. The top quarters contain the St. George's Cross, representing the patron saint of Catalonia and which is also present in the coat of arms of the city of Barcelona, and the Catalan flag .
