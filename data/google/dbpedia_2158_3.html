Ferdinand-Louis-Philippe-Charles-Henri, duke d'Orléans (born Sept. 3, 1810, Palermo—died July 13, 1842, Neuilly-sur-Seine, Fr.) was the son of Louis-Philippe of France , who succeeded to the title of duc d'Orléans when his father became king (1830). He was a noted soldier and served in Algeria from 1834 to 1836.
