Where does the name Coggeshall come from?
English (Middlesex): habitational name from Coggeshall in Essex, named from an Old English personal name Cogg + halh 'nook' . History: This name was taken to America in 1632 by John Coggeshall, who became first governor of RI, and in 1635 by John Cogswell.

Is Bradwell a village?
Bradwell is a delightful Derbyshire village containing clusters of cottages and character houses that spread up the sides of a deep gorge before opening out onto lush meadows in the Hope Valley. Bradwell's main street follows the Bradwell Brook, crossing it at one point in a narrow pinch controlled by traffic lights.
