What is the history of the VAFB?
Vandenberg was originally established in 1941 as Camp Cooke near Lompoc, Calif . The camp was an Army training center for armored and infantry troops. Transferred to the Air Force in 1957, Vandenberg began its transformation into a space and missile test facility.

Who was Vandenberg Air Force Base named after?
On 4 October 1958, Cooke AFB was redesignated as Vandenberg AFB in honor of the late General Hoyt S. Vandenberg , the second Chief of Staff of the U.S. Air Force.
