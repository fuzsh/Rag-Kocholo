How is the protagonist presented in Disabled?
The poem 'Disabled' talks of the tormented recollections and thoughts of a soldier in World War I who has lost his limbs in battle and is now confined to a wheelchair and is utterly helpless . The poem contrasts the living death he is now facing with the pleasures he once enjoyed “before he threw away his knees”.

How does the writer present the soldier in Disabled?
'Disabled' uses third-person omniscient narration to introduce the soldier through the non-descript pronoun 'he' . This further isolates the soldier as he has no identity. He is 'waiting for dark' which could metaphorically be representative of him waiting for death.

When was the poem "Disabled" written?
