Émile Vandervelde (born Jan. 25, 1866, Ixelles, Belg. —died Dec. 27, 1938, Brussels) was a Belgian statesman and a prominent figure in European socialism, who served in Belgian coalition governments from 1914 to 1937 and was influential in the peace negotiations following World War I .
