Why do you think Edmund Spenser is known as the poet's poet?
Spenser is sometimes called the "poet's poet" because so many later English poets have learned the art of versification from him . In the 19th century alone his influence may be seen in Shelley's Revolt of Islam, Byron's Childe Harold's Pilgrimage, Keats's Eve of St. Agnes, and Tennyson's The Lotos Eaters.

What is the writing style of Edmund Spenser?
Spenser used a distinctive verse form, called the Spenserian stanza , in several works, including The Faerie Queene. The stanza's main metre is iambic pentameter with a final line in iambic hexameter (having six feet or stresses, known as an Alexandrine), and the rhyme scheme is ababbcbcc.
