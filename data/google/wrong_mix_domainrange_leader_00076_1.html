What was the bloodiest battle of the Second Boer War?
The battle took place at Paardeberg, a small town in the Orange Free State. The Boers, led by Piet Cronjé, were entrenched at Paardeberg, and they were besieged by British forces, commanded by Frederick Sleigh Roberts. The battle lasted for nine days, and it was one of the bloodiest conflicts of the Second Boer War.
