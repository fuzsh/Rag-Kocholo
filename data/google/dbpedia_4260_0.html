What fishes of the class Actinopterygii can be classified as?
Actinopterygii - ray-finned fishes . The actinopterygians, or ray-finned fish, are one of the two major clades of bony fish (Osteichthyes), the other being the lobe-finned fish, or Sarcopterygians. The Chondrichthyes (cartilaginous fish) are the extant sister clade of the Osteichthyes.

What fossils are Actinopterygii?
The earliest known fossil actinopterygian is Andreolepis hedei , dating back 420 million years (Late Silurian), remains of which have been found in Russia, Sweden, and Estonia. Crown group actinopterygians most likely originated near the Devonian-Carboniferous boundary.

What is an example of a subclass Actinopterygii?
Actinopterygii, or ray-finned fish, make up 99% of living fish species. The other clade is lobe-finned fish, characterized by their fleshy fins. Examples of ray-finned fish include tuna, salmon, pike, trout, goldfish, koi, pufferfish, and seahorses .
