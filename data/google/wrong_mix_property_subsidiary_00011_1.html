Who is the founder of Digital Equipment Corporation?
DEC was founded in 1957 by Ken Olson and Harlan Anderson , engineers who had worked on very early machines at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). They began by building small circuit modules for laboratory use and, in 1961, released their first computer, the PDP-1.

Who bought out Digital Equipment Corporation?
DEC was the second largest computer company in the world by the 1980s, behind IBM. However, in the 1990s the company struggled to maintain its place in a rapidly changing marketplace. After several attempts to stay afloat by selling off assets to other competitors, DEC was acquired by Compaq for $9.8 billion in 1998.
