Who gets the Army Overseas Service Ribbon?
The Army Overseas Service Ribbon (OSR) was established by the Secretary of the Army on April 10, 1981. Effective August 1, 1981, the Army OSR is awarded to all members of the Active Army, Army National Guard, and Army Reserve in an active Reserve status for successful completion of overseas tours .

What is the Army Overseas Service Ribbon Alaska?
This ribbon was designed to recognize military personnel who have served outside the continental United States border (including Hawaii and Alaska). A member can receive this award after they have served 90 days or more at a foreign post and is subsequently rewarded a bronze oak leaf per ribbon.
