When was adrastea discovered?
July 8, 1979
Adrastea was discovered by David C. Jewitt and G. Edward Danielson in Voyager 2 probe photographs taken on July 8, 1979 , and received the designation S/1979 J 1. Although it appeared only as a dot, it was the first moon to be discovered by an interplanetary spacecraft.

When was Callisto moon discovered?
January 7, 1610
Callisto was discovered Jan. 7, 1610 , by Italian scientist Galileo Galilei along with Jupiter's three other largest moons: Ganymede, Europa and Io. Callisto is named for a woman turned into a bear by Zeus in Greek mythology. Zeus is identical to the Roman god Jupiter.
