What are the effects of the Nobel Prize?
The Nobel Prize effect is an observation about the adverse effects of receiving the Nobel Prize on laureates and their careers. These effects include reduced productivity, constraints in areas of work, and public perception of expertise in areas unrelated to the laureate's work .
