What impact did William McGuffey have on education?
McGuffey's Readers were among the first textbooks in America that were designed to become progressively more challenging with each volume . They used word repetition in the text as a learning tool, which built strong reading skills through challenging reading. Sounding-out, enunciation and accents were emphasized.

When did schools stop using McGuffey Readers?
The Eclectic Readers (commonly, but informally known as the McGuffey Readers) were a series of graded primers for grade levels 1–6. They were widely used as textbooks in American schools from the mid-19th century to the early 20th century , and are still used today in some private schools and homeschooling.
