What did Portugal trade in the 1400s?
Carrack ships created a maritime network that connected Lisbon with all of its colonies in the west and the Estado da India ('State of India') in the east, as the empire was known east of the Cape of Good Hope. Goods like gold, ivory, silk, Ming porcelain, and spices were carried and traded around the world.

What was Portugal like in the 1500s?
In the early 16th century, Portugal was the most prosperous nation in the world, thanks to its feats of navigation, exploration and conquest . From India, its ships pushed further east, reaching the Spice Islands (Indonesia) in 1512 and China in 1514.

Which of the following was conquered by the Portuguese in the early 1500s?
The Portuguese conquest of Goa occurred when the governor Afonso de Albuquerque captured the city in 1510 from the Adil Shahis. Old Goa became the capital of Portuguese India, which included territories such as Fort Manuel of Cochin, Bom Bahia, Damaon, and Chaul.

What was Portugal's main interest in exploration?
The Portuguese built an empire from 1420 onwards that was largely composed of trade centres dotted around the coasts of three continents. This colonial enterprise was driven by a search for African gold, Asian spices, and Christian kingdoms in the east .
