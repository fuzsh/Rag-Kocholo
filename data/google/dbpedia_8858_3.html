What is the historical significance of Ilkhanate?
The Ilkhanate was a semi-autonomous empire in the expansive Mongol Empire. It ruled over Persia and parts of the Middle East between 1258 and 1335. The Ilkhanate is best known for representing a period of foreign rule over Persia and for being contemporaneous with the Black Death, which killed many in the Ilkhanate .
