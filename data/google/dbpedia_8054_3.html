How does the South Korea flag look?
The Taegeukgi consists of a white background, a red and blue taegeuk circle in the center, and four black trigrams (collectively called geongongamri), one in each corner of the flag . The white background represents brightness, purity, and peace, qualities that are highly valued by the people.

What do the lines on the South Korean flag mean?
The four sagwe each have a specific meaning: starting from the top left, moving clockwise, the geon bars symbolize air/south/summer; the gam bars symbolize water/west/autumn; the gon bars symbolize Earth/north/winter; and the ri bars symbolize fire/east/spring .
