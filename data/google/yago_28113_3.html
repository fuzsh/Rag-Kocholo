Louis XI

King of France
Louis XI, called "Louis the Prudent", was King of France from 1461 to 1483. He succeeded his father, Charles VII. Louis entered into open rebellion against his father in a short-lived revolt known as the Praguerie in 1440. Wikipedia
Born : July 3, 1423, Bourges, France
Died : August 30, 1483 (age 60 years), Castle of Plessis-lez-Tours, La Riche, France
Children : Charles VIII of France , Anne of France , Joan of France, Duchess of Berry , and more
Spouse : Charlotte of Savoy (m. 1451–1483) and Margaret Stewart, Dauphine of France (m. 1436–1445)
Grandchildren : François de France , Suzanne, Duchess of Bourbon , Charles de France , and more
Siblings : Charles of Valois, Duke of Berry
Nicknames : Father of European Diplomacy, Prudent, Ragno, and more
