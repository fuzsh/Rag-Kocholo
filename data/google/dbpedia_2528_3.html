What is musical composition called?
The term "musical composition" refers to a piece of original music that has been created. It can be written for vocals or instruments or both and can be simple or very complex. Music compositions are also sometimes called opuses, pieces of music, or simply a "piece."

What refers to musical composition?
Musical composition can refer to an original piece or work of music, either vocal or instrumental, the structure of a musical piece or to the process of creating or writing a new piece of music . People who create new compositions are called composers.

What is the musical thought that a music composition is based on called?
When composing a piece of music, composers often create short, memorable musical phrases that serve as the foundation for the entire composition. These musical phrases, also known as motifs, are typically a few notes or a short sequence of musical ideas that convey a specific mood or theme.

Who is known for his musical composition called Symphonie Fantastique?
Hector Berlioz (born December 11, 1803, La Côte-Saint-André, France—died March 8, 1869, Paris) was a French composer, critic, and conductor of the Romantic period, known largely for his Symphonie fantastique (1830), the choral symphony Roméo et Juliette (1839), and the dramatic piece La Damnation de Faust (1846).
