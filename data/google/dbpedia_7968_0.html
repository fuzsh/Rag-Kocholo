Bradwell or Bradwell Juxta Coggeshall is a village and civil parish in Essex, England . It is located on the River Blackwater, approximately 5 km (3.1 mi) east of Braintree and is 19 km (12 mi) north-northeast from the county town of Chelmsford. The village is in the district and parliamentary constituency of Braintree.

What is the history of Bradwell on Sea?
History. Bradwell-on-Sea was a Saxon Shore fort in Roman times known as Othona . The Anglo-Saxons originally called it Ithancester. Saint Cedd founded a monastery within the old walls in 653, which survives as the restored Chapel of St Peter-on-the-Wall, one of the oldest churches in Britain.
