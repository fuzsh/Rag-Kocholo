Alois Jirásek (born Aug. 23, 1851, Hronov, Bohemia, Austrian Empire [now in Czech Republic]—died March 12, 1930, Prague, Czech.) was the most important Czech novelist in the period before World War I, as well as a great national figure . Jirásek was a secondary-school teacher until his retirement in 1909.
