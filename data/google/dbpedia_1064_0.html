Punjabi, which is a descendant of the Sanskrit language, belongs to the Indo-Aryan language family. It has been in use as a literary language since the 11th century. Punjabi has three distinct historical stages: Old (10th–16th centuries), Medieval (16th–19th centuries), and Modern (19th-century to the present).
