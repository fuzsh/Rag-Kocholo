What is the purpose of longitude?
Lines of longitude, also called meridians, are imaginary lines that divide the Earth. They run north to south from pole to pole, but they measure the distance east or west . The prime meridian, which runs through Greenwich, England, has a longitude of 0 degrees.

What is the significance of the 180 degrees longitude line?
The meridian at 180° longitude is commonly known as the International Date Line . As you pass the International Date Line, you either add a day (going west) or subtract a day (going east.)
