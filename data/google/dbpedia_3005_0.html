What is the field of nuclear physics?
Nuclear physics is the study of the structure of nuclei—their formation, stability, and decay . It aims to understand the fundamental nuclear forces in nature, their symmetries, and the resulting complex interactions between protons and neutrons in nuclei and among quarks inside hadrons, including the proton.

Is particle physics nuclear physics?
Particle physics grew out of nuclear physics and is the study of the elementary building blocks of matter, radiation, and their interaction. The fundamental particles include not only the hadrons but also leptons such as electrons and neutrinos. The force carriers are the photon, the gluons and W and Z bosons.
