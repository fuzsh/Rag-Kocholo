How do the Caddo people differ from other indigenous groups in Texas?
The Caddo people differ from most other American Indian groups that lived in Texas because of their territorial stability . Settlement and use of lands had great permanence: the Caddo lived and sustained themselves in the same broad forested and well-watered landscape for over 1,000 years.

How did the Caddo govern themselves?
Each individual Caddo tribe was led by a hereditary chief, or caddi, who presided over a well-defined chain of command that provided the tribe with strong, e. cient government . By 1730 both the French and the Spanish had established themselves in the Caddos' territory.
