What were the effects of the Chanak Crisis?
The Chanak Crisis fundamentally challenged the assumption that the Dominions would automatically follow Britain into war. The crisis changed the relations between the Dominions and London, paving the way for the Statute of Westminster 1931, which explicitly declared that the Dominions had the power to declare war .
