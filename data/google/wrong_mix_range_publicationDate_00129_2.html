How is Flatland a satire?
As social satire In Flatland, Abbott describes a society rigidly divided into classes . Social ascent is the main aspiration of its inhabitants, apparently granted to everyone but strictly controlled by the top of the hierarchy. Freedom is despised and the laws are cruel. Innovators are imprisoned or suppressed.

What is the peace cry in Flatland?
Women in Flatland are required by law to make a sound as a "peace-cry" while walking . This is because they have the form of extremely narrow rhombuses, easily mistaken for a line segment, and from Flatland's edge-facing two-dimensional perspective, appear as a simple point when viewed from directly ahead or behind.
