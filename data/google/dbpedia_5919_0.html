What is local government called in England?
As of May 2024, there are 317 local authorities in England, of which 21 are county councils, 164 are district councils, and 132 are single-tier authorities . Of the latter, 33 are London boroughs and 36 are metropolitan boroughs. Some single-tier councils and district councils are known as 'borough' or 'city' councils.

Is Derby City Council a local authority?
Derby City Council is the local authority for the city of Derby , in the ceremonial county of Derbyshire in the East Midlands region of England. Derby has had a council from medieval times, which has been reformed on numerous occasions.

What is a local authority in the UK?
Local councils, which is the most common type of local authority, are made up of councillors who are elected by the public in local elections . Councillors work with local people and partners, such as local businesses and other organisations, to agree and deliver on local priorities.

Where did the name Derby come from?
During its turbulent early history, Derby was also settled by the Saxons, Vikings and Normans, all of whom had a lasting impact on the area. These early settlers were likely responsible for Derby's name, with the Romans naming it Derventio and the Vikings Djúra-bý meaning 'Village of the Deer' .
