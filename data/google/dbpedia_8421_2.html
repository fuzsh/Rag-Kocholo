What is the Cyprus flag design?
On August 16, 1960, the Republic of Cyprus was proclaimed, with a national flag of neutral design. On a plain white field it bore the island in yellow silhouette with a green olive wreath (symbolizing peace) beneath .

Does Cyprus have two flags?
Cyprus has also used the flags of Greece and Turkey, which have both claimed the island at different points during its history. Cyprus has had two flags of its own since it became independent . The first was adopted in 1960 and only used for a few months before the modern flag of Cyprus replaced it.

What tree is on the Cyprus flag?
The national tree of Cyprus is the endemic small tree or high shrub golden oak (Quercus alnifolia Poech) . The genus name Quercus is derived from the ancient Latin name for the oak tree; the species has been named alnifolia due to the similarity of its leaves with that of alder (Alnus).
