What is the ancient city of Ohrid?
In Classical antiquity Ohrid was a Greek colony called Lykhnidos . Standing on a crag, it became by the 2nd century bce a post on the Via Egnatia to Bitola and Greece. It was rebuilt by the Romans after a devastating earthquake in 518 ce. Ohrid probably derives its name from Lykhnidos, via Slavic and Albanian.

What language is spoken in Ohrid?
The Ohrid dialect (Macedonian: Охридски дијалект, Ohridski dijalekt) is a member of the western and north western subgroup of the western group of dialects of the Macedonian language.
