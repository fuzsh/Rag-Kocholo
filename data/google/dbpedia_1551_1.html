What does the title supernova mean?
It is derived from the Latin word nova, meaning 'new', which refers to what appears to be a temporary new bright star . Adding the prefix "super-" distinguishes supernovae from ordinary novae, which are far less luminous.

Why is supernova research important?
A supernova (the plural is supernovae) is the explosion of a star. They are extremely important for understanding our Galaxy. They heat up the interstellar medium, distribute heavy elements throughout the Galaxy, and accelerate cosmic rays .
