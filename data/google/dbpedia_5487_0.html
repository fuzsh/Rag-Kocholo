Is magnetic tape magnetic?
Magnetic tape is a medium for magnetic storage made of a thin, magnetizable coating on a long, narrow strip of plastic film. It was developed in Germany in 1928, based on the earlier magnetic wire recording from Denmark.

What is an example of a magnetic tape?
Videotape, audiotape, and computer data tape are examples of magnetic tape. Magnetic tape may be analog or digital. It may be stored on a hub or a reel or housed in a cartridge or cassette.

What is magnetic tape best described as?
A sequential storage medium used for data collection, backup and archiving . The first electronic storage medium, magnetic tape is made of flexible plastic with one side coated with a ferromagnetic material.
