What is a subdivision of species?
In biological classification, subspecies ( pl. : subspecies) is a rank below species, used for populations that live in different areas and vary in size, shape, or other physical characteristics (morphology), but that can successfully interbreed .

What is the division of species called?
The phylum (plural, phyla) ranks below the kingdom and above the class in taxonomy. Scientists generally use the term phylum for archaea, bacteria, protists, fungi, and animals, but they substitute the term division for plants. Organisms are classified in a phylum or division based largely on general body plan.
