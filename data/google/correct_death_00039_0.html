Where did Edward Drinker Cope work?

Who was Professor Cope a paleontologist?
Edward Drinker Cope (born July 28, 1840, Philadelphia, Pa., U.S.—died April 12, 1897, Philadelphia) was a paleontologist who discovered approximately a thousand species of extinct vertebrates in the United States and led a revival of Lamarckian evolutionary theory, based largely on paleontological views .
