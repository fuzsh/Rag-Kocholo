What does Goethe mean by elective affinities?
In early nineteenth century chemistry, the phrase "elective affinities" or chemical affinities was used to describe compounds that only interacted with each other under select circumstances. Goethe used this as an organizing metaphor for marriage, and for the conflict between responsibility and passion .

What is the meaning of elective affinity?
elective affinity a feeling of sympathy, attraction, or connection to a particular person, thing, or idea . The term was originally used to refer to certain chemical processes but took on a new figurative sense after the publication in 1809 of The Elective Affinities, a novel by Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1749–1832).
