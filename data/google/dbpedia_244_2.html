What is the history of Yorkton?
Originally known as York City, the village was established 4 km northeast of its present site and was intended to serve the York colony . In 1884, with the establishment of a post office, the settlement was renamed Yorkton. With the coming of the railway in 1889, the village was moved trackside to its present site.
