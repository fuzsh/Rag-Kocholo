What are some interesting facts about William McGuffey?
McGuffey served as president of Cincinnati College (1836–39) and of Ohio University, Athens (1839–43) . He was a founder of the common school system of Ohio. In 1845 he was elected to the chair of mental and moral philosophy at the University of Virginia, Charlottesville, a position he held until his death.

How did the McGuffey readers impact education?
Compiled by educator William Holmes McGuffey, the McGuffey Readers helped to standardize English language usage in the United States and not only reflected the moral values of the country in the 19th century but also shaped them.
