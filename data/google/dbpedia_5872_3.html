Gérard Debreu

French economist and mathematician
Gérard Debreu was a French-born economist and mathematician. Best known as a professor of economics at the University of California, Berkeley, where he began work in 1962, he won the 1983 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences. Wikipedia
Born : July 4, 1921, Calais, France
Died : December 31, 2004 (age 83 years), Paris, France
Education : University of Paris (1956), University of California, Berkeley , and École normale supérieure
Influenced by : Léon Walras , Maurice Allais , and Henri Cartan
Award : Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences (1983)
Contributions : General equilibrium ; utility theory; topological methods; integration of set-valued correspondences
Books
The Theory of Value: An axiomatic analysis of economic equilibrium
1959
Mathematical Economics: Twenty Papers of Gerard Debreu
1983
