What is the Dyckia habitat?
The majority of the approximately 120 different species of Dyckia are native to central Brazil, with some found in Uruguay, Paraguay, Argentina and Bolivia. Most are found growing among rocks in warm, sunny areas ranging in altitude from sea level to 6,000 feet .

How do you identify Dyckia?
Dyckia brevifolia has short, wide green leaves with very small spines around the margins . While drought tolerant, these plants can also survive chilly and wet weather. They grow about 8 inches wide and have a beautiful small yellow flower which grows on a flower stalk.
