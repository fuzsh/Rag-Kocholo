What is WTWW?
WTWW, We Transmit World Wide, is a Christian shortwave station in Lebanon, Tennessee . It opened in 2010, and uses a 100,000-watt Continental transmitter and a Harris 50,000-watt transmitter to broadcast programming to eastern Canada, Europe, northern Africa, and the Middle East.

Why is it called Lebanon Tennessee?
Established in 1802 on an overland stagecoach route, it was named for the biblical Lebanon, which had a profusion of cedar trees, because the area's stands of juniper were mistaken for cedars by the early settlers . It developed as a trading centre for livestock and farm products.
