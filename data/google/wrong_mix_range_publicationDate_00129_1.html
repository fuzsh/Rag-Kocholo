Why did Abbott write Flatland?
Written in large part as a satire on Abbott's closed minded Victorian contemporaries , Flatland ably demonstrates the absurdity of those unwilling to admit their own ignorances, even when they scoff at the ignorance of others. Flatland is, whether intended or not, more than satire.

What is the message of Flatland?
Flatland is an exercise in interrogating one's present reality—defamiliarizing it, denaturalizing it, making it strange. Its overarching message is that the narrator's world, Flatland, is not the only world possible .

What is the point of Flatland?
Edwin Abbott wrote Flatland in 1884. In the book, Abbott created a two-dimensional world populated by various polygons as a satire of the social hierarchies of Victorian culture . In the world of Flatland, the more sides you have the higher your social status.

Who is the narrator of Flatland?
The narrator is A. Square , who is, as the name suggests, a square. As the simplest regular polygon larger than a triangle, his society places him in the lowest social rank above the middle class, which contains the equilateral triangles.
