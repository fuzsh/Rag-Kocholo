What was the first silicon graphics workstation?
In 1984, SGI released its first standalone silicon graphics workstations, the IRIS 1400 and 1500 . These workstations sported 10 MHx processors and 1.5 MB of RAM, as well as disk drives. By 1985, the IRIS 2400 was completed, a workstation that included a window manager.
