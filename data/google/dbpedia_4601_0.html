What is considered a silent film?
A silent film is a film without synchronized recorded sound (or more generally, no audible dialogue) . Though silent films convey narrative and emotion visually, various plot elements (such as a setting or era) or key lines of dialogue may, when necessary, be conveyed by the use of inter-title cards.

Was there any such thing as a silent film?
The term "Silent Film" describes the period of early cinema that lasted from its very beginnings in the late 1800s to the early 1930s . Due to technological issues, these films were released without sound and so a visual language and style of presentation developed that is still the basic grammar of movies today.

Why was there a silent film era?
In the early days of film, only moving pictures could be recorded - not the sound that went along with them . This is why all films were silent before the late 1920s.
