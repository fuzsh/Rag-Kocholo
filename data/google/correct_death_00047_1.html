Where did Asa Gray live?
From 1844 until his death at the age of 79, Asa Gray lived in Cambridge, MA , in a house on Garden Street which is now a National Historic Landmark. Such is Professor Gray's legacy that although the house is officially recorded as the 'Garden House', it is better known as the 'Asa Gray House'.

What are some fun facts about Asa Gray?
Gray was born in 1810 . He began his career as a medical doctor but found that his true passion was for plants. He studied botany under John Torrey and became the first permanent professor at the new University of Michigan in 1838; his position was the first at any U.S. institution to be exclusively devoted to botany.
