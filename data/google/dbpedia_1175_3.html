Who is considered a faculty at a university?
"Faculty" is the term for academic staff at an institution of education . In primary and secondary schools, the faculty are teachers, whereas in postsecondary institutions, professors comprise the faculty. Faculty can also include researchers, scholars and lecturers, depending on the school or university.

What is the difference between faculty and staff at a university?
Both refer to a group of people that work in a particular institute or an academic community. However, 'faculty' refers to those that have educational ranks ranging from teachers to doctors in different fields. They are responsible for the teaching. 'Staff', mainly refers to the people who manage the whole community.
