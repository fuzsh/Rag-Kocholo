Abel Gance

French film director
Abel Gance was a French film director, producer, writer and actor. A pioneer in the theory and practice of montage, he is best known for three major silent films: J'accuse, La Roue, and Napoléon. Wikipedia
Born : October 25, 1889, 18th arrondissement, Paris, France
Died : November 10, 1981 (age 92 years), 16th arrondissement, Paris, France
Spouse : Sylvie Gance (m. 1933–1978) and Marguerite Gance (m. 1922)
Awards : BAFTA Fellowship and Honorary César
Parents : Abel Flamant and Françoise Péréthon
Cause of death : Tuberculosis
