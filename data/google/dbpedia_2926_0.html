Why were bridges covered in the 1800s?
The purpose of the covering is to protect the wooden structural members from the weather . Uncovered wooden bridges typically have a lifespan of only 20 years because of the effects of rain and sun, but a covered bridge can last over 100 years. The first long-span bridges were wooden. Made from available timber, such as spruce and white pine, the bridges were flexible and strong but decayed quickly when exposed to rain and snow.
