Why was Mel Tormé called The Velvet Fog?
In 1947, he started a solo singing career. His appearances at New York's Copacabana led local disc jockey Fred Robbins to give him the nickname "The Velvet Fog" in honor of his high tenor and smooth vocal style . Tormé detested the nickname. He self-deprecatingly referred to it as "this Velvet Frog voice".
