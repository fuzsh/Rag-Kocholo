What happened to the balls in Newton's cradle?
The left ball is pulled away which lifts it, and then let go. The left ball swings back as it falls and strikes the right ball, transferring all its momentum to the right ball because they are the same mass. Transferring all the momentum implies the same velocity, which confirms all the kinetic energy.

What happens when one metal ball is pulled back causing the other balls to move in the Newtons cradle?
The collision in this Newton's cradle is elastic because both kinetic energy and momentum are conserved within the system. When the first ball is pulled back and released, it gains kinetic energy. As it collides with the stationary balls, it transfers some of its kinetic energy to them, causing them to move.
