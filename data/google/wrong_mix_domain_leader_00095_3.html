What is Denmark's foreign relations?
The foreign policy of Denmark is based on its identity as a sovereign state in Europe, the Arctic and the North Atlantic. As such its primary foreign policy focus is on its relations with other nations as a sovereign state compromising the three constituent countries: Denmark, Greenland and the Faroe Islands .

What is the relationship between Denmark and Greenland?
Despite the distance between Greenland and Denmark - about 3532 km between their capitals - Greenland has been associated with Denmark politically and culturally for a millennium . Greenland was a Danish colony until 1953, when it was redefined as a district of Denmark.
