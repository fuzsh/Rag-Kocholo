How are irregular galaxies different from other galaxies?
Irregular galaxies have no identifiable shape or structure to them . They are often chaotic in appearance, without a bulge or any trace of spiral arms. The different shapes and orientation of galaxies are a result of their history, which may have included interactions with other galaxies.

How does the irregular galaxy compare to the Milky Way?
Irregular galaxies may contain abundant amounts of gas and dust. This is not necessarily true for dwarf irregulars. Irregular galaxies are commonly small, about one tenth the mass of the Milky Way galaxy .

What is unique about irregular galaxies?
Irregular galaxies are the most unusual of galaxies. They don't seem to fit into either the spiral or elliptical galaxy categories . They don't have nice spiral arms, but they do have dark patches of gas and dust. Some irregular galaxies clearly look like two galaxies ran into each other!

What is the difference between peculiar and irregular galaxies?
Irregulars may be either spiral or barred spiral. Peculiars are not fundamentally a different type . They are simply galaxies in the act of colliding; the collision distorts their shape and makes them appear "peculiar".
