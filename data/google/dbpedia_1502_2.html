What should be written in prefix?
A prefix is a word, syllable, or letter added to the beginning of a root word to alter its meaning . For example, in the word disappear, dis- means “do the opposite,” and the root word -appear means “to be visible.” Disappear means “opposite of being visible” — to no longer be seen.

How do you give a prefix?
Adding prefixes to the base, or root, of existing words to form new words is common in English. The prefix is added in front of the base word (pre- means before) to create a new word with a different meaning . For example, if you add the prefix dis- to the word like, you get dislike—the opposite of like.
