What does d(3,2) mean in particle size?
Surface moment mean diameter, D[3,2] (“Sauter mean”): the diameter of a particle whose ratio of volume to surface area is the same as that of the complete sample . Mathematically, if V is the total volume and A is the total surface area of the sample, D3,2 = 6 * (V / A).

What is D90 in particle size analysis?
The parameter D90 should more correctly be labeled as Dv(90). It signifies the point in the size distribution, up to and including which, 90% of the total volume of material in the sample is 'contained' . For example, if the D90 is 844nm, this means that 90% of the sample has a size of 844nm or smaller.
