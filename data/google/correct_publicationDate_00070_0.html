What is the book Flatland about?
In Flatland, Abbott describes a society rigidly divided into classes . Social ascent is the main aspiration of its inhabitants, apparently granted to everyone but strictly controlled by the top of the hierarchy. Freedom is despised and the laws are cruel. Innovators are imprisoned or suppressed.
