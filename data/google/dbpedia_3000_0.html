Examples of vernacular Greek Revival continued to be built even farther west, such as in Charles City, Iowa.

What was the Greek revival architecture in the South?
CHARACTERISTICS OF SOUTHERN GREEK REVIVAL ARCHITECTURE The typical southern Greek Revival home was a two-story box-like structure nearly square in plan . Three or four sides were fixed with a parade of colonnades that supported a three-member entablature. A very flat-pitched roof commonly covered the house and portico.
