The story's original title in French is Notre Dame de Paris, which highlights the monumental, historical and cultural importance of the Cathedral.

What is the moral of The Hunchback of Notre Dame?
People are primarily judged on their appearances, rather than their internal characters , in The Hunchback of Notre Dame. Victor Hugo argues, however, that appearances can be deceiving, and that judging people based on how they look can often cause people to be treated unfairly and even ostracized by society.

Why is The Hunchback of Notre Dame a tragedy?
(1) The Hunchback of Notre Dame follows the pattern of a tragedy quite closely. (2) It contains a tragic hero with a flaw that brought him to his own misery and death . (3) As with other tragedies, Quasimodo's misfortunes are largely accredited to God, the universe, or whatever caused him to be born so ugly.
