Who ruled Lucca?
From 1805 until 1814, Lucca was ruled as a principality by Élisa Baciocchi , a sister of Napoleon. Assigned by the Congress of Vienna (1815) to the Spanish infanta María Luisa, widow of the former king Louis of Etruria, it passed in 1824 to her son Charles Louis, who ceded it to Tuscany in 1847.
