Umberto II , full name Umberto Nicola Tommaso Giovanni Maria di Savoia (15 September 1904 – 18 March 1983), was the last King of Italy. He reigned for 34 days, from 9 May 1946 to 12 June 1946, although he had been de facto head of state since 1944 and was nicknamed the May King (Italian: Re di Maggio).

When did Italy last have a King?
The last king of Italy was Umberto II, son of Victor Emmanuel III. He ruled between May 9, 1946 and June 12, 1946 . Not only was Umberto's power taken away from him in the referendum that made Italy a republic, but he was also forced to leave his native country and live in exile. He died in Switzerland in 1983.
