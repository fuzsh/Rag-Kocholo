What are the characteristics of sans serif fonts?
A sans serif—or simply “sans”—is a typeface designed without serifs (from “sans”, the French word for “without”). Typically, sans serif faces have lower stroke contrast and larger x-heights than serifs . There are many sub-genres of sans serif type.
