What is the theme of Goopy Gyne Bagha Byne?
It was written by Upendrakishore Raychowdhury and contained themes of nationalism . The plot revolves around two musicians, Goopy and Bagha, who are shunned by their village and end up in the forest together, making music. They enchant the forest ghosts with their music, who promise to bestow magical powers on them.

Why are the Goopy Bagha films so important in Ray's oeuvre?
In 1961, after the revival of Sandesh, Ray began contemplating the idea of making a film based on that story, and he was partly compelled by his son Sandip to make a film which was less 'grim and adult'. This was matched by Ray's own desire to make a movie that, unlike his previous films, would cater to children .
