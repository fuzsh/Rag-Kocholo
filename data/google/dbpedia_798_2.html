Who came up with the phrase "love is blind"?
“Love is blind” was first noted clearly in Geoffrey Chaucer's 'The Merchant's Tale', a part of 'The Canterbury Tales', published in 1405. It reads: For loue is blynd alday and may nat see. There is another good example of the phrase in use in William Shakespeare's Two Gentlemen of Verona.
