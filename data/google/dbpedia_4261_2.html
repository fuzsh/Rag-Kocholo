Actinopterygii, or ray-finned fish, are the largest clade of bony fish. These fish are characterized by the presence of thin, paired fins supported by dermal rays . The other clade of bony fish are lobe-finned fish, whose fins are fleshy. Ray-finned fish make up over 50% of the planet's living vertebrate species.
