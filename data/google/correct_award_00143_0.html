Frédéric Mistral (born Sept. 8, 1830, Maillane, France—died March 25, 1914, Maillane) was a poet who led the 19th-century revival of Occitan (Provençal) language and literature. He shared the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1904 (with José Echegaray y Eizaguirre) for his contributions in literature and philology .
