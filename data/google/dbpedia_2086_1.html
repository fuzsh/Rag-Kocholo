Do US territories count as states?
A territory is an area that is overseen directly by a government, but doesn't have any sovereignty of its own. So a territory of the United States is one that is overseen by the US federal government, with no official sovereignty . This is in contrast to states, which shares sovereignty with the federal government.

What defines a US territory?
In the United States, a territory is any extent of region under the sovereign jurisdiction of the federal government of the United States, including all waters (around islands or continental tracts) . The United States asserts sovereign rights for exploring, exploiting, conserving, and managing its territory.
