When did schools stop using McGuffey Readers?
The Eclectic Readers (commonly, but informally known as the McGuffey Readers) were a series of graded primers for grade levels 1–6. They were widely used as textbooks in American schools from the mid-19th century to the early 20th century , and are still used today in some private schools and homeschooling.

What religion are the McGuffey readers?
The Readers were originally colored by William Holmes McGuffey's Presbyterian Calvinist beliefs; they included scriptural selections and guiding questions meant to inculcate moral values of industry, thrift, temperance, and kindness. They were significantly revised in 1879 and made more secular in tone.
