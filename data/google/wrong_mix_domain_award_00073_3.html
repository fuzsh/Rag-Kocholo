Who discovered the femtosecond?
The first to make femtosecond measurements was the Egyptian Nobel Laureate Ahmed Zewail , for which he was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1999. Professor Zewail used lasers to measure the movement of particles at the femtosecond scale, thereby allowing chemical reactions to be observed for the first time.
