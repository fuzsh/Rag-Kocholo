What does the god Hypnos symbolize?
Born of Nyx and Erebus, Hypnos personifies tranquility and guides both gods and mortals into peaceful sleep. His influence extends to the underworld, entwined with symbols like wings and the opium poppy .

What is the depiction of Hypnos?
Hypnos is usually depicted in ancient Greek art as a winged boy, or an adolescent with wings in his hair . The arms of the statue have been lost, but other ancient depictions in art and literature suggest that he would have been holding an opium poppy in his left hand, and pouring out sleep from a horn in his right.
