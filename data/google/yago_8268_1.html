How old is Lewes Delaware?
Founded in 1631 by Dutch colonists, it was the first white settlement along the Delaware River. Originally called Zwaanendael, the town was renamed (c. 1685) for Lewes, Sussex, England, after William Penn was granted the rights to the area. A port town, Lewes has a seafaring tradition dating back more than 300 years.
