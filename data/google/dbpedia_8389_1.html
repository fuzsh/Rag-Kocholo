Antoine-Augustin Cournot (born August 28, 1801 , Gray, France—died March 31, 1877, Paris) was a French economist and mathematician. Cournot was the first economist who, with competent knowledge of both subjects, endeavoured to apply mathematics to the treatment of economics.

Who invented Cournot model?
Cournot competition is an economic model that describes an industry structure. Rival companies offering an identical product compete on the amount of output they produce, independently and at the same time. The model is named after its founder, French mathematician Augustin Cournot .

What did Cournot contribute to economic ideas?
Cournot introduced the ideas of functions and probability into economic analysis . He derived the first formula for the rule of supply and demand as a function of price and in fact was the first to draw supply and demand curves on a graph, anticipating the work of Alfred Marshall by roughly thirty years.
