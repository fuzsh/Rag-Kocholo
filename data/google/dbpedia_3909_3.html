What is the meaning behind Kilroy was here?
“Kilroy was here”, accompanied by a cartoon drawing of a man looking over a wall, was a popular piece of graffiti drawn by American troops in the Atlantic Theater and then later in the Pacific Theater. It came to be a universal sign that American soldiers had come through an area and left their mark .

What does Kilroy mean in slang?
Kilroy is a phrase used in military slang to indicate that someone has been somewhere . The phrase originated during World War II and is associated with a graffiti doodle of a bald-headed man with a prominent nose peeking over a wall.

What is the meaning of Killroy?
