What is the music of Africa vocal form?
The basic form of African Vocal Music is CHORAL SINGING known as CALL AND RESPONSE where one singer (SOLOIST) or small group of singers sings a line and the whole group (CHORUS) makes a reply (often a fixed REFRAIN) – like a “musical conversation” – in alternation with the “lead singer”. The soloist often IMPROVISES.

How do you describe the music of Africa?
Traditional African music is rhythm-based, with the heavy use of percussion instruments and a polyrhythmic structure that uses multiple rhythms . Melodies are played on a five-notes-per-octave scale, and harmonies are polyphonic, with several melodies played simultaneously. Call and response is also popular.
