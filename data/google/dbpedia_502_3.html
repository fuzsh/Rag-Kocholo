What makes Chiapas unique?
Chiapas is home to several indigenous groups descending from the Maya, who inhabit the highland villages surrounding San Cristobal. The indigenous people of Chiapas speak their own language (often in addition to Spanish), practice their own unique customs and can be identified by their traditional dress .

How did Chiapas became part of Mexico?
Under the leadership of Catholic priest Matías Antonio de Córdoba, Chiapas declared independence from Spain in 1821; in 1824, the state joined the Mexican alliance . Chiapas' constitution was drafted in 1826, and the state was fully incorporated into Mexico in 1841.
