How did Sextans get its name?
Sextans is a faint, minor constellation on the celestial equator which was introduced in 1687 by Polish astronomer Johannes Hevelius. Its name is Latin for the astronomical sextant , an instrument that Hevelius made frequent use of in his observations. Visible at latitudes between +80° and −90°.
