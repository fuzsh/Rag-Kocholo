Why did FDR build a home in Warm Springs?
He first came to Warm Springs (formerly known as Bullochville) in 1924 for polio treatment, and liked the area so much that, as Governor of New York, he had a home built on nearby Pine Mountain. The house was finished in 1932. Roosevelt kept the house after he became President, using it as a presidential retreat.

Why is Warm Springs important?
The national prominence of the springs dates from Franklin D. Roosevelt's visit there in 1924 following an attack of polio. Convinced that the warm waters would aid in the aftercare of polio victims who needed supported exercise , Roosevelt organized the Georgia Warm Springs Foundation in 1927.
