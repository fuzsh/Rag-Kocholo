What is considered a silent film?
A silent film is a film without synchronized recorded sound (or more generally, no audible dialogue) . Though silent films convey narrative and emotion visually, various plot elements (such as a setting or era) or key lines of dialogue may, when necessary, be conveyed by the use of inter-title cards.

Was Charlie Chaplin a silent film?
All of Chaplin's films up to and including The Circus (1928) were silent , although many were re-issued with soundtracks. City Lights (1931) and Modern Times (1936) were essentially silent films, although they were made with soundtracks consisting of music and sound effects, with talking sequences in the latter film.
