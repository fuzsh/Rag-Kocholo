Collier Memorial State Park

4.7
(812) State park in Klamath County, Oregon
Directions
Website
Collier Memorial State Park is a state park in southern Oregon. The park is operated and maintained by the Oregon Parks and Recreation Department. It is located on U.S. Highway 97, approximately 30 miles north of Klamath Falls and 105 miles south of Bend. The park covers 146 acres along the Williamson River.

What is the history of Collier Seminole State Park?
The park was originally created by Barron Gift Collier to preserve the royal palms, and later the park was donated to the County. The park then served as a memorial to Barron Collier and those who fought on both sides of the Seminole Wars. In 1947, the county donated the land which became Collier-Seminole State Park.
