What is the history of Rehovot Israel?
Polish Jews founded Rehovot, one of Israel's oldest communities, in 1890 , about 20 kilometers southeast of modern Tel Aviv; Yemenite Jews joined them in 1906. In the 1990s its population swelled again, by immigration from the Soviet Union, and by Operation Solomon, an airlift of Ethiopian Jews from Africa.
