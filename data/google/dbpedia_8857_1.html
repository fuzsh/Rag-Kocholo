Was the Ilkhanate Sunni or Shia?
The Ilkhanate (1256-1335) was never Shia and was originally not Muslim. The Mongol leaders of the Ilkhanate were Buddhist for many years and generally tolerated many other religions such as Christianity. Ghazan Khan converted to Sunni Islam in 1295, yet Ghazan remained relatively tolerant of religions.

How was the Ilkhanate governed?
In the Ilkhanate, the Mongols directly administered some territories, such as much of Persia and Mesopotamia, yet they also allowed for some autonomous regions to exist , regions which had to pay extensive tribute to the Mongol powers.
