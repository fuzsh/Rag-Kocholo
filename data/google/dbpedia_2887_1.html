The letter R refers to the word “Rodalies” and S is for “Suburban”. The trains not only connect the city center with the metropolitan area but also take its passengers to nearby towns like Tarragona, a port city to the northeast of Spain and Lérida, a city to the west of Catalonia.

What is the history of the railways in Spain?
History. The first railway line in the Iberian Peninsula was built in 1848 between Barcelona and Mataró . In 1851 the Madrid-Aranjuez line was opened. In 1852 the first narrow gauge line was built; in 1863 a line reached the Portuguese border.
