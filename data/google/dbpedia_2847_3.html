Who was Nicholas Brown in the 1800s?
Nicholas Brown. Nicholas Brown (1769-1841) gave his name to Brown University. He graduated from the College of Rhode Island in 1786, and was a businessman and philanthropist based in Providence, Rhode Island . He was such an immense benefactor of his alma mater that in 1804 it was renamed Brown University in his honour.
