What is The Wages of Fear about summary?
In the South American jungle, supplies of nitroglycerine are needed at a remote oil field. The oil company pays four men to deliver the supplies in two trucks. A tense rivalry develops between the two sets of drivers on the rough remote roads where the slightest jolt can result in death.

What country are The Wages of Fear based on?
Sordid and despairing as are the director's other films, Wages of Fear was adapted from a novel by George Arnaud. Whereas Arnaud set his story in Guatemala, Clouzot's existential film takes place in an unspecified Latin American country and a fictional village, Las Piedras.
