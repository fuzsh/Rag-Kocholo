What is the last mist by Maria Luisa Bombal about?
La última niebla [The Final Mist] (1935) by María Luisa Bombal presents a female protagonist traumatized by the restrictive gender norms of 1930s Argentina . The thirty-five page novella opens with the surreptitious marriage of the upper-class protagonist to her cousin, Daniel, after the death of his first wife.

What is the final mist about?
In The Final Mist, Bombal writes about the state of alienation of both a woman and a man living under the unwritten terms of a fraudulent matrimonial pact . There is no real love between the two, only the acceptance of a situation that, in the high society to which they belong, must be kept under wraps.
