Where did the thurn und taxis come from?
Thurn and Taxis traces its origins to the Tasso family of Lombardy in northern Italy . Tasso translates to “badger”. The family relocated to Germany and changed their name to the more-or-less equivalent Taxis. Thurn (roughly, “tower”) was added to the name in the 17th century.

What was the principality of the thurn und taxis?
The Thurn and Taxis were sovereigns of a small principality, among the many Staterelles in which the Holy Roman Empire was fragmented; the principality existed until the abolition of the Empire, in 1805, and was part of the Electoral Rhine Circle .
