Did you know Tucson was once the capital of the Arizona Territory? In 1863, the U.S. Congress made Arizona a U.S. territory. By vote of the Arizona Territorial Legislature, in 1867 the territorial capital was moved from Prescott to Tucson. For 10 years from 1867 to 1877 Tucson was the capital of the Arizona Territory .

What was the old Arizona Territory?
Arizona, formerly part of the Territory of New Mexico , was organized as a separate territory on February 24, 1863. The U.S. acquired the region under the terms of the 1848 Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo and the 1853 Gadsden Purchase. Arizona became the forty-eighth state in 1912.
