José Echegaray y Eizaguirre (born April 19, 1832, Madrid, Spain—died Sept. 4, 1916, Madrid) was a mathematician, statesman, and the leading Spanish dramatist of the last quarter of the 19th century. Along with the Provençal poet Frédéric Mistral, he was awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1904 .
