Why was he called William the Lion?
The king's nickname 'the Lion' was a posthumous one and is most likely because William had chosen that animal as his heraldic badge . The design of this badge was a red lion rampant on a yellow background, and it became the emblem of Scottish monarchs thereafter; today it is known as the Royal Banner of Scotland.
