What is the premise of the White Fang?
The novel was intended as a companion piece to The Call of the Wild (1903), in which a domesticated dog reverts to a wild state. White Fang is the story of a wolf dog that is rescued from its brutal owner and gradually becomes domesticated through the patience and kindness of its new owner, Weedon Scott .

What is the theme of the White Fang?
White Fang is a classic novel that tells the story of a wild wolf-dog's journey from the harsh wilderness to domestication. Set in the Yukon Territory during the Klondike Gold Rush, the book explores themes of survival, loyalty, and the innate wildness within all creatures .

What point of view is White Fang written in?
In the White Fang book, the perspective changes from humans to wolves, and back again. London uses the third person point-of-view , also called limited omniscient, as a way to show things that are out in the wild and involve animals, and things that couldn't be understood otherwise.
