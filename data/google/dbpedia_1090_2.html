A dinghy is a type of small boat, often carried or towed by a larger vessel for use as a tender . Utility dinghies are usually rowboats or have an outboard motor. Some are rigged for sailing but they differ from sailing dinghies, which are designed first and foremost for sailing.

What's the difference between yacht and dinghy?
A “dinghy” is a small boat used to carry people out to a larger vessel . Boat people claim that a dinghy is anything 20 feet or smaller; if it's bigger they call it a tender. A “yacht” is a boat with sleeping and cooking areas on it. These are usually pretty big, but they can be any size.
