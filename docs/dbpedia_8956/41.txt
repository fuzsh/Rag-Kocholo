Sana’a is the capital of Yemen. It is considered one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It is also one of the highest capital cities in the world. Its considerable population makes it Yemen’s most populous city. The old city of Sana’a, classified as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, has a distinctive visual character due to its unique architectural characteristics, most notably expressed in its multi storeyed buildings decorated with geometric patterns. Located within the old city is the Great Mosque of Sana’a, the largest in the city. One of the most popular destinations in Sana’a is the old Salt Market, where it is possible to buy a range of goods such as salt, bread, spices, cotton and antiques.
