The Walt Whitman House, located in Camden, New Jersey, is a historic building that served as the final residence of the renowned American poet Walt Whitman. This house holds significant historical value as it was the place where Whitman spent his last years before his death.

Literary Significance of the Walt Whitman House

While residing in this house, Walt Whitman completed several poems, many of which were focused on public events. This fact underscores the house's importance as a place of creativity and literary production.

The Walt Whitman House as a Museum

The Walt Whitman House, a six-room building, is currently operated as a museum by the New Jersey Division of Parks and Forestry. The house is open to the public, allowing visitors to explore the place where Whitman lived and created his works.
