The Runaways were an all-female rock band formed in Los Angeles, California, in the mid-1970s. The group was comprised of teenage musicians, most notably Joan Jett and Lita Ford, and managed by Kim Fowley. They quickly gained recognition and became pioneers in the punk and hard rock genres, thanks to their raw, energetic style and rebellious image.

The Runaways' music often centered on themes of teenage rebellion and female empowerment, capturing the angst and vitality of youth. Their songs served as anthems for young girls wanting to break free from societal norms and assert their independence. Their legacy continues to inspire a new generation of musicians and listeners with its unapologetic and spirited approach.
