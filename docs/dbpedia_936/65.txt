Mid City is a central L.A. neighborhood a couple miles west of downtown.

“It's a very nice neighborhood that feels homey. It's close to everything—downtown, Hollywood, and the beach.” —Claudia H., resident

I-10 runs along Mid City’s southern border, connecting it to the 405 and the 110, central L.A.’s other major arteries.

“The neighbors are friendly, and we are close to all major freeways.” —Jere D., resident

“I like the friendly neighborhood feel in the middle of the city and that I'm close to so many places of interest.” —Doris D., resident

Locals have access to local bus routes and the Metro’s Purple Line along Wilshire Boulevard.

“The 1920s–1940s homes are often well-maintained and dripping with character.” —Lise L., resident

“Commercial development has helped the area, specifically at the intersection of San Vicente, Venice, and Pico.” —Mike K., resident

“I like the diverse population. There are people from all walks of life and backgrounds.” —Andy C., resident

Queen Anne is the largest park in the neighborhood. Locals come here to play tennis, baseball, soccer, and basketball.

“Mid City is a great location and is surrounded by a lot of natural beauty.” —Tatiza M., resident
