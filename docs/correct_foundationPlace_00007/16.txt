Imagine you have an unorganized list of numbers. Think of it like having a handful of differently-sized balls all mixed up. Your goal is to have the balls increase in size from one end of your hand to the other. To do this, you compare two balls right next to each other. If the ball on the right is smaller than the ball on the left, you swap them. Then, you move one ball to the right and compare those two (the second ball and the third ball). If the ball on the right (the third ball) is smaller than the ball on the left (the second ball), you swap them. You keep doing this through the whole list of balls until the end. Once you arrive at the last ball, the largest ball should be located here. Now, you go back to where you started and do the whole process again. Here's the catch - when you've already found the location for the largest ball, you no longer need to compare it with the ball before it. This means, in your second run of swapping, you don't have to go all the way to the end. You can stop one ball before the end. Now, the second-largest ball is in its rightful location. This process repeats and you go through the list over and over again, each time comparing one less ball, until you no longer have to make any more swaps. At this point, all your balls are completely organized in order of size from one end to the other. That's how bubble sort works!
