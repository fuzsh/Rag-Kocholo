Lindsay and Related Families

The main genealogy in this book concerns John Lindsay and Margaret Allan from Ayrshire, Scotland and their descendants.

Margaret Allan Lindsay and several of her children came to Canada on an earlier sailing. John Lindsay, her husband, followed on another boat but died enroute and was buried at sea in 1843. Arrangements had already been made for the family to occupy certain surveyed concessions of wooded lands in the Upper Ottawa River Valley now know as Horton Township, Renfrew County, Ontario.

The William Lindsay and Ann Campbell Families from Cambusnethan, Lanarkshire, Scotland are also presented here, as there were several intermarriages with the descendants of the above Ayrshire Lindsay Families. These Lindsay’s settled in the Ramsay Township, Lanark County, Ontario. Descendants of some of these Lindsay’s settled in White Lake, McNab Township, Renfrew, County, Ontario.

Surnames Related to the Lindsay Families

The major family surnames with Horton or McNab Township roots in this Lindsay genealogy are: KERR, McKLEM, McINNES, CAMERON, EARLY, STEWART, McLAREN, STEVENSON, CAMPBELL, O’REILLY, BROWNLEE, STORIE, WILSON, PHILLIPS, CARWELL, MILLER, McGREGOR, BARR, JAMIESON, SCOTT, FORREST, CARMICHAEL, SHAW, McARTHUR, SMITH, ROSS, WHYTE, McLEAN, EADY, DEWAR, McMILLAN. Your surname might be found within this book if your ancestors were in either the Horton or McNab Townships of Renfrew County, Ontario.
