Biography

Private Clement Hutton served in the United States Civil War.

Enlisted: 16 Sep 1861

Mustered out: 2 Oct 1861

Side: USA

Regiment(s): Michigan 2d Cavalry

Private Clement Hutton Died of Disease during Civil War.

Clement Hutton lived in Appalachia, in Mississippi.

Clement Charles Hutton, son of Reverend John Hutton and Jane Burton, was born on 14 May 1820 and baptised on 29 May 1820 in Church Lawford, Warwickshire, England.[1]

In the 1841 census Clement (age 20) was on Bath St, Leamington, Warwickshire, England.[2]

In the 1860 United States census Clement (age 38), Draper, was in Hillsdale, Hillsdale, Michigan.[3]

Clement died on 9 August 1862 of disease in the civil war in Rienzi, Alcorn, Mississippi, United States.[4] His estate passed probate on 22 March 1867 in Principal Registry.[5] Clement was buried on 11 August 1862 in Corinth National Cemetery in Corinth, Mississippi.[6]
