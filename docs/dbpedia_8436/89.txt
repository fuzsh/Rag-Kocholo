Lilongwe, the capital city of Malawi, is located in the central region of the country. As of 2018, the estimated population of Lilongwe was around 1 million people, making it the largest city in Malawi. Lilongwe serves as the administrative and political centre of Malawi, it is home to the government offices, ministries, embassies, and diplomatic missions and is a bustling hub for commerce, offering a variety of businesses, markets, and shopping centres. Lilongwe offers several attractions for visitors, including the Lilongwe Wildlife Centre, dedicated to wildlife conservation and education. Lilongwe is a melting pot of different ethnic groups, contributing to its vibrant cultural scene.
