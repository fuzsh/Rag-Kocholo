Hank Azaria, born Henry Albert Azaria on April 25, 1964, is a versatile American actor, comedian, and producer. He rose to fame by lending his voice to numerous characters in the long-running animated sitcom The Simpsons since 1989, including Moe Szyslak, Chief Wiggum, Comic Book Guy, Snake Jailbird, and formerly Apu Nahasapeemapetilon, Lou, Carl Carlson, and Bumblebee Man. Azaria's exceptional voice acting has earned him multiple accolades, including six Primetime Emmy Awards, an Annie Award, and a Screen Actors Guild Award. In addition to his voice work, Azaria has showcased his talent in live-action roles, starring in notable films such as Quiz Show (1994), The Birdcage (1996), Grosse Pointe Blank (1997), and Night at the Museum: Battle of the Smithsonian (2009), to name a few.
