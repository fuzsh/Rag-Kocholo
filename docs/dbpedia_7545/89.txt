Seychelles – the Basics

Nickname – “the land of perpetual summer”

Demonym: Seychellois, Seychelloise

Location: Indian Ocean, east of east Africa; near Madagascar; zoom in on the map above to see exactly where it is – it’s really tiny 🙂

Main cities: Mahe (MAH-heh), Victoria, La Digue (Lah-DEEG) Praslin (PRAH-lin)

Main Airport: Seychelles International Airport (SEZ); 4.5 hours from Dubai

Weather: 24° to 30 °C (75° to 86 °F) most of the year; very few storms

Famous for its luxury resorts and beautiful beaches; visited by royalty, movie stars and other famous people

UNESCO Heritage sites (must-sees for tourists):

Aldabra Atoll, four large coral islands with 152,000 giant tortoises, the world’s largest population of this reptile

Vallée de Mai Nature Reserve, a natural palm forest reserve where one can see the famous coco de mer, the world’s largest nut

Main industries: fishing, tourism, coconut and vanilla processing, coir (coconut fiber), rope, boat building

Main holidays:

National day: June 29

Assumption/La Digue festival – August 15

Best time to visit: April, May; October, November; Creole Festival is held in Mahe, Praslin & La Digue islands (23 Oct 2018 – 29 Oct 2018 )

Currency: Rupee (=0.27 AED; 0.074 USD as of Sept 2018)

Ethnic Dress

Seychellois will typically wear western clothes when they’re in the UAE. So you won’t be able to tell if someone is from Seychelles based on what they wear. But ask a Seychellois, and they’ll tell you some interesting tips on how they can spot a fellow countryman.

Back in Seychelles, you’re likely to see more traditional Seychellois dresses which are very colorful and comfortable.

Flag

The flag has a unique design with rays emanating from the lower left corner. The colors are yellow, red, white, and green, with a blue wedge at the upper left. The flag symbolizes the ocean, the link to Africa, and the multicolored nature of the population. Red, white and green are also the official colors of the SPUP (Seychelles Peoples United Party) while blue and the yellow are the colors of the country’s democratic party.
