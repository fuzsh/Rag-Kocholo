Needing a new exhibition layout, Wirral and Wigan Finescale Railway Modellers purchased a South Coast set ‘OO’ gauge project. TIM ROGERS explains how they upgraded and improved it to go on tour

RATHER than being an exact model, Newhaven Harbour is based on the infrastructure of the Newhaven and Seaford Branch in East Sussex and is intended to provide a flavour of the general location.

It reflects operations on the BR Southern Region between 1968 and 1974 at which time Newhaven was served by 27 miles of track and three stations - Harbour, Marine and Town. Passenger traffic was handled by a variety of Electric Multiple Units (EMUs) – 2-HAL, 2-BIL, 4-VEP, 4-CEP were common while it even saw 5-BEL ‘Brighton Belle’ Pullman sets on special workings. Goods traffic was predominantly bananas transported in their distinctive ‘yellow-spot’ vans. During this period the engineering department was busy filling in several tidal pools to the east of Marine station with sidings laid on the beach, and all manner of dropside open wagons were pressed into service for the work.
