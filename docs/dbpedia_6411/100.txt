Peter Masterson: The Multi-Talented Mastermind Behind Broadway and Hollywood Hits

Peter Masterson was a force to be reckoned with in the world of entertainment, boasting a career that spanned over three decades as an actor, director, producer, and writer. Best known for his work on the hit musical The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas, Masterson left an indelible mark on Broadway and Hollywood.

Early Life and Career

Born Carlos Bee Masterson Jr. on June 1, 1934, Masterson made his Broadway debut in 1967 in The Trial of Lee Harvey Oswald, playing the titular character. Although the play closed after nine performances, Masterson's talent was undeniable, and he went on to work with his cousin, writer Horton Foote, on several projects.

A Renaissance Man in Entertainment

Masterson's versatility knew no bounds, with acting credits in films like Ambush Bay (1966), In the Heat of the Night (1967), and The Exorcist (1973). He also made a mark on television, producing the ABC television movie City in Fear (1980) and directing The Trip to Bountiful (1985), which earned Geraldine Page an Academy Award for Best Actress.

Notable Works and Collaborations

Masterson's collaboration with Larry L. King on the book for The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas (1978) was a highlight of his career, leading to a successful sequel, The Best Little Whorehouse Goes Public (1994). His directing credits include Full Moon in Blue Water (1988), Night Game (1989), and Whiskey School (2005).

Awards and Honors

Masterson's work on The Trip to Bountiful earned him a nomination for the Grand Jury Prize at the Sundance Film Festival in 1986. His daughter, Mary Stuart Masterson, who often worked with him, won a Golden Globe nomination for her role in the 1991 film Fried Green Tomatoes.

Personal Milestones and Legacy

Peter Masterson's legacy extends beyond his impressive body of work. His daughter, Mary Stuart Masterson, followed in his footsteps, becoming an accomplished actress in her own right. Masterson passed away on December 18, 2018, at the age of 84, leaving behind a rich cultural heritage and a testament to his boundless creativity.

Fun Facts and Trivia

Masterson's wife, Carlin Glynn, won a Tony Award for her role in The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas.

He worked with Robert Vaughn and David Janssen on the television movie City in Fear.

Masterson's final film as a director was Whiskey School, released in 2005.

Peter Masterson's remarkable career serves as a testament to his dedication, passion, and innovative spirit. His contributions to the world of entertainment continue to inspire new generations of artists and audiences alike.
