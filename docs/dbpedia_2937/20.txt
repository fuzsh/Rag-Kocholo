Amateurish, uncouth, and obviously underdeveloped, The Toiler And The Wayfarers is Froelich's only feature. I didn't know if it's my lower-than-usual expectations or I just like the characters but this somehow worked for me despite the flaws. With the grainy B&W, the gay themes, and also the fact that it's an obscure indie effort, I can't help but think of Winter Kept Us Warm, though their plots are dissimilar. An adrift coming-of-age of sort, the film gave us a sympathetic view of these three runaways in the big city of Minneapolis and the loneliness & mishaps that come from being different, whether it be their sexuality or their nationality.
