Delirious and seductive, a fairytale twisted away from it’s usual narrative into a sinister form. Tzang Merwyn Tong’s second film is a spin on a well known Brother’s Grimm tale, The Red Riding Hood. Despite sharing the same characters and similar motivations, the film takes a rather unexpected turn. Going into it blind, one leaves either with startling amusement or cringing inwardly.

The film opens with a rather foreboding monologue by the perpetrator, a symbolic caricature of the wolf character we’re familiar with. He appeals to the open minded nature of the audience, possibly imploring us to listen to his side of the story before being too quick to cast stones.
