Bryan Forbes was a talented and versatile figure in the British cinema, serving it first as a writer and actor but producing his best work when he turned to directing with such pictures as Whistle Down the Wind and The L-Shaped Room. He became head of production at Elstree studios but his tenure ended unhappily, and, with the cinema thereafter offering him fewer opportunities, he concentrated on writing novels.

Born John Theobald Clarke in Stratford, East London, in 1926, he was educated at West Ham Secondary School, where the school magazine called him “the finest 14-year-old Shylock of his generation”.
