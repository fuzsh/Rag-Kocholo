Joseph Rudyard Kipling, born on December 30, 1865 in Mumbai, was an English journalist, short story writer, and poet. He received his education from the United Services College. Kipling is best known for his iconic short stories such as "Rikki-tikki-tavi" and "The Man Who Would Be King" as well as his poems including "Mandalay" (1890), "Kim" (1901), and "The Light That Failed." His notable awards include the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1970 and the Audie award for Audie Drama and Excellence in Production. Kipling passed away on January 18, 1936 in London, United Kingdom.
