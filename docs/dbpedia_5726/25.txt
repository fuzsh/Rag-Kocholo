No, Summer of ’42 is not based on a true story. However, it is loosely inspired by the experiences of the film’s screenwriter, Herman Raucher, who spent a summer on Nantucket Island during World War II.

The title refers to the summer of 1942 when the story takes place. It signifies a time of innocence, growth, and discovery for the main characters in the film.
