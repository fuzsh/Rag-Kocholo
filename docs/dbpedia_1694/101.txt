LOWELL - Bestselling Portuguese-American author Katherine Vaz will discuss what inspires her writing and how the process unfolds in a virtual talk via Zoom.

Hosted by the Saab Center for Portuguese Studies in partnership with the UMass Lowell Department of World Languages and Cultures, ‘Navigating Short Fiction: A Conversation with Luso-American Author Katherine Vaz’ will start at 5 p.m. Wednesday, April 3.

Vaz will touch upon some of the secrets to making stories come alive. She will also discuss how images work in stories and how to achieve great emotional impact with surprising plots, among other topics.

In addition, she will explore the particulars of ‘Our Lady of the Artichokes’ as the lead story in a collection, and its current adaptation process into a screenplay.

Vaz is the first Portuguese-American to have her work recorded by the Library of Congress.

The author of three novels and numerous collections of stories, she is a former Briggs-Copeland Fellow in Fiction at Harvard University, a Fellow of the Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study, and a recipient of a National Endowment for the Arts Fellowship.

Her newest book ‘Above the Salt’ was chosen as Book of the Week by People Magazine and is receiving rave reviews.

Her previous novel ‘Mariana’ has been selected by the Library of Congress as one of the Top 30 International Books of 1998. Her collection ‘Fado & Other Stories’ won the Drue Heinz Literature Prize, and ‘Our Lady of the Artichokes’ received the Prairie Schooner Book Award.

The virtual talk is free and open to the public.
