EDITORS COMMENTS

This engraving captures a significant moment in the life of Adolphe Thiers, the President of the French Republic. The image showcases Thiers visiting the construction site of his house at Place Saint Georges in Paris, where he is warmly welcomed by the population. The scene depicts a bustling crowd gathered around Thiers as he inspects the progress of his new residence. The artist skillfully portrays their admiration and respect for this influential statesman through their expressions and body language. It is evident that Thiers holds a special place in their hearts. The black and white composition adds an air of nostalgia to this historical snapshot, transporting us back to 1873 when this engraving was featured in "The Illustrious Universe". This piece serves as a testament to Thiers' popularity among both politicians and ordinary citizens alike. As we gaze upon this artwork from Stefano Bianchetti's private collection, we are reminded of Adolphe Thiers' lasting impact on France's political landscape during his presidency. His dedication to public service resonates even today, making him an enduring figure in French history.
