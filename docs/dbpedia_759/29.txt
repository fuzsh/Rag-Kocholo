Cost Of Attending George Washington University Law School

The cost of attending George Washington University Law School for the 2023-2024 academic year can vary depending on a number of factors, including residency status, course load, and financial aid eligibility.

For full-time students who are residents of the District of Columbia, the estimated cost of attendance for George Washington University law school tuition, fees, and living expenses is approximately $95,236.

For non-residents, the estimated George Washington University law school tuition fee and other costs increase to approximately $104,166. These figures include room and board, tuition and fees, personal expenses, books and supplies, and transportation.

However, it may be noted that these are estimated costs, and actual expenses, including George Washington University law school tuition fees, may differ based on individual circumstances.

The law school offers a variety of financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, and loans, to help students cover the cost of attendance. Students are encouraged to carefully review their financial aid options and plan accordingly to manage their educational expenses.

How Hard Is It To Get Into GW Law School?

In addition to the low acceptance rate, GW Law School also has high standards for academic achievement and personal qualifications in their applicants. The median LSAT score for the 2022-2023 entering class was 168, which is in the 93rd percentile of all test takers. The median undergraduate GPA was 3.76, indicating a high level of academic achievement.

However, the admissions committee also considers other factors beyond test scores and grades when evaluating applications. They look for evidence of leadership, community involvement, and a demonstrated commitment to public service. Strong letters of recommendation and a well-written personal statement can also make a difference in the admissions process.
