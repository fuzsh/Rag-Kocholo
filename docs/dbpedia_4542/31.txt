Biography

brevet Brigadier General Benjamin Tupper served with 6th Massachusetts Regiment (1777), Continental Army during the American Revolution.

Benjamin Tupper is an NSSAR Patriot Ancestor.

NSSAR Ancestor #: P-307852

Rank: brevet Brigadier General

Benjamin Tupper is a DAR Patriot Ancestor, A116972.

Benjamin Tupper was born on March 11, 1738 in Stoughton, Norfolk County, Massachusetts. His parents were Thomas Tupper and Remember Perry.

He married Huldah White on November 18, 1762.

Together they had these children:

Benjamin Tupper

Minerva Tupper

Anselm Tupper

Edward Tupper

Sophia Tupper

Rowena Tupper

From Historical Register of Officers of the Continental Army, Pg. 405:

Tupper, Benjamin (Mass). Major of Fellow's Massachusetts Regiment, May, 1775; Lieutenant-Colonel, 4th November, 1775; Lieutenant-Colonel 21st Continental Infantry, 1st January, 1776; Lieutenant-Colonel 2d Massachusetts, 1st November, 1776; Colonel 11th Massachusetts, 7th July, 1777; transferred to 10th Massachusetts, 1st January, 1781; transferred to 6th Massachusetts, 1st January, 1783, and served to 12th June, 1783.[1] (Died - June, 1792.)

[2] [3] [4] [5] [6] [7]

General Tupper was a valuable and founding member of The Ohio Land Company. He, along with his associates, created Marietta. . . the first settlement in Ohio.

He died on June 07, 1792 in Marietta, Washington County, Ohio.
