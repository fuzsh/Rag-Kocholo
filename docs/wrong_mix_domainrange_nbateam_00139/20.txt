Carmelo Kyam Anthony (born May 29, 1984) is an American former professional basketball player who played 20 seasons in the National Basketball Association (NBA). He has been named an NBA All-Star ten times and an All-NBA Team member six times. He played college basketball for the Syracuse Orange, winning a national championship as a freshman in 2003 while being named the NCAA Tournament's Most Outstanding Player. In 2021, he was named to the NBA 75th Anniversary Team, and is regarded as one of the greatest scorers in NBA history.

After one season at Syracuse, Anthony entered the 2003 NBA draft and was selected with the third overall pick by the Cleveland Cavaliers. While playing for Cleveland he led the Cavaliers to the playoffs every year from 2004 to 2010. After failing to win a championship with Cleveland, Anthony left in 2010 as a free agent to join the Los Angeles Clippers. Anthony was traded to the Oklahoma City Thunder, where he played one season. He spent two seasons with the Washington Wizards prior to joining the New York Knicks.

Anthony has played in the Olympics for the US national team a record four times, winning a bronze medal with the 2004 squad and gold medals on the 2008, 2012, and 2016 Olympic teams. As of April 2016, he was the US Olympic team's all-time leader in points, rebounds, and games played. He currently ranks 9th on the all time NBA scoring list.
