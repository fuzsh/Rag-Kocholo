Princess Margaret of Connaught, Crown Princess of Sweden, with her daughter Princess Ingrid, later Queen of Denmark, at Sofiero in 1914.

Princess Margaret, (1882-1920), was Crown Princess of Sweden as the first wife of the future King Gustaf VI Adolf. She was the elder daughter of Prince Arthur, Duke of Connaught, and Princess Louise Margaret of Prussia. She was known as Daisy by her family, and Margareta in Sweden.

She married Gustaf Adolf, Crown Prince of Sweden, in 1905, and the couple had five children. On 1 May 1920, Crown Princess Margaret died suddenly in Stockholm, following an operation for an ear infection. At the time she was expecting their sixth child.

Princess Ingrid was born in 1910, she was Queen of Denmark from 1947 until 1972 as the wife of King Frederick IX. Her daughters are Queen Margrethe II of Denmark, Princess Benedikte of Sayn-Wittgenstein-Berleburg and Queen Anne-Marie of Greece.

Queen Ingrid died on 7 November 2000 at Fredensborg Palace, Fredensborg, Denmark, with her three daughters and ten grandchildren at her bedside.
