Nathaniel Gorham was a member of the Continental Congress after the Revolutionary War and was on the Committee of Detail during the Constitutional Convention in . As a member of the Committee of Detail, Gorham was one of only five delegates to shape the first drafts of the Constitution based on the resolutions of the Convention.

Although Gorham had a successful political life, he entered into a risky financial venture with Oliver Phelps, purchasing millions of acres of unsettled land in New York. Unfortunately, American currency inflated in the , and Gorham failed to meet payments on the land. He went into enormous debt and lost his social standing.
