Arnold Alois Schwarzenegger is an Austrian-American actor, producer, businessman, politician, author, and former professional bodybuilder, who had served two terms as the 38th Governor of California from 2003 until 2011. He is best known for his titular role as the Terminator/T-800 in the Terminator film series, the sixth (Terminator: Dark Fate) having been released internationally by Disney following the merger of Disney and 20th Century Studios. His other well-known film roles include playing Alan "Dutch" Schaefer in Predator, Douglas Quaid/Carl Hauser in Total Recall, Harry Tasker in True Lies, and Howard Langston in Jingle All The Way.

For Disney, he portrayed Prince Hapi in Around the World in 80 Days.

He was also spoofed by Genie in Aladdin, and his Terminator role was spoofed by Genie again in the TV series. Additionally, Arnold Mousenegger in the Chip 'n Dale Rescue Rangers series was named after and based on him. He was also referenced briefly in Homeward Bound, as during the scene where Chance and Shadow are being pursued by a hungry mountain lion, Chance refers to the feline predator as "Arnold Schwarzekitty". Additionally, in Beverly Hills Chihuahua, during the scene where after Monte and his tribe of Chihuahuas save Chloe and Delgado from three mountain lions, he quotes, "Come with us if you want to live," a reference to one of Schwarzenegger's titular character's very famous lines: "Come with me if you want to live," from Terminator 2. He was also mentioned in Alexander and the Terrible, Horrible, No Good, Very Bad Day, as well as in the K.C. Undercover episode "Out of the Water and Into the Fire".

Additionally, Schwarzenegger attended the opening to Indiana Jones Adventure: Temple of the Forbidden Eye.
