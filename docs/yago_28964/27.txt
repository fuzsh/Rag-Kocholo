Louise of Mecklenburg-Strelitz (Luise Auguste Wilhelmine Amalie; 10 March 1776 – 19 July 1810) was Queen of Prussia as the wife of King Frederick William III. She was the mother of future monarchs Frederick William IV of Prussia and William I, German Emperor. The Order of Louise was founded by her husband in her memory.

Marriage[edit]

On 24 December 1793, she married Frederick William, Crown Prince of Prussia.

Issue[edit]

Frederick William IV of Prussia (15 October 1795 – 2 January 1861) married Princess Elisabeth Ludovika of Bavaria but had no issue.

William I, German Emperor (22 March 1797 – 9 March 1888) married Princess Augusta of Saxe-Weimar and had issue.

Princess Charlotte of Prussia (13 July 1798 – 1 November 1860) married Nicholas I of Russia and had issue.

Princess Friederike of Prussia (14 October 1799 – 30 March 1800) died in infancy.

Prince Karl of Prussia (29 June 1801 – 21 January 1883) married Princess Marie of Saxe-Weimar-Eisenach and had issue.

Princess Alexandrine of Prussia 23 February 1803 – 21 April 1892) married Paul Frederick, Grand Duke of Mecklenburg-Schwerin and had issue.

Prince Friedrich Jules of Prussia (13 December 1804 – 1 April 1806) died in infancy.

Princess Luise of Prussia (1 February 1808 – 6 December 1870) married Prince Frederick of the Netherlands and had issue.

Prince Albrecht of Prussia 4 October 1809 – 14 October 1872) married Princess Marianne of the Netherlands and had issue.

Titles and styles[edit]

10 March 1776 – 24 December 1793 Her Serene Highness Princess Luise of Mecklenburg-Strelitz.

24 December 1793 – 16 November 1797 Her Royal Highness The Crown Princess of Prussia.

16 November 1797 – 19 July 1810 Her Majesty The Queen.
