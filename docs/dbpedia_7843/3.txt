Established in 1995, the FH Joanneum (FH Joanneum University of Applied Sciences) is a non-profit public higher education institution located in the urban setting of the medium city of Graz (population range of 250,000-499,999 inhabitants), Styria. This institution also has a branch campus in Bad Gleichenberg and Kapfenberg. Officially recognized by the Agentur für Qualitätssicherung und Akkreditierung Austria (Agency for Quality Assurance and Accreditation Austria), FH Joanneum is a small-sized (uniRank enrollment range: 4,000-4,999 students) coeducational Austrian higher education institution. FH Joanneum offers courses and programs leading to officially recognized higher education degrees such as pre-bachelor's degrees (i.e. certificates, diplomas, associate or foundation), bachelor's degrees and master's degrees in several areas of study. See the uniRank degree levels and areas of study table below for further details. This 29-year-old Austrian higher-education institution has a selective admission policy based on entrance examinations. FH Joanneum also provides several academic and non-academic facilities and services to students including a library, sports facilities, financial aids and/or scholarships, study abroad and exchange programs, as well as administrative services.
