Paris, Feb 10 (EFE). — French Foreign Minister Laurent Fabius confirmed on Wednesday that he is leaving his post to head the Constitutional Council.

Fabius announced at his last weekly cabinet meeting that he would succeed JeanLouis Debre, who had been the president of the Constitutional Council since 2007.

Upon leaving the Elysee Palace, Fabius said “yes” to the journalists who asked him whether this was his last council of ministers, confirming the recent reports.

Fabius, who had been in office since May 2012, presided over the Paris United Nations Climate Change Conference held from Nov. 30 to Dec. 11.
