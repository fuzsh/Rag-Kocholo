Mark Woodforde (born 23 September 1965) is a tennis player from Australia. Woodforde achieved a career-high singles ranking of No. 19 reached on 22 April 1996, and a career-high doubles ranking of No. 1 reached on 16 November 1992.

Woodforde is a former world number one doubles player, most notably with his partner Todd Woodbridge, having held the rank with Woodbridge for 52 weeks from October 1996 to October 1997. He first held the number one position in November 1992, last holding it in January 2001, for a total of 83 weeks.

Woodforde played their first Davis Cup tie for Australia in 1988, with their last tie played in 2000. Woodforde has received 24 total nominations and played 24 ties. Woodforde has a singles win–loss record of 4–10, and a doubles win–loss record of 17–5, for a total win–loss of 21–15.
