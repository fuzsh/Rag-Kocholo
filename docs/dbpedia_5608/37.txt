FAQs About Larry Miller

Who is Larry Miller?

Larry Miller is an American comedian, actor, podcaster, and columnist, known for his character roles in various films and TV shows as well as his stand-up comedy.

When was he born?

He was born on October 15, 1953.

What are some of his notable film roles?

He has appeared in numerous films including Pretty Woman as Mr. Hollister, The Nutty Professor as Dean Richmond, and 10 Things I Hate About You as Walter Stratford.

Has he appeared in any TV shows?

Yes, he has appeared in several TV shows such as Mad About You as Lou Bonaparte, Boston Legal as Edwin Poole, and 10 Things I Hate About You as Walter Stratford in the TV series adaptation.

Did he have a podcast?

Yes, he hosted a weekly podcast called This Week with Larry Miller, which later was renamed The Larry Miller Show. The show concluded in December 2020.

What is his background in education?

He attended Amherst College in Massachusetts, where he majored in music before transitioning into a career in comedy.

Has he written any books?

Yes, he published a book titled Spoiled Rotten America: Outrages of Everyday Life, a collection of humorous essays that won the Audie Award for humor in 2007.

What happened to him in 2012?

In April 2012, he suffered a life-threatening brain injury after falling and hitting his head. He was in a medically-induced coma for a month but has since made a full recovery.

Is he married?

Yes, he is married to Eileen Conn, a television writer and producer. They married in 1993 and have two children.

What are some of his interests outside of acting and comedy?

Before his career in comedy, he was interested in music, playing the cello, piano, and drums. He initially worked as a musician in nightclubs before transitioning to comedy.
