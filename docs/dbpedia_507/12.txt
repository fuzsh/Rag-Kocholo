Székesfehérvár, also known as Alba Regia, is a city in central Hungary, located approximately 60 kilometers southwest of Budapest. With a population of over 97,000, it is the capital of Fejér County and is renowned for its rich history and cultural heritage. The city has a humid continental climate with warm summers and cold, snowy winters. Székesfehérvár was the first capital of Hungary from the 10th to the 16th centuries and played a significant role in the country's political and religious development. It is home to numerous historical monuments, including the Basilica of St. Stephen, the former royal palace, and the ruins of the Roman city of Gorsium.
