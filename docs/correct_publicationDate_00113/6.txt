K-PAX is a 2001 American science fiction-mystery film based on Gene Brewer's 1995 novel of the same name, directed by Iain Softley, starring Kevin Spacey, and Jeff Bridges.

K-PAX is based in a psychiatric hospital about a man (Robert Porter, from New Mexico whose life had been devastated by the murder of his wife and child) with trauma who has developed Disassociative Identity Disorder or AKA alternate/Multiple Personality; becoming “Prot,” an extraterrestrial from the planet “K Pax”.

Prot is so happy in his delusions that he almost creates a better reality (past, present and future), and becomes a mentor and teacher to fellow patients, hospital staff and clinicians. Prot with his alternative approach to the world; even has great success helping a few patients ease anxieties, give up delusions, relax anger, quit fears or drop obsessions so they can get nearer to discharge dates. Prot's true identity as a missing person "Robert Porter" of New Mexico is proven by a Sheriff. Robert Porter is unable to maintain his imagined identity and world as Prot, so he eventually collapses into a catatonic state.
