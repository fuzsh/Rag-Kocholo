Martina Navratilova Bio

Martina Navratilova is a retired Czech-American tennis player who is widely regarded as one of the greatest female athletes of all time. In 1975, she defected to the United States and continued to pursue her tennis career there. Navratilova was known for her powerful serve-and-volley style of play, as well as her athleticism and agility on the court. She won a total of 18 Grand Slam singles titles and 31 Grand Slam women's doubles titles in her career . In addition, she was the World No. 1 singles player for a total of 332 weeks, a record that still stands to this day. She retired from competitive tennis in 2006 at the age of 49. Off the court, Navratilova has been an outspoken advocate for LGBT rights and has used her platform to promote equality and acceptance for all. She came out as a lesbian in 1981 and has since been a vocal advocate for LGBTQ+ rights. In 2000, she became the first openly gay athlete to be inducted into the International Tennis Hall of Fame. In addition to her tennis career , Navratilova has also worked as a television commentator and analyst, serving as a commentator for numerous national and international tennis events. She has also written several books, including her memoir "Martina," which was released in 1985. Today, Navratilova continues to be a beloved figure in the world of tennis and an inspiration to people around the world.
