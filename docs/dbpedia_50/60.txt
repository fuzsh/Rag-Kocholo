A postal code is a string of characters that is appended to a postal address to help in mail sorting and delivery. It is also referred to as a ZIP code (in the United States) or postcode (in many other countries). Within postal systems, postal codes are essential to the proper and efficient processing of mail.

Postal codes help identify specific geographic locations, making it easier for postal services to organize and route mail. By limiting the destination to a particular area, city, or neighbourhood, these alphanumeric codes streamline the sorting process. Typically placed at the end of an address, postal employees rely on this code for crucial information to ensure timely and accurate package delivery.

Postal codes play a vital role in mail delivery and are frequently used in other administrative and logistical duties. These responsibilities include efficient distribution of goods and services, demographic analysis, and targeted marketing. As an integral part of the contemporary postal infrastructure, postal codes are essential to the efficient operation of international mail services.
