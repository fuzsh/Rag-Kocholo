Khyber Pakhtunkhwa is a releasable of Pakistan. It is located in South Asia .

Background[]

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa is a province in northwestern Pakistan. It is situated in the mountainous region of the country and shares borders with Afghanistan to the west and the Pakistani provinces of Punjab to the southeast, Gilgit-Baltistan to the northeast, and Azad Kashmir to the east.

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa has a rich historical and cultural heritage. It is home to various ethnic groups, with Pashtuns being the dominant community. The province is known for its scenic beauty, including the rugged mountain ranges of the Hindu Kush and the Khyber Pass itself. The region has played a significant role in the history and geopolitics of South Asia, particularly due to its location at the crossroads of Central Asia, South Asia, and the Middle East.
