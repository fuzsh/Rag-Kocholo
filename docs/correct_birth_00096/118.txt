Who is Victoria Azarenka?

Victoria Azarenka is a professional tennis player from Belarus, born on July 31, 1989. She reached the world No. 1 ranking in singles in 2012 and held it for a total of 51 weeks. Azarenka has won 21 WTA Tour singles titles, including two Australian Open championships in 2012 and 2013, making her the first Belarusian player to achieve a major singles title. She’s also a three-time finalist at the US Open.

In addition to her singles success, Azarenka has excelled in doubles, winning 10 WTA Tour titles and reaching the world No. 7 ranking in 2008. Notably, she secured an Olympic gold medal in mixed doubles at the 2012 London Olympics. Azarenka is known for her powerful play and has consistently ranked in the top 10 for several years, showcasing her remarkable talent in both singles and doubles competitions.

Victoria Azarenka Early Life

Victoria Azarenka was born in Minsk, Byelorussian SSR, Soviet Union, to Alla and Fyodor Azarenka. Her tennis inspiration is German legend Steffi Graf, especially motivated by Graf’s Calendar Golden Slam in 1988. At just 15, Azarenka moved from Minsk to Scottsdale, Arizona, USA, for full-time training, supported by NHL goalie Nikolai Khabibulin and his wife.

Later, she registered in Monaco in 2012 but made Manhattan Beach, California, her main home in 2013. Azarenka dated American musician Redfoo from late 2012 to early 2014. In 2014, she faced depression during her break from professional tennis due to injuries, speaking openly about her challenging experience.

Victoria Azarenka Career

Victoria Azarenka’s tennis career began in 2003 when she debuted on the ITF Junior Circuit, winning a doubles title. In 2005, she became the junior world No. 1, winning the Australian and US Junior Grand Slams. Her breakthrough on the WTA Tour came in 2009 when she won her first career title at the Brisbane International and reached the fourth round of the Australian Open.

Azarenka continued to climb the rankings, winning the Sony Ericsson Open in 2009 and reaching the French Open quarterfinals. In 2012, Azarenka achieved significant success, winning her first Grand Slam singles title at the Australian Open. She also won an Olympic gold medal in mixed doubles at the London Olympics with Max Mirnyi. Injuries affected her career in 2014–2015, but she made a comeback in 2016, winning the Brisbane International and the Indian Wells-Miami double.

After taking time off due to pregnancy in 2016, Azarenka returned to tennis in 2017, facing a custody battle. She struggled with injuries in 2018 but made a strong comeback in 2020, winning the Western & Southern Open. In 2021, she reached the US Open semifinals. Azarenka started 2022 reaching the Australian Open fourth round and achieved her 500th tour-level win at the Qatar Open. She continued her career, reaching the 2024 Australian Open semifinals and winning the 2024 Mutua Madrid Open doubles title with Beatriz Haddad Maia.
