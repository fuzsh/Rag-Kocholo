THE death of Slobodan Milosevic brings an end to one of the grimmest chapters of the post-Cold War era. Yet the passing of the strongman, who with an often cynical brilliance bestrode the Balkan political scene for 15 years, leaves behind a host of contradictions and historical conundrums — and millions of grieving, displaced and impoverished families, the victims of his calamitous misrule.

Milosevic’s final hours are every bit as opaque as his beliefs and brutal system of governance.
