Yasunari Kawabata, 69, winner of the 1968 Nobel Prize in literature, talks with news reporters at his home in Kamakura, Japan, on Oct. 17, 1968. Kawabata is the first Japanese writer to be given the award, which has a cash value of $70,000. Kawabata was president of the Pen Club of Japan from 1948 to 1965, and is a member of the Japanese Academy of Letters. (AP Photo)
