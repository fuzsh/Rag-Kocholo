Biography

Sid Luft was "an American show business figure, the second husband of actress Lynn Bari and later the third husband of actress and singer Judy Garland."[1]

Michael was born 2 November 1915 in New York City, the son of Norbert Luft and Leonora Meyers .

His first wife was Marjorie Fisher, whom he married 28 November 1943 in Los Angeles..[2] Majorie was an actress with the stage name Lynn Bari. They were divorced.

He was the third husband of Judy Garland, his second wife, whom he married in 8 June 1952 in San Benito, California.[3] They were the parents of two children and divorced 19 May 1965.

He married Patricia Hemingway in 1972 in New York City.[4] They divorced in 1980.[5]

He married Camille Keaton in 1993.[6]

He passed away in 2005.[7]

Obituary:[8]

Sid Luft Dies in California

By Ian Gregor, The Associated Press

Los Angeles--Producer Sid Luft, who is credited with reviving the career of his then-wife, Judy Garlind, in the 1950s, has died. He was 89.

Luft, whose movie production credits included "Kilroy was Here" 1947; "French Leave" 1948; and "A Star is Born" 1854, died Thursday in Santa Monica of an apparent heart attack, John Kimble, a longtime friend and business partenr of Luft, said Friday.

Luft and Garland were married in 1952 and divorced a tumultuous 13 years later. The marriage was Garland's third and Luft's third.

They had two children together, Lorna in 1952 and Joey in 1955. Luft also was stepfather to singer and actress Liza Minnelli, who Garland had with director Vincente Minnelli in 1946.

Luft was credited with helping resurrect Garland's career after she was released from her contract at MGM in 1950 following a series of personal and professional crises. It was at MGM that she became a star and made such films as "The Wizard of Oz" and "Meet Me in St. Louis."

Luft, as Garland's manager and agent, produced stage shows for her in London and New York and then brought her back to motion pictures, said Coyne Steven Sanders, Luft's longtime friend and author of the 1990 Garland biography "Rainbow's End."

"A Star Is born," produced by Luft and directed by George Cukor, brought Garland an Academy Award nomination for best actress. The oft-filmed story of a troubled movie star whose career is over=shadowed by that of his protegee also got five other nominations including for best actor (James Mason), best score and best song.

Luft in 1995 also successfully produced a special show for CBS that marked Garland's first appearance on television.

Garland professionally turned to Luft again in 1966 and he booked her on U.S. and international stage tours, Sanders said. "Sid was a great showman, he had great instincts and he knew how to package and put together a show," Sanders said. "Certainly, I can say, knowing him, that Judy was the love of his life. He still loved her and always did."

After he and Garland divorced, Luft married Patti Hemingway in 1970. That union also ended in divorce. He married actress Camille Keaton in 1993.
