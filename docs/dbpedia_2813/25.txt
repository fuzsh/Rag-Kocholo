Frank Miller, vice president of programs for the CBS radio network, died of heart failure at Beth Israel Hospital in Boston on Wednesday while on assignment as executive producer of World Series radio coverage. He was 63 years old and lived in Manhattan.

Mr. Miller joined CBS Radio in 1972 as director of programming and was named a vice president in 1978. He played a key role in the network's sports programming, including Monday Night Football and N.C.A.A. tournament basketball.

Before joining CBS, Mr. Miller was director of special events and network operations for the Mutual Broadcasting System, where he also served as assistant to the president and director of administration. In addition, he had been production manager of WOR Radio in New York and, from 1964 until 1965, vice president of D'Antoni-Baer Productions, a television production company.

Mr. Miller, who was born in Buffalo, N.Y., attended the Collegiate School in Manhattan and graduated from Hamilton College. He was a lieutenant in the Navy during World War II.
