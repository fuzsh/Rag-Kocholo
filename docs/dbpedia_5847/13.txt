Oliver Parker is a director, producer, screenwriter, and former actor born on September 6, 1960, in Plymouth, Devon, England. He acted for about fifteen years, appearing in Casualty as Mark Calder, Agatha Christie's Poirot as Philip Ridgeway, Shepherd on the Rock as Simon McIntyre, The Big Game as Charles Harman, and Leviathan: The Story of Hellraiser and Hellbound: Hellraiser II as Himself.

He began directing in 1995, starting with an adaptation of William Shakespeare's Othello. In 2006, he wrote and directed Fade to Black, a movie set in Italy. The BAFTA Film Award nominee wrote and directed several short films for Channel 4, including Unsigned, The Short Cut, and A Little Loving.

Oliver is best known for Johnny English Reborn (2011) and The Importance of Being Earnest (2002).
