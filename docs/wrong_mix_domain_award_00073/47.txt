Ahmed Zewail (1946-2016), an Egyptian-American scientist who was awarded the Noble Prize in the scientific field as the first Arab who achieves that. He was known as (the father of the femtochemistry). As egyptian, he was the third person who won Noble Prize, after eleven years of Naguib Mahfouz (literature field-1988) and the Ex-president Anwar Al-Sadat (Nobel Peace Prize) in 1977, and Muhammed Al-Baradei followed him (2005 in Peace). Other international awards include the Wolf Prize in Chemistry (1993), The Tolman Medal (1997), the Priestly Medal from the American Chemical Sosciety in 2009 and Davy Medal from the Rroyal Sosciety in 2011.
