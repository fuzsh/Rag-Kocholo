The William Howard Taft National Historic Site is a protected area located in Cincinnati, Ohio. This site is dedicated to preserving the birthplace of the United States President, William Howard Taft. It offers a unique opportunity for visitors to explore the historical significance of this location and learn more about the life and legacy of President Taft.

Establishment of the William Howard Taft National Historic Site

The William Howard Taft National Historic Site was established on December 2, 1969. Its primary purpose is to protect and preserve the birthplace of the 27th President of the United States, William Howard Taft. This site provides a glimpse into the early life of President Taft and the historical context of his time.
