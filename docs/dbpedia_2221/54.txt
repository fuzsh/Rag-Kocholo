Björn Westerlund was a prominent Finnish businessman and the first President and CEO of Nokia Corporation, leading the company from its formation in 1967 until his retirement in 1977. He played a key role in the merger that established Nokia as a major industrial player in Finland. Beyond his corporate duties, Westerlund briefly served as the Minister of Trade and Industry in 1961, representing the Swedish People's Party in V. J. Sukselainen's second government. His leadership and contributions significantly impacted Finland's business landscape.
