Biography

Norman Manley ONH MM QC is Notable.

Norman Manley ONH MM QC was born in Jamaica.

Norman Washington Manley ONH MM QC (4 July 1893 – 2 September 1969) was a Jamaican statesman who served as the first and only Premier of Jamaica from 1959 - 1962. A Rhodes Scholar, Manley became one of Jamaica's leading lawyers in the 1920s and co-founded the People's National Party in 1938. Manley was an advocate of universal suffrage, which was granted by the British colonial government to the colony in 1944.

Norman was born on 4 July 1893 in Manchester, Jamaica[1] He served in the Royal Artillery in World War I and earned the Military Medal [2] He passed away in 1969.
