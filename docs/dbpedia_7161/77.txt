Answer: The capital of France is Paris which is located in the north-central part of the country.

Paris is also referred to as the “City of Light” (La Ville Lumière). Paris is also a global center for art, fashion, culture, and history. Paris is renowned for its iconic landmarks, including the Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and the Arc de Triomphe. The city’s history dates back over 2,000 years, and its cultural influence has extended worldwide, making it a symbol of romance and sophistication. As the political and economic heart of France, Paris houses important government institutions, including the French Parliament, the President’s residence (Élysée Palace), and various ministries. The city is divided into districts, known as arrondissements, each with its unique character and charm. Paris is celebrated for its art and intellectual legacy, with institutions like the Louvre showcasing some of the world’s most famous artworks. The city is also a culinary delight, offering exquisite French cuisine in its numerous bistros and restaurants.
