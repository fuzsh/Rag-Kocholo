Jemima Khan has to make an effort to be taken seriously. As the daughter of billionaire tycoon and Eurosceptic politician Sir James Goldsmith, sister of Zac — the Conservative minister Lord Goldsmith of Richmond Park — ex-wife of Pakistani cricketer-turned-prime minister Imran Khan and a former squeeze of Hugh Grant, she has long been defined by the men in her life.

So when Khan, 49, decided to write a rom-com, she insisted on doing it by herself despite offers to collaborate. When one leading comic actor was told she had written a promising screenplay, his first question was: “With who?”
