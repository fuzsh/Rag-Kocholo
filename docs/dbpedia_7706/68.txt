Louis Calhern (born Carl Vogt in Brooklyn, New York, February 19, 1895, died May 12, 1956) acted in movies like Blond Crazy (1931) with James Cagney, 20,000 Years in Sing Sing (1932) by Michael Curtiz starring Spencer Tracy and Bette Davis, Duck Soup (1933) by Leo McCarey with the Marx Brothers, Notorious (1946) by Alfred Hitchcock with Cary Grant and Ingrid Bergman, Annie Get Your Gun (1950) with Betty Hutton, The Asphalt Jungle (1950) directed by John Huston with Sterling Hayden, The Magnificent Yankee (1950) by John Sturges with Ann Harding, Julius Caesar (1953) directed by Joseph L. Mankiewicz from the 1599 play by William Shakespeare, starring Marlon Brando, Blackboard Jungle (1955) by Richard Brooks with Glenn Ford.
