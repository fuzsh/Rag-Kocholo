University of the East Caloocan (UE Caloocan) is a private educational institution in Caloocan City, Philippines. It is one of the campuses under the University of East (UE) system. Founded in 1954 by Dr. Francisco T. Dalupan Sr., the school started out as UE Tech, a technical-vocational training center. When the school began offering extension program, it officially became the University of the East Caloocan.

Presently, UE Caloocan is housed in a 4.86-hectare land complete with modern facilities and equipment. It is also considered as one of the largest university in the CAMANAVA area. It provides degrees in Business Administration, Engineering, Arts, Sciences and Fine Arts. In addition to tertiary programs, the university also has a senior high school program offering Academic, Arts and Design, Sports, and Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) tracks.

UE Caloocan is duly recognized by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) and the Department of Education (DepEd). Its programs, on the other hand, are accredited by organizations such as the Philippine Accrediting Association of Schools, Colleges and Universities (PAASCU) and the Philippine Association of Colleges and Universities Commission on Accreditation (PACUCOA).
