Novak Djokovic Career

2007 saw Novak compete in the US Grand Finals. But in the decisive set, he lost against the legendary Swiss tennis player Roger Federer. When Djokovic won the Australian National, his maiden Grand Slam event, in 2008, he became the first Serbian to win one of the four most prestigious singles titles in tennis. He also came away with a bronze medal from the 2008 Summer Olympics. At the 2020 Australian National, he captured his 17th Grand Slam victory, and he continued to win titles. Djokovic won his 100th straight match on the green in the Wimbledon Championships semifinal of 2021 when he overcame Márton Fucsovics.

Novak Djokovic Early Life

When Novak Djokovic was four years old, his parents gave him a small racket and a soft foam ball, which he later described as “the most beloved toy in his life.” This is how he began playing tennis. After that, his parents sent him to a tennis camp in Novi Sad. In the summer of 1993, Djokovic went to a tennis camp at Mount Kopaonik, where he was six years old. Teniski Klub Partizan ran the program, which was supervised by tennis player Jelena Genčić. As she watched the young Djokovic play tennis, the woman said, “This is the greatest talent I have seen since Monica Seles.” After working with young Novak Djokovic for six years, Genčić concluded that it would be beneficial for him in the long run.

In a statement, the Australian Border Force said, “Non-citizens who do not hold a valid visa on entry or who have had their visa cancelled will be detained and removed from Australia.” Djokovic had not spoken about his vaccination status, and in 2021, he said that he was “opposed to vaccination.” On 10 January 2022, he won the court case when Australian Circuit Court Judge Anthony Kelly reinstated him and ordered the government to release Djokovic from Melbourne hotel quarantine within 30 minutes of his decision.
