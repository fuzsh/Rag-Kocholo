Joaquin Phoenix, born Joaquin Rafael Bottom on October 28, 1974, is a revered figure in the world of acting. Known for his compelling performances and ability to breathe life into dark and unconventional characters, Phoenix has left an indelible mark on independent cinema. From his early roles in television series alongside his brother River to major film roles such as SpaceCamp (1986) and Parenthood (1989), he has shown remarkable range and versatility. His transition from being credited as Leaf Phoenix back to his birth name marked a new chapter in his career that saw him garner critical acclaim for films like To Die For (1995) and Quills (2000).

Phoenix's portrayal of Commodus in Gladiator (2000) earned him an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor, solidifying his status as one of Hollywood's most promising talents. He further cemented this reputation with standout performances in films like Signs (2002), The Village (2004), Hotel Rwanda (2004), and Walk the Line (2005), the latter earning him a Grammy Award, a Golden Globe Award, and another Academy Award nomination.

After a brief hiatus from acting, Phoenix returned with renewed vigor, delivering powerful performances in The Master (2012), Her (2013), Inherent Vice (2014), You Were Never Really Here (2017) and Joker (2019). His performance as the title character of Joker won him the coveted Academy Award for Best Actor.

Off-screen, Phoenix is known for his animal rights activism. A vegan since three years old, he uses his platform to support charitable causes related to global meat consumption's environmental impact. He also maintains a private family life with actress Rooney Mara and their son.
