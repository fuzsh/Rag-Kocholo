⛺ Llano, Tx has a humid subtropical climate that is mild with no dry season, constantly moist (year-round rainfall). Summers are hot and muggy with thunderstorms. Winters are mild with precipitation from mid-latitude cyclones. Seasonality is moderate. (Köppen-Geiger classification: Cfa).

⛳ Llano, Tx is situated in or near the subtropical dry forest biome.

🌡️ The annual average temperature is 18.8 degrees Celsius (65.8 degrees Fahrenheit). See the temperatures page for a monthly breakdown and the fixed scale graph.

🐬 Average monthly temperatures vary by 21.6 °C (38.9°F). This indicates that the continentality type is continental, subtype subcontinental.

☔ Total annual precipitation averages 671.5 mm (26.4 inches) which is equivalent to 671.5 Litres/m² (16.47 Gallons/ft²).

In the winter time records indicate temperatures by day reach 16.4°C (61.5°F) on average falling to 0.7°C (33.3°F) overnight.

In spring time temperatures climb reaching 26.1°C (79°F) generally in the afternoon with overnight lows of 12°C (53.6°F).

During summer average high temperatures are 34.8°C (94.7°F) and average low temperatures are 21.4°C (70.5°F).

Come autumn/ fall temperatures decrease achieving average highs of 26.7°C (80.1°F) during the day and lows of 12.2°C (53.9°F) generally shortly after sunrise.

✨ The longest day of the year is 14:00 long on the 22 of June and the shortest day is 9:59 long on the 21 of December.

🐇Were you to burrow down through the centre of the Earth from Llano, Tx you would pop up nearest to the climate station at Amsterdam Island, French Southern Lands where you would find a Marine west coast climate.
