Victoria's Steve Nash is the NBA's Western Conference player of the week.

Nash, 32, received the award for leading the Phoenix Suns to four wins last week, extending their winning streak to 13 games.

The four-time all-star averaged 15.5 points and a league-high 13.8 assists as Phoenix defeated the Memphis Grizzlies, Houston Rockets, Portland Trail Blazers and Minnesota Timberwolves.

Caron Butler of the Washington Wizards was the Eastern Conference recipient.

Nash, named the NBA's most valuable player in each of the past two seasons, is averaging a career-high 19.2 points through 38 starts this season.

He also is averaging 11.5 assists, 3.1 rebounds and 35.6 minutes.

Nash has averaged 13.8 points, 7.3 assists, 2.8 rebounds and 30.2 minutes in 741 games (582 starts) over 11 NBA seasons with the Suns and Dallas Mavericks.

Drafted 15th overall out of Santa Clara in 1996, he played two seasons with the Suns before being traded to Dallas for Martin Muursepp, Bubba Wells, Pat Garrity and a first-round draft pick on June 24, 1998.

Nash rejoined Phoenix as a free agent on July 14, 2004.
