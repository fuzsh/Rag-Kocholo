Q. Consider the following statements on Indian revolutionaries: Udham Singh was known as Shaheed-i-Azam or ‘the Great Martyr’. Madan Lal Dhingra had assassinated William Hutt Curzon Wyllie. Chandrasekhar Azad was involved in the Kakori Train Robbery in 1925 and the shooting of J P Saunders. Khudiram Bose was noted for his role in the Muzaffarpur Conspiracy Case and the attempted assassination of Magistrate Kingsford. Which of the above given statements is/are correct?

Answer: 1, 2, 3 & 4

Notes:

Udham Singh was an Indian revolutionary who is also known as Shaheed-i-Azam or ‘the Great Martyr’. He is noted for assassinating Michael O’Dwyer in retaliation for the Jallianwala Bagh massacre.

Madan Lal Dhingra was an Indian revolutionary who assassinated William Hutt Curzon Wyllie, a British official. He was sentenced to death for the assassination.

Chandrasekhar Azad was an Indian revolutionary noted for his involvement in the Kakori Train Robbery in 1925 and the shooting of J P Saunders, a British police officer. This was to avenge the killing of Lala Lajpat Rai.

Khudiram Bose was an Indian revolutionary who is noted for his role in the Muzaffarpur Conspiracy Case and the attempted assassination of Magistrate Kingsford, a British judge.
