Princess Henriette of Belgium (30 November 1870 – 28 March 1948), was the daughter of Prince Philippe, Count of Flanders, and Princess Marie of Hohenzollern. She was the younger twin sister of Princess Joséphine Marie of Belgium, who died at the aged six weeks in 1871.

Marriage[edit]

She married in Brussels, Belgium on 12 February 1896 Prince Emmanuel of Orléans, a son of Prince Ferdinand, Duke of Alençon, and Sophie Charlotte in Bavaria, and a great-grandson of King Louis Philippe I.

Issue[edit]

Princess Marie Louise of Orléans (31 December 1896 – 8 March 1973) married Prince Filippo of the Two Sicilies and had issue.

Princess Sophie of Orléans (19 October 1898 – 9 October 1928) died unmarried.

Princess Geneviève of Orléans (21 September 1901 – 22 August 1983) married Antoine de Chaponay, Marquis of Chaponay-Morance and had issue.

Prince Charles Philippe of Orléans, Duke of Nemours (4 April 1905 – 10 March 1970) married Marguerite Watson (1899–1993) nae issue.
