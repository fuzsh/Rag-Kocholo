Biography

Heike (Onnes) Kamerlingh Onnes is Notable.

Heike (Onnes) Kamerlingh Onnes was awarded the Nobel Prize.

Onnes was awarded the 1913 Nobel Prize in Physics for (in the words of the committee) "his investigations on the properties of matter at low temperatures which led, inter alia, to the production of liquid helium".

Heike was born in 1853, son of Harm Kamerlingh (Onnes) Kamerlingh Onnes (1819-1880), koopman, 34 and Anna Gerdina Coers (1829-1899), 24.[1]

He is known for his fundamental work on superconductivity. He worked in Leiden and was already a professor at 33. He then married 26 year old Maria Adriana Wilhelmina Elisabeth Bijleveld (1861-1938) in 1887.[2]

They lived in the Haagweg nr 49 (wijk XII) with several dienstbodes.[3]

He passed away at the age of 72.[4] He was buried at the Kerkhof Dorpskerk in Voorschoten.[5]
