Sony Wonder (founded as Sony Kids’ Music/Sony Kids' Video) is the kids and family entertainment label of Sony Pictures Home Entertainment and the former record label owned by Sony Music Entertainment.

History[]

It was founded in 1991 by Sony Music Entertainment as its children and family music and home video division. The division once distributed material from CINAR (now Cookie Jar Entertainment) from 1992 to 2008, Nelvana from 1993 to 2008, DIC Entertainment from 1992 to 2008, BKN International from 1999 to 2008, Broadway Video from 1992 to 2008, Amazin' Adventures from 1992 to 1997, TLC from 1994 to 2008, NBC from 2002 to 2006, The Lyons Group (now Lyrick Studios) from 1992 to 2005, Barney and Friends from 1992 to 2008, Nickelodeon from 1992 to 2008, Bohbot Communications from 1992 to 2001, UPA from 2000 to 2008, Harvey Comics from 1994 to 2008, Harvey Entertainment from 1994 to 2008, Golden Books from 1992 to 2008, Children's Television Workshop (now Sesame Workshop) from 1994 to 2008, Sesame Street from 1994 to 2008, Kidsongs from 1995 to 1998, Discovery Kids Channel (and later Discovery Channel Kids, Discovery Kids, The Hub, Hub Network, and Discovery Family) from 1996 to 2008, The Discovery Channel (now Discovery Channel) from 1994 to 2008, Ready Set Learn! from 1994 to 2008, Hallmark Entertainment (now RHI Entertainment) from 1994 to 2008, The Puzzle Place from 1995 to 2003, Discovery Communications (now Discovery, Inc.) from 1994 to 2008, Bohbot Kids Network from 1997 to 2001, BKN Kids II from 1999 to 2000, Jay Ward Productions (now Bullwinkle Studios) from 2000 to 2008, Golden Films from 1994 to 2008, Enchanted Tales from 1994 to 2008, The Beginner's Bible for Kids from 1995 to 2008, Jumbo Pictures from 1992 to 2008, Time-Life from 1992 to 2008, Nick Jr. from 1995 to 2008, HIT Entertainment from 1994 to 2008, Entertainment Rights from 2001 to 2008, Big Idea Entertainment from 1997 to 2008, and Classic Media (now DreamWorks Classics) from 2000 to 2008. They also distributed releases from Random House's home video division from 1994 to 2008.

In 1992, Sony Wonder acquired video distribution rights to Nickelodeon's series and re-released early Nick Jr. videos (including Eureeka's Castle) by Hi-Tops Video.

On July 21, 1995, Sony Wonder and Sony Pictures Entertainment joined forces with The Jim Henson Company by releasing new movies with Columbia Pictures and TriStar Pictures through the joint venture Jim Henson Pictures and new sing-along-songs on videocassette and DVD. The next day, Nickelodeon and Sony Wonder launched Nick Jr. Video after the deal with Hi-Tops Video expired. Earlier in 1988, CBS Video (and later CBS DVD, and CBS Home Entertainment), Warner Home Video (now Warner Bros. Home Entertainment), and GoodTimes Home Video (and later GoodTimes Entertainment, and GT Media) launched Nickelodeon Home Video. Earlier in 1989, Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. videos, and DVDs were mainly distributed by Warner Reprise Video, Elektra Entertainment, and Rhino Home Video (now Rhino Entertaiment), and Kid Rhino Home Video. Earlier in 1990, Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. videos were mainly distrbuted by Hi-Tops Video, and Media Home Entertainment. Earlier in 1991, Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. videos, and DVDs were mainly distributed by Warner Music Vision (now Warner Music Entertainment). Earlier in 1992, Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. videos, and DVDs were mainly distributed by Warner Bros. Family Entertainment, Video Treasures (now Anchor Bay Entertainment), Fox Video (now 20th Century Fox Home Entertainment), and CBS/Fox Video. Earlier in 1994, after Viacom's merger with Paramount Pictures, Sony Wonder's deal with Nickelodeon continued, arriving Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. videos, and DVDs distributed by Paramount Home Entertainment (and CIC Video internationally until 1999). In 1995, Hi-Tops Video's deal with Nickelodeon expired. In 1996, Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. videos, and DVDs were mainly distributed by Genius Products. In 2000, Warner Reprise Video's deal with Nickelodeon expired. In 2001, CBS/Fox Video's, and Kid Rhino Home Video's deal with Nickelodeon expired. In 2004, Elektra Entertainment's deal with Nickelodeon expired. In 2006, Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. DVDs were mainly distributed by Vivendi Entertainment (now Gaiam Vivendi Entertainment). In 2007, Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. DVDs were mainly distributed by Starz Home Entertainment, and Gaiam. In 2008, Anchor Bay Entertainment's, Starz Home Entertainment's, and Sony Wonder's deal with Nickelodeon expired, which left Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. DVDs distributed by Paramount Home Entertainment, Genius Products, and Vivendi Entertainment. In 2009, GT Media's deal with Nickelodeon expired. In 2011, Genius Products' deal with Nickelodeon expired, which left Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. DVDs distributed by Shout! Factory. In 2013, Gaiam's, and Gaiam Vivendi Entertainment's deal with Nickelodeon expired, which left Nickelodeon, and Nick Jr. DVDs distributed by Paramount Home Entertainment, Cinedigm, and New Video Group. In 2016, 20th Century Home Entertainment's deal with Nickelodeon expired. In 2017, Warner Bros. Family Entertainment's, and Warner Bros. Home Entertainment's deal with Nickelodeon expired. In 2018, Warner Music Entertainment's, Rhino Entertainment's, and Shout! Factory's deal with Nickelodeon expired.

On May 4, 1998, Sony Wonder bought Sunbow Productions and it was later bought by TV-Loonland on October 3, 2000 along with Sony Wonder's television business assets.

On September 1999, Sony Wonder acquired album label right to children's music group of Hi-5 from Kids Like Us and Nine Films and Television.

In 2008, Sony BMG announced that it was shutting down Sony Wonder to focus on its core music business.[11][12][13] However, earlier in 2006, it was announced that Sony Wonder became a division of Sony Pictures Home Entertainment as its kids’- and family-entertainment label.[14][15] Classic Media's, Sesame Workshop's and Random House's video deals were sold to Genius Products for an undisclosed amount, and later to Vivendi Entertainment.

Despite the similarity in name, Sony Wonder is not directly related to the former Sony Wonder Technology Lab, an interactive technology and entertainment museum, although the museum was also owned by Sony.
