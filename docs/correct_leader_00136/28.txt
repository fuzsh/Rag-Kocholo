General Louis Botha, (1862-1919), Afrikaner soldier and statesman, 1894-1907. Botha was Commander-in-chief of the Boer forces from 1900 during the 2nd Boer War (1899-1902), leading a guerrilla campaign against the British. After the war he served as Prime Minister of Transvaal from 1907-1910, before being elected President of the Union of South Africa, an office he held until 1919. From Ogdens Guinea Gold Cigarettes. EDITORS COMMENTS

This print showcases General Louis Botha, a prominent figure in South African history. Born in 1862, Botha was not only an Afrikaner soldier but also a respected statesman. The image captures him during his tenure as Commander-in-chief of the Boer forces from 1900 to 1902, when he led a daring guerrilla campaign against the British during the Second Boer War. After the war, Botha's leadership qualities were recognized and he served as Prime Minister of Transvaal from 1907 to 1910. His political career continued to flourish when he was elected President of the Union of South Africa, holding this esteemed office until his passing in 1919. In this colorized print, we see General Louis Botha outdoors wearing a hat that adds an air of authority to his presence. His bearded face reflects wisdom and experience gained through years of military service and political maneuvering. As one gazes at this portrait, it is evident that both determination and resilience are etched into every line on his face. This photograph serves as a reminder of an era marked by conflict and transformation in South Africa's history. It encapsulates not only the bravery displayed by soldiers like General Louis Botha during times of war but also their ability to transition into influential statesmen who played pivotal roles in shaping their nation's future.
