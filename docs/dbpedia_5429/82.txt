Who: Timothy Peter Dalton

Born: 21 March 1944 or 1946, Colwyn Bay, Denbighshire, Wales, UK

Timothy Peter Dalton is a British actor of film and television. Dalton is known for portraying James Bond in The Living Daylights (1987) and Licence to Kill (1989), as well as Rhett Butler in the television miniseries Scarlett (1994), an original sequel to Gone with the Wind.

Timothy Dalton trivia facts:

His Bond character was the last one to smoke cigarettes, though they have continued to drink martinis.

Dalton is a Manchester City F.C. supporter, and is often seen at the City of Manchester Stadium to watch his team play.

His favourite favourite Bond movie is Dr.No (1962).
