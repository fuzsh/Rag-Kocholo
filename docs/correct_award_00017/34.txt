Liquefaction of Helium:

2. Discovery of Superconductivity:

In 1911, Kamerlingh Onnes discovered superconductivity while studying the electrical resistance of mercury at extremely low temperatures. He observed that, below a critical temperature, mercury’s electrical resistance dropped to zero. This phenomenon, now known as superconductivity, was a groundbreaking discovery in physics.

3. Invention of the Dewar Flask:

Kamerlingh Onnes also improved the design of the vacuum flask, which is now commonly known as the Dewar flask. This invention allowed for the storage of liquefied gases at low temperatures and was instrumental in his experiments with low-temperature physics.
