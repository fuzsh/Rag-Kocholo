Garnett H. Buckner, 84, of Hendersonville passed away Tuesday, July 13, 2004, in his home after an extended illness.

He was a native and lifelong resident of Henderson County, the son of the late L.B. and Julia Buckner, and the husband of the late Mildred Buckner. He was also preceded in death by a granddaughter, Karen.

He was an Air Force veteran of World War II and retired from Olin after 34 years of service. He was a member of Mud Creek Baptist Church, where he was a former deacon.

He is survived by three sons, Wayne Buckner and wife, Aline, of East Flat Rock, Danny Buckner of Hendersonville and David Buckner and wife, Tina, of Etowah; two daughters, Carol Kentopp and husband, Richard, of the state of Washington and Judy Hedrick and husband, Kenny, of Greenwood, S.C.; a brother, Cecil Buckner of Hendersonville; two sisters, Nellie Buckner and Lucy Pace of Hendersonville; six grandchildren, Jason, Adam and Justin Hedrick, Jeff and JonnBuckner and Kimberly Kentopp; five great-grandchildren; and several nieces and nephews.

A graveside service will be held at 11 a.m. Friday at Shepherd Memorial Park. The Rev. Greg Mathis will officiate. Military rites will be conducted by the Hedrick-Rhodes Veterans of Foreign Wars Post 5206, Hubert M. Smith American Legion Post 77 and the Disabled American Veterans Chapter 14. The family will receive friends from 7 to 8 p.m. today at the Jackson Funeral Service, and at other times will be at the home. In lieu of flowers, memorials may be made to the American Cancer Society, 120 Executive Park, Building One, Asheville, N.C. 28801.
