FRANKLIN THoMAs-DocTOR OF ENGINEERING

In acknowledgem~nt of his leadership in the whole field of engineering,

academic and applied, and for tireless contributions to the welfare of his

community and state. His professional achievements have earned him the

respect of his colleagues; he is currently the president of the American

Society of Civil Engineers. As an originator, and for the last twenty-one

years vice-chairman, of the Metropolitan Water District, he has helped to

make possible the growth and development of Southern California. As an

administrative official of the California Institute of Technology for many

years, he has had a large share in the building of a distinguished educa­

tional institution. A leader in Pasadena city governmental, commercial,

charitable, and cultural affairs, he has performed notable service to his

fellow citizens.

Presented by Robert L. Gifford, Member of the Board of Trustees

GLENN L. MARTIN-DOCTOR OF SCIENCE

Respected pioneer and distinguished leader in the aviation industry, who

this year marks the completion of four decades as a manufacturer of air­ craft. As a designer and builder of many famous military and commercial

airplanes, his contributions to his country's acknowledged superiority in

the air are numerous and significant. Holder of Expert Aviator's Certifi­

cate No. 2 of the Aeronautical Club of America, he established in 1909 one

of the first airplane factories in the nation, located at Santa Ana, Califor­ nia. Member of many scientific and honorary societies, he is the recipient

of foremost honors in his field, including the presidency of the Institute of

Aeronautical Sciences and the Collier Trophy for greatest achievement in

aeronautics in America.

Presentedbr Robert E. Vivian, Dean of the School of Engineering

ERNEST ORLANDO LAWRENCE-DOCTOR OF SCIENCE

His pioneering research and discoveries in nuclear physics have been of

unique service in the advancement of science. As Professor of Physics at

the University of California, he invented and developed the cyclotron,

which has lighted the path to new benefits for mankind. During the war

he directed one of the most important projects of the Manhattan District,

which was charged with producing the atomic bomb. A Nobel Laureate,

inspiring teacher, imaginative research worker, and able administrator, he

has not only compiled an outstanding record of personal achievement but

also made a universal contribution to science through the development of

a distinguished and respected radiation laboratory and through generous

assistance to other men and institutions engaged in scholarly research.

Presented by Chester M. Van Atta, Professor of Physics

LEWIS MADISON TERMAN-DOCTOR OF LAWS

Distinguished author, educator, and editor, whose studies of human endow­

ment and of methods of measuring mental ability have greatly enriched

and advanced the field of psychology. Pre-eminent for his research and

contributions on the nature of gifted children, he has been widely honored

for his scholarly accomplishments by many learned societies and uni­

versities. Former president of the American Psychological Association and

adviser to the government on the selection and classification of personnel

for the armed forces, his activities, writings, and researches have been

numerous and continuous, but he has always found time for wise and

friendly counsel to youth and to advanced students in the field of psycho­

logical studies.

Presented by Albert S. Raubenheimer, Educational Vice-President

VIRGIL MELVIN HANCHER-DOCTOR OF LAWS

In recognition of his distinguished educational administration and states­

manship. As president of the State University of Iowa since 1940, he has

displayed high qualifications of leadership beyond the borders of his own

institution, as evidenced by his presidency of the State Universities Asso­ ciation. Noted for his study of problems of the relations between higher

education and the Federal Government, he has carried forward this project

with unfailing regard for the unique freedoms that typify the American

system of education. An able lawyer, he has won the respect and admira­

tion of his fellow members of the bar. Member of various societies and

organizations devoted to the progress of research and learning, and recip­

ient of many honors, he serves as an inspiration to students and colleagues.

Presented by Tracy E. Strevey, Dean of the College of Letters, Arts, and Sciences
