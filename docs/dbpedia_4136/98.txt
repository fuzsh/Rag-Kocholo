The Oriental Museum boasts a collection of over 23,500 artefacts from various Asian and Far East countries, including China, Egypt, Korea, India, and Japan. These artefacts provide a comprehensive insight into the rich and diverse cultures of these regions.

Significance of the Museum's Collections

The Chinese and Egyptian collections of the Oriental Museum have been designated by the Museums, Libraries and Archives Council, now known as the Arts Council England, as being of national and international importance. This recognition underscores the significance of these collections in the global cultural landscape.

Location of the Oriental Museum

The Oriental Museum is situated at Elvet Hill House, on the southern edge of Durham. Its location offers easy access for visitors and provides a serene environment for exploring the vast collections of the museum.
