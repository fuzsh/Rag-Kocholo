Princess Christine of Orléans-Braganza

Princess Christine of Orléans-Braganza was born on December 12, 1943, in Mandelieu-la-Napoule, France. She was the daughter of Prince Pedro Henrique of Orléans-Braganza and Princess Maria Elisabeth of Bavaria. Her family moved to Brazil when she was young, and she grew up in Rio de Janeiro. Christine studied history and psychology in Brazil and later moved to the United States to pursue a degree in library science. She worked as a librarian for many years in Washington, D.C. In 1978, Christine married Francisco Mancini Martins. They had two children together, Pedro and Amélia. Christine was involved in charitable and cultural activities, especially in promoting Brazilian culture in the United States. She was a member of the Brazilian-American Cultural Institute and the Brazilian Women's Group. Princess Christine passed away on March 3, 2011, at the age of 67, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. She was survived by her husband and children.
