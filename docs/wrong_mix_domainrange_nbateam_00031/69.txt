On Thursday evening, the Denver Nuggets will host the Memphis Grizzlies in Colorado.

Before the game (on Wednesday), the team announced that they will be without starting forward Aaron Gordon after he was bitten by a dog.

Via The Nuggets: "Aaron Gordon suffered lacerations to his face and hand resulting from a dog bite on December 25th. Aaron is in good condition and will remain away from the team while he recovers."

Shams Charania of The Athletic and Stadium also reported more details.

Via Charania: "Aaron Gordon suffered serious dog bites to his face and shooting right hand on Monday -- requiring 21 stitches, sources say. It will take some time to heal, with no timetable yet."

Gordon has played in 28 games so far this season and is averaging 13.6 points, 6.9 rebounds, 3.4 assists and 1.0 steals per contest while shooting 52.5% from the field.

The Nuggets are the second seed in the Western Conference with a 22-10 record in their first 32 games.

They are 8-2 over their last ten games and in the middle of a five-game winning streak.

Most recently, the Nuggets beat the Golden State Warriors by a score of 120-114.

In 32 minutes of playing time, Gordon had 16 points, ten rebounds, three assists and one steal while shooting 7/10 from the field and 1/4 from the three-point range.

Gordon is in his tenth season in the league and has also spent time with the Orlando Magic.

His career averages are 13.4 points, 6.3 rebounds and 2.6 assists per contest.
