Jason Maxiell signed with the Detroit Pistons on Friday before announcing his retirement from basketball.

“Jason and his representatives reached out to us regarding his desire to sign one last contract with the team in order to retire as a Detroit Piston,” general manager Jeff Bower said in a release. “Jason made an impact on and off the court with the organization, spending eight seasons in Detroit, many with deep playoff runs. We’re pleased that Jason has chosen to remain connected to our organization and wish him well in the future.”

Maxiell was the 26th pick in the 2005 NBA draft out of Cincinnati, and spent eight years in Detroit, averaging 6.1 points, 4.4 rebounds over 19.1 minutes in 523 games. His 426 blocks ranks ninth in team history. He was also known for his ferocious dunks.

He played on three Eastern Conference Finals teams in his first three seasons, averaging a career best 7.9 points with 5.3 rebounds in 82 games in 2007-08.

“The best years of my professional career were spent in Detroit and it’s important to me to retire as a Detroit Piston,” said Maxiell in a statement. “This is a special franchise with great tradition and history. I want to thank all the fans who supported me during my eight seasons representing Detroit.”

He last played for the Charlotte Hornets in 2014-15, appearing in 61 games.
