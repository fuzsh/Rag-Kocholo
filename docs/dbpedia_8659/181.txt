Biography

British theoretical physicist, widely known for the "Higgs Mechanism". Emeritus professor at the University of Edinburgh, he was awarded the Nobel prize in 2013 for his work on the mass of subatomic particles.

Higgs married Jody Williamson, an American lecturer in linguistics at Edinburgh and a fellow activist with the Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament (CND), in 1963. He had two sons: Christopher, a computer scientist, and Jonny, a jazz musician. Higgs and Williamson divorced in 1972. Higgs died after a short illness at home in Edinburgh on 8 April 2024 at the age of 94.
