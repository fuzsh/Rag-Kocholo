"Der bewegte Mann" (the moved man) is a German comedy, based on the comic of the same name and the follow-up "Pretty Baby" by Ralf König.

With over 6.5 million cinema-goers in Germany, it is one of the most successful German films in 1994 and was only surpassed by the US productions "The Lion King" and "Forrest Gump". Despite or even because of its box office success, the film was criticised, including by König himself. Although in Ralf König's template, both heterosexuals and homosexuals are caricatured by an exaggerated portrayal, in the film this only affects the gay protagonists, so that the film's perspective for the mass market is shifted to heteronormativity.
