Biography

John Fremont is Notable.

John Charles Frémont or Fremont (January 21, 1813 – July 13, 1890) was an American military officer, explorer, and politician who became the first candidate of the anti-slavery Republican Party for the office of President of the United States. During the 1840s, that era's penny press accorded Frémont the sobriquet The Pathfinder. Historians call him The Great Pathfinder. - [2] Served as the 5th Territorial Governor of Arizona (from 6 Oct 1878 through 11 Oct 1881)

Legacy

Four U.S. states have counties named in Gov. Fremont's honor: Colorado, Idaho, Iowa and Wyoming.
