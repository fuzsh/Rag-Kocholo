Lleyton Hewitt, born on February 24, 1981, in Adelaide, Australia, is a former professional tennis player and one of Australia's most successful athletes. Bursting onto the scene as a teenager, Hewitt claimed the Wimbledon singles title in 2002 and secured the world No. 1 ranking. Known for his tenacity, on-court intensity, and exceptional baseline game, he won two Grand Slam singles titles and two Davis Cups for Australia. Hewitt's resilience and leadership extended beyond his playing career as he became a successful Davis Cup captain. His impact on Australian tennis and global admiration for his competitive spirit define Hewitt's enduring legacy.
