November 4, 2003

November 4, 2003, Greencastle, Ind. - Steven V. Wilkinson, a 1963 graduate of DePauw University, has been elected to the board of directors of Entergy Corporation (NYSE: ETR). The 61-year-old Wilkinson was a partner with Arthur Andersen until his retirement in 1998, serving as director of the firm's North American utility practice. He was named a partner with Andersen in 1974.

"We are pleased to bring to the Entergy board someone with the business, accounting and auditing expertise of Steven Wilkinson," says Robert v.d. Luft, chairman of Entergy. "He is a highly respected professional in the field of auditing, and his experience with public utilities will benefit the board of directors and all of Entergy's stakeholders."

Wilkinson currently serves as chairman of Cabot Microelectronics Corporation's audit committee and as a member of the compensation committee. In addition, he is a past board chairman of Central DuPage Health and Blackburn College, where he continues to serve as a board member. A company news release at Yahoo! Finance notes Wilkinson's "bachelor's degree in economics from DePauw University and... master's degree in business administration from the University of Chicago Graduate School of Business. Wilkinson is a member of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants." You can read the entire announcement by clicking here.
