Wilfried Martens served almost continuously as Prime Minister of Belgium in nine coalition governments between 1979 and 1992. Much of this long time in office was dominated by domestic politics, and by the challenge of keeping ­together a country sharply divided in more ways than one. Martens, however, was also a significant player on the international stage. A fervent believer in the principle of European Union, he was a co-founder of the European People’s Party, now the chief European political party of the centre-right.
