Biography

William Lewis is Notable.

William Lewis was an American pioneer in athletics, law and politics. He was the first African-American named to a College Football All-America Team, Harvard University. In 1910, President William Taft appointed him as Assistant Attorney General.[1]

William Lewis, son of Ashley Lewis and Josephine Baker, was born on 28 Nov 1868 in Berkeley, Virginia. In 1896, he married Elizabeth Baker.[2]

He died on 1 Jan 1949 in Boston, Massachusetts.
