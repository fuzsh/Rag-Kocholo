Mike Miller Facts

Mike Miller

Born August 14, 1964

Hometown: Monmouth, Illinois

Alma Mater: East Texas State University (BA, 1987)

Graduated from Monmouth HS (IL) and then played at D-II East Texas State (now known as Texas A&M-Commerce)

Started his coaching career back in his home state at Western Illinois, spending one season as an assistant under Jack Margenthaler

Was an assistant at Sam Houston State for one year before joining Jim Wooldridge‘s staff at Texas State in 1991

After three seasons, Miller was elevated to head coach; led the program for the next six seasons

Went 87-79 overall, winning two Southland titles and reaching the NCAA Tournament in 1997 after winning the Southland Tournament

Reunited with Wooldridge in 2000, working on his staff at Kansas State for the next five seasons

Left K State in 2005 to be the head coach at Eastern Illinois, leading the Panthers for seven seasons

Compiled an overall record of 75-130, highlighted by the 2009-10 season when the team went 19-12 (11-7 OVC)

The school declined to renew Miller’s contract in 2012 following a 12-17 campaign

Worked with Wooldridge for one more season at UC Riverside before moving up to the professional coaching ranks

Was an assistant with the Austin Toros/Spurs of the NBA G League for two seasons under head coach Ken McDonald

Became the head coach of the Westchester Knicks – the G League affiliate for the New York Knicks – in 2015

Went 108-92 in four seasons at the helm, reaching the Playoffs in 2016; was named G League Coach of the Year in 2018

Moved up to the New York Knicks staff – led by David Fizdale – ahead of the 2019-20 season

Named interim head coach of the Knicks in December 2019 after Fizdale’s dismissal; went 17-27 in 44 games at the helm

Spent one year on Mark Daigneault’s staff in Oklahoma City and since 2021 has been with the Wizards and head coach Wes Unseld Jr.
