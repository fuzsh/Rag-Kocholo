Red Mitchell was an actor who had a successful Hollywood career. Mitchell found his beginnings in film with roles in the Susan Hayward crime adaptation "I Want to Live!" (1958) and the Leslie Caron dramatic adaptation "The Subterraneans" (1960). Mitchell began to focus on film after appearing in the Tal Farlow documentary "Talmage Farlow" (1981), "Forever Evil" (1988) and the Kevin Costner dramatic adaptation "J.F.K." (1991). Later in his career, Mitchell acted in "8 Seconds" (1994) with Luke Perry.
