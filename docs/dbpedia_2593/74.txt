Sir Charles Carteret, 3rd Bt.1

M, #209131, b. 4 June 1679, d. 3 June 1715

Last Edited=5 May 2007

Sir Charles Carteret, 3rd Bt. was baptised on 4 June 1679 at St. Margaret's Church, Westminster, London, England .1 He was the son of Sir Philip Carteret, 2nd Bt. and Elizabeth Carteret.1 He died on 3 June 1715 at age 35, without surviving issue.1 He was also reported to have died on 6 June 1715.1 He was buried on 8 June 1715 at Westminster Abbey, Westminster, London, England .1

He succeeded as the 3rd Baronet Carteret, of St. Ouen, Jersey [E., 1670] in 1693.1 He held the office of Gentleman of the Privy Chamber to Queen Anne.1 He held the office of High Bailiff of Jersey.1

On his death, his baronetcy became extinct.1

Sir Robin McAlpine1

M, #209132, b. 18 March 1906, d. 1993

Last Edited=25 May 2014

Sir Robin McAlpine was born on 18 March 1906.2 He was the son of Sir Thomas Malcolm McAlpine and Maud Dees.3 He married, firstly, Nora Constance Perse, daughter of Horace Frank Perse, on 14 July 1939.2 He married, secondly, Philippa Janet Tennyson-d'Eyncourt, daughter of Sir Eustace Gervais Tennyson-d'Eyncourt, 2nd Bt. and Pamela Gladstone, on 20 March 1970.1 He died in 1993.1

He was given the name of Robert McAlpine at birth.2 He was educated at Charterhouse School, Godalming, Surrey, England.2 In 1939 his name was legally changed to Robin McAlpine by Deed Poll.2 He was appointed Commander, Order of the British Empire (C.B.E.) in 1957.2 He was president of the Federation of Civil Engineering Contractors.3 He was chairman of Sir Robert McAlpine and Sons Ltd before 1969.2 He was appointed Knight in 1969.2

Child of Sir Robin McAlpine and Nora Constance Perse
