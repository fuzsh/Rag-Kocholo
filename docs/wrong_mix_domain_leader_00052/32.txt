Wim Kok was a former trade union leader who served as prime minister of the Netherlands from 1994 to 2002, dragging his country out of recession, implementing free-market reforms under the cloak of a centre-left “purple” coalition, and pioneering the “third way” philosophy that Tony Blair would later offer to Britain.

Kok was also responsible for a series of social reforms in the Netherlands, including the full legalisation of prostitution, same-sex marriage and assisted suicide. He was blamed by President Chirac of France for undermining French youth by making drugs freely available in the “narco-state” of the Netherlands.
