Attensity Corp., with headquarters in both Salt Lake City and Mountain View, Calif., said Monday it has closed on $12 million in second-round funding.

The financing was led by Palomar Ventures, with additional investors including In-Q-Tel, the strategic investment arm of the U.S. Central Intelligence Agency; Jefferson Partners; The Entrepreneurs' Fund; Highway 12 Ventures; Triangle Venture Capital Group; and Village Ventures.

This is the second time that In-Q-Tel, a private, nonprofit enterprise funded by the CIA, has invested in Attensity's technology.

Privately held Attensity provides data analysis software for government agencies and commercial enterprises.
