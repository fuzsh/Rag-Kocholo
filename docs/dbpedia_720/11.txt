Inspired is 70% owned by Leisure Link, which used to make its money by moving fruit machines from pub to pub. It looks an unlikely starting point for a company that hopes to be in the vanguard of the digital revolution.

But that is the plan hatched by Crowley and his boss, Leisure Link’s chief executive, Russell Hoyle. With its machines in 20,000 pubs and clubs, Leisure Link believes it has a chance to bring digital vending — of music, mobile-phone top-ups, ringtones, electronic games and airline and cinema tickets — to a mass audience.

It is not just talk. Crowley has already secured strong support from BT, Lastminute.com and Woolworths, one of Britain’s biggest music retailers.
