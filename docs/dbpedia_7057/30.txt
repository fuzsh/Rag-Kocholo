Ohio Northern University (ONU) is a private university located in Ada, Ohio. It was founded in 1871 and has a total undergraduate enrollment of 2,426 students. ONU is known for its nationally ranked academics, high graduation rates, and commitment to volunteer service. Ohio Northern University has an enrollment of 3,116 students, an acceptance rate of 69%, and a student-to-faculty ratio of 12:1. The university is a member of the North Coast Athletic Conference (NCAC) and competes in 22 varsity sports. ONU's mascot is the Polar Bear.

ONU offers over 60 undergraduate programs in five colleges: Arts & Sciences, Business, Engineering, Pharmacy, and Law. ONU also offers several graduate programs, including master's degrees in business administration, education, and law. The average annual tuition fee for international students at Ohio Northern University is $36,500 for arts and sciences.

ONU is located in a small town setting, but it is also close to the major cities of Toledo, Cleveland, and Columbus. The university is known for its beautiful campus and its friendly and supportive community.
