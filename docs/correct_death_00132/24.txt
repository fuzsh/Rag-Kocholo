Actor Anthony Quinn, famous for his movie roles in Zorba the Greek and Lawrence of Arabia died Sunday in a Boston hospital. He was 86.

His death was reported by the mayor of Providence, Rhode Island, Vincent "Buddy" Cianci, a friend of the actor. Cianci said Quinn died of respiratory failure.

The actor appeared in more than 100 feature films and won Academy Awards for best supporting actor in Viva Zapata! and Lust for Life.

He recreated his role in Zorba the Greek on stage in the 1980s.

Quinn was also an avid painter and sculptor. He began drawing portraits of Hollywood stars as a boy living in East Los Angeles.
