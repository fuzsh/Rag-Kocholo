Best Brazilian left wingers in Handball history

Bruno Souza

Bruno Souza is considered one of the best Brazilian left wings in the history of handball. He had a brilliant career, playing for clubs such as Esporte Clube Pinheiros and Teka Cantabria in Spain. Bruno was a key member of the Brazilian national team, helping the team win the Pan American Championships in 2003 and 2007. Known for his accurate shooting and leadership on the court, he left a lasting legacy in Brazilian handball.

Felipe Borges

Felipe Borges is one of the leading names in Brazilian handball, known for his technical ability and accurate shooting. He has played for clubs such as EC Pinheiros, BM Granollers and Montpellier HB. Borges was a key member of the Brazilian national team, helping them win the Pan American Championship in 2016. His ability to make decisions at critical moments makes him one of the most respected left-backs in the country.

Guilherme Valadão

Guilherme Valadão is one of Brazil’s most talented left-backs. He played for clubs such as Esporte Clube Pinheiros and was a key member of the Brazilian national team. Valadão stood out for his vision and ability to create plays, helping Brazil to reach new heights in international handball. His dedication and performance on the court inspired many young players. He can’t be left out of the list of the best Brazilian left-backs in the history of handball.

José Guilherme de Toledo

José Guilherme de Toledo, known as Zé Toledo, is one of Brazil’s leading left-backs. He has played for clubs such as BM Granollers and HC Dobrogea Sud. Zé Toledo is known for his powerful shot and finishing ability. André Soares

André Soares, also known as Andrezinho, is one of the big names in Brazilian handball. He played for clubs such as Esporte Clube Pinheiros and HC Taubaté. Andrezinho was a key member of the Brazilian national team, contributing to important victories in South American and Pan American championships.
