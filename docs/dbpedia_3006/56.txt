Biography

Abstract

August Carl Helmholz was Emeritus Professor of Physics at the University of California, Berkeley (1940-2003). His research interests included the cyclotron and high-energy physics.

May 24, 1915Birth, Evanston (Ill.).

1936Obtained AB, Harvard University, Cambridge (Mass.).

1936 – 1937Awarded Henry Fellowship, Cavendish Laboratory, University of Cambridge.

1940Obtained PhD in Physics, University of California, Berkeley, Berkeley (Calif.).

1940 – 2003Instructor and Physicist, Radiation Laboratory (1940-1942); Assistant Professor of Physics (1942-1948); Physicist, Manhattan Project, Berkeley Radiation Laboratory (1943-1946); Physicist, Radiation Laboratory (1946-1958); Associate Professor of Physics (1948-1951); Professor (1951-1980); Chair, Department of Physics (1955-1962); Physicist, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (1958-2003); and Emeritus Professor (1980-2003), University of California, Berkeley, Berkeley (Calif.).

1962 – 1963Guggenheim Fellow, European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN).

1964 – 1966Member, Governing Board, American Institute of Physics.

October 29, 2003Death, Lafayette (Calif.).
