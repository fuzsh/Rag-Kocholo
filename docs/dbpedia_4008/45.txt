Carlisle

Cathedral city and county town in England

Distance: Approx. 123 meters

Latitude and longitude: 54.89472222,-2.93638889

Carlisle ( kar-LYLE, locally KAR-lyle; from Cumbric: Caer Luel) is a cathedral city in the ceremonial county of Cumbria in England. It is the administrative centre of Cumberland Council which covers an area similar to the historic county of Cumberland. Carlisle's early history is marked by the establishment of a human settlement to serve castles along Hadrian's Wall in Britain.

Tullie House Museum and Art Gallery

Museum in Carlisle, United Kingdom

Distance: Approx. 148 meters

Latitude and longitude: 54.8952,-2.9405

Tullie House Museum and Art Gallery is a museum in Carlisle, England. Opened by the Carlisle Corporation in 1893, the original building is a converted Jacobean mansion, with extensions added when it was converted. At first the building contained the museum and also a library, an art school and a technical school.

Carlisle Cathedral

Church in Cumbria, England

Distance: Approx. 50 meters

Latitude and longitude: 54.89472222,-2.93861111

Carlisle Cathedral is a Grade I listed Anglican cathedral in the city of Carlisle, Cumbria, England. It was founded as an Augustinian priory and became a cathedral in 1133. It is also the seat of the Bishop of Carlisle.

Luguvalium

Distance: Approx. 78 meters

Latitude and longitude: 54.895,-2.937

Luguvalium (or Luguvalium Carvetiorum) was an ancient Roman city in northern Britain located within present-day Carlisle, Cumbria, and may have been the capital of the 4th-century province of Valentia. It was the northernmost city of the Roman Empire.

Carlisle Market Hall

Market hall in Cumbria, UK

Distance: Approx. 148 meters

Latitude and longitude: 54.896,-2.9365

Carlisle Market Hall is a market hall located in Carlisle, Cumbria, England. It was built between 1887 and 1889 for Carlisle Corporation by Arthur Cawston and Joseph Graham, both of Westminster, with ironwork manufactured by Cowans, Sheldon & Company. It is one of the few covered Victorian markets remaining in the country.

Carlisle city walls

Distance: Approx. 133 meters

Latitude and longitude: 54.894,-2.939

Carlisle city walls were a defensive structure surrounding the centre of Carlisle, Cumbria. The city walls ran from Carlisle Castle in the north-west of the city to The Citadel in the south-east. Between these points, the city was protected by the former North and East Walls (demolished around 1811) and the West Walls which remain largely intact.

Carlisle Guildhall

Distance: Approx. 115 meters

Latitude and longitude: 54.89488889,-2.93644444

Carlisle Guildhall is an historic building in Carlisle, Cumbria. It is in the city centre, on the corner of Fisher Street and Greenmarket. It is a Grade I listed building, listed on 1 June 1949.

Cumberland (unitary authority)

District in Cumbria, England

Distance: Approx. 123 meters

Latitude and longitude: 54.89472222,-2.93638889

Cumberland is a unitary authority area in Cumbria, England, which means that is a non-metropolitan county and district. It borders Scotland, Northumberland, Westmorland and Furness, and the Irish Sea. Part of the area is in the Lake District National Park and notable landmarks include Carlisle Cathedral, Carlisle Castle and Hadrian's Wall.
