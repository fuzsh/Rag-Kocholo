Jean-Luc Dehaene, the former Belgian Prime Minister, who died Thursday in France following a fall, was harshly felt at UEFA. The politician, aged 73, was in fact the chief investigator of the European football institution on financial fair play. “Mr Dehaene was a great man. A statesman and man of conviction,” said Michel Platini, UEFA president, in a statement. “For the love of football he agreed to play a key role in the establishment of financial fair play, becoming the first chairman of the UEFA Club Financial Control Panel and then the body's chief investigator. of financial control of clubs, a mission which was close to his heart and in which he will have invested until the end", underlined the former playmaker of the France team.
