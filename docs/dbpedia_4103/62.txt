Description

Goopy Gyne Bagha Byne is a whimsical Indian adventure comedy film released in 1969. It was penned and directed by the legendary Satyajit Ray, inspired by a tale from his grandfather, Upendrakishore Ray Chowdhury. Infused with fantasy and music, the film features captivating melodies and lyrics composed by Ray himself. Serving as the inaugural installment in the Goopy-Bagha series, it paved the way for two sequels: Hirak Rajar Deshe in 1980 and Goopy Bagha Phire Elo in 1992. The latter, although written by Satyajit Ray, was directed by his son, Sandip Ray.
