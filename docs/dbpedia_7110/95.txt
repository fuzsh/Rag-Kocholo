Fresh off reports that it’s exploring a venture with PSA Group to build cars in Europe, the chairman of Fiat Chrysler Automobiles said the automaker will remain an active player in helping to shape an automotive industry being transformed by technology.

John Elkann , the chairman and chief executive officer of Exor NV, wrote in a letterBloomberg Terminal to shareholders of the holding company about his belief Fiat Chrysler can benefit from the emergence of electrified and autonomously driven vehicles. Exor, with a 29 percent stake and 42 percent of voting rights, is the Italian-American carmaker’s largest holder.
