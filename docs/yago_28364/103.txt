Belgium’s former King Albert II and his wife, Queen Paola, have played host to his love child Princess Delphine at their home after years of legal battles over her paternity and royal title.

The awkward meeting at Belvédère Castle at the weekend came after a series of rulings by Belgian judges, who confirmed this year that the former monarch was Delphine Boël’s father and this month conferred on her the title of “her royal highness”.

“On Sunday, a new chapter, rich in emotions, peace of mind, understanding and hope, started,” said a joint statement issued by the royals, along with a picture. “During our meeting, each of us was able to express our feelings and experiences in serenity and empathy.
