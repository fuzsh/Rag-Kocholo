St. James Episcopal Church in Baltimore, MD is a historic congregation celebrating its 200th Anniversary Jubilee Year in 2024. Founded by Reverend William Levington, a free black man, the church has a rich legacy of vision, sacrifice, and Christian faith. Throughout its history, St. James has not only served as a place of worship but also established a school for free and enslaved blacks, producing numerous leaders in the Episcopal Church.
