Charles Bukowski was an influential German-American poet, novelist, and short story writer born in 1920 and raised in Los Angeles. Known for his raw, gritty style, Bukowski's work often reflected his own tumultuous life, including his struggles with alcoholism and marginalization.

Bukowski's writing delves deeply into the human condition, often portraying despair and the harsh realities of life. He critiques social structures and highlights the cyclical nature of dystopia, offering a stark, unfiltered view of society's underbelly.
