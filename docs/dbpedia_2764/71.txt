Synopsis

Presents the field journals of the Oxford University alumnus who taught geology at Colorado's School of Mines and who recorded in words and sketches the exciting discovery of dinosaur remains in the wilderness of Colorado and Wyoming between 1877 and 1880.

Synopsis

Arthur Lakes was an Oxford-educated clergyman and geologist. These field journals, written between 1877 and 1880 and discovered in 1994, are filled with his eyewitness accounts of the early days of vertebrate palaeontology in Colorado and Wyoming.
