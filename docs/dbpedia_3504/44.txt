The award-winning director Pratibha Parmar believes her first feature film, Nina’s Heavenly Delights, can do for Glasgow’s Asian culture what East is East, FilmFour’s affectionate comedy about a 1970s Asian family, did for Salford.

A romantic comedy, the movie is “an urban fairy story” that will bring “a touch of magical realism” to the city, she says.

East is East cost £2.5m to make in 1999 and took £10m at the British box office. Parmar hopes her movie, which has a budget of £4.5m, will boost Scotland’s profile as a film-making and holiday destination.

It tells the story of a female Asian chef who falls in love after returning home to her family’s Glasgow curry house from the bright lights of London.
