Laurence Marks is the comedy writer who, along with his writing partner, Maurice Gran, is responsible for some of the best-loved, most-watched television sitcoms and comedy dramas of the Eighties and Nineties. These include Shine on Harvey Moon, Goodnight Sweetheart, Love Hurts, Birds of a Feather and my particular favourite, The New Statesman, starring a sensational but seemingly crazed Rik Mayall. Was Rik crazed? “Rik was a pleasure, an absolute pleasure, a gentleman of the first order,” Marks replies. “But also completely mad.
