Fern E. Adams OBITUARY

Fern E. Adams, 90, passed away on May 3, 2005 in Santa Rosa with her loving family at her side. She was born on “the kitchen table” at her family’s Black Butte ranch in Tehama County. She was the daughter of the late Eugene M. Corbett and Stella V. Wise.

She was the beloved wife of 67 years of the late Emmett C. Adams; loving mother of Robert E. Adams, Jean Orlando, John P. Adams (Sharon) and Judy Mahoney; adored grandmother of Barbara Warner (Art), Patricia Campbell (Neal), Richard Orlando (Katy), Robert Orlando (Cindy), Julie Hanover (Keith), David Adams (Kristiine), Matthew Mahoney and Lori Dees (Rene); 16 great-grandchildren and three great-grandchildren. She is also the sister of the late Paulyne Arata (Angelo) and brother Daryl Bond (Joyce) and numerous nieces and nephews.

She had a distinguished career in the retail industry. Community activities included first woman president of the Willows and Glenn County Chambers of Commerce, chair of the Willows Planning Commission. She was an active member of Soroptimist Club, a Girl Scout leader and PTA president and served as state president of the Native Daughters of the Golden West in 1964-65. At that time, she was selected to represent the state of California as a judge for the Freedoms Foundation in Valley Forge, Penn. Fern was elected “Buyer of the Year” for the San Francisco Apparel Mart twice. She lived in Willows for 50 years before moving to Santa Rose in 1980. She and Emmett returned to Willows in 1992 and after Emmett’s death she again resided in Santa Rosa.
