UEFA European Women’s Football Championship, also known as UEFA Women’s Euro, is the main international women’s football competition between UEFA national teams. Held every four years, one year after the UEFA Men’s European Championship, the tournament has been a landmark on the women’s football scene since its first edition in 1984.

History and Evolution:

The first edition of the UEFA Women’s Euro was held in Denmark, with Denmark winning the title at home. Since then, the tournament has grown in popularity and competitiveness, attracting more and more attention and audience. The number of participating teams has increased over the years, from 6 in the inaugural edition to 16 in the most recent edition, held in 2022.

Format and Regulation:

The current format of the UEFA Women’s Euro consists of a qualifying round, followed by a final round with 16 teams divided into four groups. Teams face each other in single games within their groups, with the best two from each group advancing to the quarterfinals. From this stage onwards, the games are eliminatory, with semi-finals, final and third place dispute.

Champions and Highlights:

A Germany is the most successful team in the history of the tournament, with eight titles won (1989, 1991, 1995, 2001, 2005, 2009, 2013 and 2017). Other teams to have lifted the trophy include Norway (twice), the Netherlands (once) and England (once). The most recent edition, held at England in 2022, it was historic for the hosts, who won their first title by beating Germany in the final.

Impact and Importance:

The UEFA Women’s Euro plays a fundamental role in the promotion and development of women’s football in Europe. The tournament serves as a platform for the continent’s best female players to showcase their talents, inspiring new generations of athletes and attracting more fans to the sport. Furthermore, the event generates a significant economic impact on the cities and countries that host it.

2025 Edition:

The next edition of the UEFA Women’s Euro will be held at Switzerland in 2025, with the final phase scheduled to take place between July 2nd and July 27th. The expectation is that the tournament will once again be a great success, consolidating the Women’s Euro as one of the main sporting events in the world.
