Robert Bernard Sherman (1925 - 2012) was an American songwriter who specialized in musical films with his brother, Richard Morton Sherman (1928). The Sherman brothers were responsible for more motion picture musical song scores than any other songwriting team in film history. Some of best-known songs were incorporated into animation films including: Mary Poppins, The Jungle Book (1967), The AristoCats (1970), The Many Adventures of Winnie the Pooh (1977), Chitty Chitty Bang Bang, The Tigger Movie (2000) .

Maurice Auguste Chevalier (1888 - 1972) was a French actor, cabaret singer and entertainer. His trademark attire was a boater hat and tuxedo.

Floyd Huddleston (1918 - 1991) was an American songwriter, screenwriter, and television producer.

Al Rinker (1907 - 1982) was an American musician. Phil Harris (Wonga Philip Harris) (1904 - 1995) was an American comedian, actor, singer, and jazz musician. As a voice actor, he played Baloo in The Jungle Book (1967), Thomas O'Malley in The Aristocats (1970), and Little John in Robin Hood (1973).

Benjamin Sherman Crothers (1910 - 1986), known professionally as Scatman Crothers, was an American actor and musician. He was also a prolific voiceover actor who provided the voices of Meadowlark Lemon in the Harlem Globetrotters animated TV series, Jazz the Autobot in The Transformers and The Transformers: The Movie (1986), the title character in Hong Kong Phooey, and Scat Cat in the animated Disney film The Aristocats (1970).

Louise "Liz" English (1962) is an English actress.

Thurl Ravenscroft (1914 - 2005) was an American actor, voice actor and bass singer. He did voice-over work as Monstro the Whale from Pinocchio (1940), Card Painter from Alice In Wonderland (1951), Al the Alligator (Singing Pound Dogs) from Lady and the Tramp (1955), Captain the Horse from 101 Dalmatians (1961), Sir Bart from The Sword In The Stone (1963), Colonel Hathi's crew from The Jungle Book (1967), Kirby the vacuum cleaner from The Brave Little Toaster (1987).
