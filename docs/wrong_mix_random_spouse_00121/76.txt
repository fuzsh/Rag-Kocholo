Who is Serena Williams?

Serena Jameka Williams is a former professional tennis player from the United States. She was born on September 26, 1981, in Saginaw, Michigan, and currently resides in Palm Beach Gardens, Florida. Williams turned pro in October 1995 and retired from professional tennis in September 2022. Throughout her career, she played right-handed with a two-handed backhand.

Williams was coached by Richard Williams from 1994 to 2022, Oracene Price from 1994 to 2003, Patrick Mouratoglou from 2012 to 2022, Eric Hechtman in 2022, and Rennae Stubbs in 2022. She has earned US$94,816,730 in prize money, which ranks her first in all-time rankings. In her singles career, Williams had a record of 858-156, winning 73 career titles, which ranks her fifth in overall rankings.

She achieved her highest singles ranking at number one on July 8, 2002. Williams has won 23 Grand Slam doubles titles and had a career record of 192-35, with her highest doubles ranking at number one on June 21, 2010. Williams’ Grand Slam singles titles include seven Australian Open titles, three French Open titles, seven Wimbledon titles, and six US Open titles.

She has also won several other tournaments, including the Grand Slam Cup, the Tour Finals, and a gold medal at the 2012 Olympic Games. In mixed doubles, Williams had a career record of 27-4 and won Grand Slam titles at Wimbledon in 1998 and the US Open in 1998. She has also had success in team competitions, winning the Fed Cup in 1999 with a record of 17-3 and the Hopman Cup in 2003 and 2008. Additionally, Williams won four gold medals representing the United States at the Olympic Games in doubles in 2000 and 2008, and in singles and doubles in 2012.
