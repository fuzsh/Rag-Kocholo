Located on the Menashe plateau between the Carmel mountain range and the Jezreel Valley. In 2011 it became a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.

Ramot Menashe area contains of forests, streams and open spaces with natural gall oak forests, open spaces, fields, villages, orchards, springs and flowing streams. Listed by UNESCO as a biosphere, Ramot Menashe is a model of sustainable coexistence between man and nature in Israel.
