Shane Battier Bio

Shane Battier is a retired American professional basketball player who played in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for 13 seasons. Born on September 9, 1978 in Birmingham, Michigan, Battier attended Detroit Country Day School where he played for the school's basketball team, leading them to three consecutive state championships. He then went on to attend Duke University from 1997 until 2001 where he played basketball and was a two-time consensus first-team All-American. Battier is widely regarded as one of the best defensive players in the history of the NBA. Following his successful college career, Battier was selected by the Memphis Grizzlies in the 2001 NBA Draft. He spent six seasons with the Grizzlies before being traded to the Houston Rockets for the 2006-07 season. Battier also played for the Miami Heat, where he won back-to-back NBA championships in 2012 and 2013. He retired after the 2013-14 season and joined the Heat's front office as their Director of Basketball Development and Analytics. Throughout his NBA career , Battier was known for his exceptional defense and his ability to knock down three-point shots. He was a two-time NBA champion and was named to the NBA All-Defensive team twice. Battier scored over 9,000 points and grabbed over 4,000 rebounds during his career . In addition to his accomplishments on the court, Battier is known for his philanthropic work. He founded The Battier Take Charge Foundation in 2008 which provides resources and support for education initiatives in underserved communities.
