Biography

Chris Sarandon was born on July 24, 1942 in Beckley, West Virginia to Cliffie Cardullias and Christopher Sarandon (Sarondonethes), both of Greek descent.

Chris got his start in music, playing the drums and singing back-up with a local band called The Teen Tones.

He graduated from Woodrow Wilson High School in 1960 and attended West Virginia University majoring in speech while appearing in musical productions.

Chris went on to attend the Catholic University of America in Washington, D.C. where he received his master's degree in theater and also met his first wife, actress Susan Sarandon.

Moving to New York in 1968, he appeared on Guiding Light for two years as well as appearing on stage on Broadway throughout the 1970's.

Chris made his film debut in Dog Day Afternoon and was nominated for an Oscar in 1976 for Best Actor in a Supporting Role. That year he also received a nomination for a Golden Globe for Best Male Acting Debut in a Motion Picture for the same role.

He is probably best known for his 1987 portrayal of the evil prince in "The Princess Bride."

He was divorced from Susan Sarandon in 1979. He then married the model, Lisa Ann Cooper in 1980 and had 3 children. They were divorced in 1989. He is now married to Joanna Gleason since 1994.
