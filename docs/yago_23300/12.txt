Nazli Sabri, Queen of Egypt

1936 (10 July) dinner menu card from the Palais d'Edfina, with bold signature of "Nazli" in ink at top; a couple of age marks. Photo

Nazli Sabr (1894 – 1978) was Queen of Egypt from 1919 to 1936 as the second wife of King Fuad. Following the death of King Fuad in 1936, her son Farouk became the new King of Egypt, and she became the Queen Mother. In 1946, Nazli left Egypt and went to the United States because of health problems.
