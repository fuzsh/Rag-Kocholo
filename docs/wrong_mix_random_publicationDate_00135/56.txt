The director of the famous movie "Rashomon" is Akira Kurosawa.

Akira Kurosawa (1910–1998) was a prominent and influential figure in the world of cinema.

He was a Japanese film director and screenwriter, who directed 30 films in a career spanning 57 years.

His films have had a significant impact on global cinema, influencing many filmmakers around the world.

"Rashomon" (1950) is one of Akira Kurosawa's most distinguished films.

It depicts a complex narrative of the same incident (a samurai's murder and the rape of his wife) told from the perspectives of four different characters—a bandit, the wife, a samurai (through a medium), and a woodcutter.

Each of them has a different version of the events, leaving the viewer to grapple with the elusive nature of the truth.

The movie is noteworthy for its narrative structure, its intensive examination of subjective reality versus objective truth, and its profound exploration of ethical complexities.

It has won numerous awards, including the Golden Lion at the Venice Film Festival in 1951, and it played a significant role in introducing the Western world to Japanese cinema.
