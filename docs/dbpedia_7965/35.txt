Marthe Keller: The Swiss Actress Who Captivated Hollywood

Marthe Keller is a Swiss actress known for her captivating performances in films like Marathon Man (1976) and Black Sunday (1977), earning her a Golden Globe nomination for Best Actress in a Supporting Role.

A Career Spanning Decades and Genres

Keller's career in film began in the 1960s, with early appearances in German and French films. Her breakthrough role came with the 1971 film Un Cave, followed by La Raison du Plus Fou (1973) and Toute Une Vie...And Now My Love (1974).

Breakthrough in Hollywood

Keller's most notable American film appearances include her Golden Globe-nominated performance as Dustin Hoffman's girlfriend in Marathon Man (1976) and her striking portrayal of a femme fatale Palestinian terrorist in Black Sunday (1977).

She also acted alongside Al Pacino in the romantic drama film Bobby Deerfield (1977), based on Erich Maria Remarque's novel Heaven Has No Favorites. The film sparked a romantic relationship between the two actors.

Awards and Honors

In 2001, Keller was nominated for a Tony Award for Best Featured Actress for her performance as Mrs. Bertholt in the Broadway adaptation of Abby Mann's play Judgment at Nuremberg.

Presidential Prestige

In April 2016, Keller was announced as the president of the jury for the Un Certain Regard section of the 2016 Cannes Film Festival, a testament to her esteemed reputation in the film industry.

Beyond Film: A Multifaceted Artist

In addition to her work in film, Keller has developed a career in classical music as a speaker and has worked in theater, including her Tony Award-nominated performance in Judgment at Nuremberg.

Tribute to a Legacy

Marthe Keller's remarkable career is a testament to her dedication, versatility, and talent. From her breakout roles in the 1970s to her continued work in film, theater, and music, Keller has solidified her place as a silver screen legend.
