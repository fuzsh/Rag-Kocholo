Kip Christian Moore was born on April 1, 1980, in Tifton, Georgia, United States.

Moore's interest in music began at a young age, and he started playing guitar while attending college.

After graduating, he moved to Nashville, Tennessee, to pursue a career in music.

Moore signed with MCA Nashville in 2010 and released his debut single, "Mary Was the Marrying Kind," followed by his debut album, "Up All Night," in 2012.

"Up All Night" produced several successful singles, including "Somethin' 'Bout a Truck," "Beer Money," and "Hey Pretty Girl," which helped establish Moore as a rising star in country music.

Moore's subsequent albums include "Wild Ones" (2015) and "Slowheart" (2017). Both albums received critical acclaim and spawned hits such as "I'm to Blame," "More Girls Like You," and "Last Shot."

Known for his energetic live performances and heartfelt songwriting, Moore has built a loyal fan base within the country music community.

Some of his other popular songs include "Running for You," "She's Mine," and "Dirt Road."
