Second feature from Sixth Generation filmmaker Guan Hu. Understandably never garners attention from scholars of this movement. (It doesn’t even have an IMDB page, for that matter...) Unlike 1994’s Dirt, which — along with Zhang Yuan’s Beijing Bastards, from the year prior — offers an urgent look at Beijing's counter-culture and its State-defying, rock music-playing youths, this is a casual romantic comedy, occasionally skirting subversiveness but always settling for nationalist sentiment. Its meet-cute is set in a city bracing for the announcement of the 2000 Olympic Games (which ultimately saw Beijing’s hosting bid lose-out to Sydney’s, a twist that here serves as a ballast for our characters’, and the nation’s, dogged resilience).
