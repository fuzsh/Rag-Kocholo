What is a name that means night?

Names that mean nighttime are common all over the world.

Our list features names from places as far-flung as Japan, Turkey, India, Hawaii, Polynesia, and different parts of Africa.

There are also a few options inspired by the mythology of the ancient world.

While many of the names on our list refer to the nighttime, others are related to the night sky.

There is a variety of astral names that talk about the stars and moon, names about flowers that bloom at night, and names that speak of the night rain.

Navigating the night reveals so much possibility when it comes to naming your little one.

What is a girl name that means night?

Ajambo: (African, Luo) Ajambo means “born at night.”

Amaris: (Spanish) Meaning “child of the moon”

Amaya: (Japanese) This name, which means “night rain,” has been increasing in popularity in recent years. Amaya shortens to the sweet Maya.

Ankareeda: (Indian) A name you’re not likely to hear often, Ankareeda is an Indian name meaning “night star.”

Anniki: (Finnish) The name of the goddess of the night in Finnish mythology, Anniki is often shortened to Nikki.

Artemis: (Ancient Greek) One of the most-loved Greek deities, Artemis is the goddess of the hunt, the wilderness, wild animals, nature, vegetation, childbirth, the moon, and chastity.

Asra: (Arabic) This name, which means “travels at night,” can also be spelled with an “I”: Isra.

Astra: (Greek) Meaning “star”.

Aylin: (Turkish) This name refers to the halo around the moon.

Bellatrix: Any Harry Potter fans out there? You might associate this with the wild and powerful witch Bellatrix Lestrange. Bellatrix is one of the stars in the Orion constellation.

Celeste: (Latin) Meaning “heavenly.”

Ciara: (Irish) This Gaelic name means “night.”

Cynthia: (Greek) Cynthia is another name for the moon goddess Artemis in Greek mythology.

Danica: (Slavic) This Slavic-origin name means “morning star.”

Deva: (Hindi) A Hindi moon goddess, Deva also means “divine” and “godlike.”

Diana: (Latin) Belonging to the much-loved Princess Diana, this girls’ name refers to a Roman goddess associated with hunting and virginity and, in later literature, the moon.

Estelle: (French) Estelle means “star,” and is often shortened to Stella or Elle.

Esther: (Hebrew) A Biblical name, Esther also means “star.”

Hesper: (Ancient Greek) Another star name, Hesper means “evening star.”

Ilta: (Finnish) Meaning “night”

Jaci: (Native American) Meaning “moon”

Jemisha: (Sanskrit) A powerful name, Jemisha means “queen of the midnight darkness.”

Layla: (Arabic) An Arabic name that has long been popular in English, Layla can also be spelled Leila or Laila. It means “night” and originally comes from the Persian and Arabic story of Layla and Majnun.

Luna: (Ancient Roman) Luna is the Roman goddess of the moon, and a popular girls’ name — with good reason. It’s lyrical and gentle while still boasting a snappy two-syllable sound.

Lyra: (Ancient Greek) Ed Sheeran recently named his baby girl Lyra, which is bound to give it a popularity boost. It refers to a constellation that is named after the lyre of Orpheus.

Liviana: (Hebrew) A popular Hebrew name, Livinia means “white moon.”

Lulana: (African) Lulana means “shining moon.”

Koko: (Native American) A short and punchy name, Koko means “night.”

Miyako: (Japanese) Depending on the kanji used, Japanese names can have different meanings. If spelled with a certain way, Miyako means “beautiful night child.”

Nisa: (Arabic) This name has Arabic and Hebrew origins and means “night.”

Nisha: (Sanskrit) A Sanskrit name, Nisha means “night.”

Nova: (Latin) Nova is an astrological term that refers to a star that increases in brightness and then fades. It also means “new.”

Nyx: (Greek) A Greek name that means “night,” Nyx looks and sounds like a snazzy, modern name. Its male version is Nox.

Ratree: (Thai) Ratree refers to night jasmine.

Rhiannon: (Welsh) Rhiannon is the goddess of the moon in Welsh mythology.

Selena: (Greek) A variant name for the moon goddess Artemis

Shirina: (Hindi) An Indian name, Shirina means “night.”

Yvaine: (Scottish) A traditional Scottish name, Yvaine means “evening star.”

Nash: (English) Nash refers to a star in the constellation Sagittarius.

Nishant: (Sanskrit) This name points to the hours before dawn, it means “end of night.”

Orion: (Ancient Greek) Orion is one of the most popular night names for boys right now. It refers to one of the brightest constellations in the sky, which is named after Orion, the huntsman in Greek mythology.

Rajnish: (Sanskrit) Meaning “the lord of the night,” which is what the moon is called in Hindi.

Raka: (Sanskrit) A Sanskrit name, Raka means “full moon.”

Samir: (Arabic) A gentle name with a beautiful meaning, Samir is an Arabic name that means “a friend to talk with in the evening.”

Sterling: (Old English) Sterling means “little star.”

Tarek: (Arabic) This Arabic name means “morning star.”

Tynan: (Irish) In Gaelic, Tynan means “dark.”

Yiska: (Native American, Navajo) Another reference to the start of a new day, YIska means “night is over.”
