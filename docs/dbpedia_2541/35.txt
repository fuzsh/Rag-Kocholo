The Trusts Arena, Auckland, New Zealand

Latitude and longitude coordinates are: -36.860260, 174.635300.

The Trusts Arena is a multi-purpose arena and an entertainment facility with a capacity of 4,901 people, located at 65-67 Central Park Drive, Henderson, Auckland, New Zealand. The stadium hosts national and international sports matches, music concerts, conventions, trade shows, and other events. It hosted the 2010 Australasian Gaelic Games. It was designed by Warren and Mahoney, opened on 11 September 2004. Owned by Waitakere City Stadium Trust, the arena also hosts boxing events and has its own boxing gym, named Arena Boxing. There is another facility adjacent to the arena, called Douglas Track & Field. Trusts Stadium is the main venue for The Northern Mystics, a New Zealand netball team based in Auckland.
