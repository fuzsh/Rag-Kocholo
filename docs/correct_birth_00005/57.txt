The council house in Liverpool which was home to Paul McCartney's family, and which was dubbed the birthplace of The Beatles, has been acquired by the National Trust, it announced today (Monday). The terraced house, 20 Forthlin Road, Liverpool, was McCartney's family home for nine years. McCartney and John Lennon used it for writing and rehearsing in the early days before and after they sprang to fame. See PA Story SHOWBIZ Beatles House.
