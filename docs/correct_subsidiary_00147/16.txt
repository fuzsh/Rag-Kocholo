Intro and history: Oracle Corporation is an American multinational computer technology corporation, founded in 1977 by Larry Ellison, Bob Miner, and Ed Oates. The company specializes in developing and marketing database software and technology, cloud engineered systems, and enterprise software products. Oracle is known for its own brand of database management systems and is the second-largest software company by revenue and market capitalization. Industry: Computer software, Cloud computing Product, Services and Operations: Oracle provides a wide range of software and services, including cloud solutions, database management systems, servers, storage solutions, engineered systems, and more. The company also offers consulting services and operates Oracle University for training. Funding Details: Oracle is a publicly traded company listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE: ORCL). As of 2021, its market capitalization is approximately $180 billion. Acquisitions: Oracle has made numerous acquisitions over the years, including PeopleSoft in 2005, Siebel Systems in 2006, BEA Systems in 2008, Sun Microsystems in 2010, and NetSuite in 2016. Awards and Achievements: Oracle has received numerous awards and recognitions, including being named to the Fortune 500 list, the Forbes Global 2000 list, and the Fortune World's Most Admired Companies list.
