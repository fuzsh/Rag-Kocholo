As Secretary of the Royal Commission on the Ancient and Historical Monuments of Scotland (RCAHMS), Kenneth Steer was able to combine his academic rigour as an archaeologist with his talent for administration, to guide the commission through a period of consolidation and expansion, which shaped its future development.

Kenneth Arthur Steer was born in 1913. The son of a headmaster, he was brought up in Rotherham and attended Wath Grammar School, before reading history at the University of Durham. Graduating in 1935, he remained at the university to undertake doctoral research on Roman Co Durham, for which he was awarded a PhD in 1938.
