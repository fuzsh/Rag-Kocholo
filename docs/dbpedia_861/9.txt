Biography

Lillian (Massey) Treble is Notable.

Lillian (Massey) Treble is/was a significant Ontarian .

Lillian Massey Treble was a Canadian philanthropist who founded the Fred Victor Mission and the Lillian Massey School of Household Science and Art in Toronto, Ontario.[1]

Born about 1854, she was the only daughter of Hart Massey and Eliza Phelps. She spent her childhood in Newcastle, Durham, Ontario.[2]

She married John Mill Treble in 1897 in Toronto.[3]

By the 1911 Census of Canada, she was widowed and living in Toronto with a secretary, four domestic servants, a coachman, a gardener and his wife, and three boarders.[4]

She died in 1915 in Santa Barbara, California,[5] and was buried at Mount Pleasant Cemetery, Toronto.[6]
