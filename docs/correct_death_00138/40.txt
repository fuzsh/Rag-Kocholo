nrhp # 79000318- The Franklin Pierce House was an historic house at 52 South Main Street in Concord, New Hampshire, United States. Built in 1852, it was a significant local example of Second Empire architecture, and was one of two surviving Concord homes of President Franklin Pierce at the time of its listing on the National Register of Historic Places in 1979.[1] Both Pierce and his wife Jane died in this house.[2] It was destroyed by fire on September 17, 1981.
