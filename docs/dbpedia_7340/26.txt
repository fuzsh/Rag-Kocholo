2012-13 Pittsburgh Penguins Results

The Pittsburgh Penguins of the National Hockey League ended the 2012-13 season with a record of 36 wins and 12 losses, with a 3-0 showing in shootouts for 72 points, finishing first in the NHL's Atlantic Division. The team compiled a postseason mark of 8-7.

The Penguins scored 165 goals, most in the league. Pittsburgh gave up 119 goals. Chris Kunitz netted 22 goals for the team, while Sidney Crosby contributed 41 assists. Marc-Andre Fleury earned 23 wins in front of the net for the squad and a team-best 2.39 goals against average, pacing goalies with significant playing time.

Dan Bylsma coached the team. The 2012-13 Pittsburgh Penguins lost in the NHL conference finals.
