Episode Number: 342

Doug Turner & Allen Walsh Early voting is now underway in the June Primaries. New Mexico in Focus continues our conversations with the state’s gubernatorial candidates. This week, Correspondent Gwyneth Doland sits down with republican hopefuls Doug Turner and Allen Weh. Find out what they think about the economy, public safety, immigration reform, and their chances of going up against Diane Denish in the general election. Also, on the show this week, how cities are handling the ongoing budget struggles. Mayors and representatives from Albuquerque, Santa Fe, and Rio Rancho talk about making tough financial decisions and how they’re working together to create new jobs. Then, our Line panelists weigh in on President Obama’s nominee to the Supreme Court, potential strife in the state Republican Party, and a massive jet fuel leak under Kirtland Air Force Base. Guests: Gene Grant (Host), Gwyneth Doland (NMiF Correspondent and Editor, New Mexico Independent), Doug Turner (Republican Gubernatorial Candidate), Allen Weh (Republican Gubernatorial Candidate), Richard Berry (Albuquerque Mayor), Tom Swisstack (Rio Rancho Mayor), and Kate Noble (Santa Fe Economic Development Department). Panelists: Sophie Martin (Managing Editor, DukeCityFix.com), Jim Scarantino (Editor, New Mexico Watchdog), Richard Romero (Former Senate President Pro Tem, Albuquerque), and Arthur Alpert (ABQJournalWatch.com).
