Mahesh Shrinivas Bhupathi is a former Indian tennis player born on 07th June 1974 in Madras, Tamil Nadu. Mahesh used to compete in the doubles category and was the World No. 1. He pursued graduation at the University of Mississippi, United States. He is now married to Lara Dutta and has a daughter, Saira. As a tennis player, Mahesh Bhupathi played the French Open in 1997 with his Japanese partner Rika Hari in the mixed doubles category. Together they won the French Open, making him the first Indian to achieve a victory at a major tournament. In 1999, Mahesh and his Indian partner Leander Paes became the first pair to reach the finals of all four Grand Slams since 1952.

They later achieved World No.1 rank in the men's doubles team. After winning seven ATP Tour titles, they were also World No.1 in ATP rankings and even won the gold medal at the Asian Games in 2002 in the men’s doubles category, which was the final game they played together. In 2006, Bhupathi won the Australia Open under the mixed doubles category, paired with Martina Higgins. This win allowed Mahesh entry into the elite group of tennis players who had a ‘career Grand Slam’, i.e., who won all four Grand Slams in the same year. He was a member of the Indian Davis Cup team from 1995 to 2011 and was named the non-playing David Cup captain in 2016.
