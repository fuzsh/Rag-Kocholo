Gerald Wilburn Page (born August 12, 1939) is an American writer and editor. His oeuvre has consisted of short stories and articles related to fantasy, science fiction, mystery and horror, particularly weird horror.

He sold his first story to the magazine Analog where it appeared in 1963.

Page acquired Coven 13 magazine from Arthur H. Landis and retitled it Witchcraft and Sorcery. The retitled magazine lasted for six issues. Page acted as editor with Jerry Burge serving as art director.

In 1969 he joined the editorial staff of TV Guide.

He edited DAW Books's anthology series The Year's Best Horror Stories from 1976 to 1979.
