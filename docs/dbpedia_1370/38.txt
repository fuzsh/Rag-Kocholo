ABU Golden Kite World Song Festival

The ABU Golden Kite World Song Festival’s were held from 1989 to 1991 and were organised by the Asia-Pacific Broadcasting Union. The ABU Golden Kite World Song Festival’s were a competitive contest held in the Malaysian capital Kuala Lumpur and broadcast across the region as well as Europe. Read more about the contest here.

Finland participated in all two editions of the contest:

1990 – Gutter Twins – “Kun tuneet kulejetta“

1991 – Samuli Edelmann – “Peggy“

Eurovoice

EuroVoice European Music Contest was a Song Contest held in 2010, aimed at being an alternative to the Eurovision Song Contest. Participating songs were uploaded to the Eurovoice website, with the entries then selected from the submissions to compete in the contest.

Finland was represented by Fadeout, the group however did not qualify for the live Super Final in Athens.

Intervision Song Contest

The Intervision Song Contest was first held from 1965 to 1968, 1977 to 1980 and was revived again in 2008. The contest was first held as an Eastern bloc version of the Eurovision Song Contest, while the 2008 event was between countries from the Commonwealth of Independent States. During its history, 28 countries competed.

Liet International

Liet International is a song contest for regional and minority languages spoken across Europe. The contest was first held in 2002 as an evolution of Liet, a Frisian language song contest held in the Netherlands. The competition is open to singers and bands from across Europe.

Finland has been represented by the Sápmi from its Northern and Inari groups:

2009 – SomBy – “Ii iđida” – 1st – 85 points

2014 – Aila-duo – “Naharij kandâ” – 2nd – 64 points

MGP Nordic

MGP Nordic was created in 2002 following the creation of Melodi Grand Prix Junior by the Danish public broadcaster DR in 2000. During the course of its history; Denmark, Finland, Norway and Sweden would compete. The contest would be held in 2002 and from 2006 to 2009.

Finland debuted in MGP Nordic in 2007 and was the last country to debut in the contest:

2007 – Sister Twister – “Fröken Perfekt” – Did not qualify for the Superfinal

2007 – Linn Nygård – “En liten önskan” – 4th – 6,545 points

2008 – Big Bang! – “Här är vi” – Did not qualify for the Superfinal

2008 – Footboys – “Fotboll” – 4th – 70 points

2009 – The Black White Boys – “Kommer du ihåg mig?” – Did not qualify for the Superfinal

2009 – Amanda Sjöholm – “Jag vill leva” – 3rd – 20 points

Sámi Grand Prix

Sámi Grand Prix has been held annually since 1990 as part of the Sámi Easter Festival in Kautokeino in Norway. The region of Sápmi covers northern Norway, Sweden, Finland and Russia, and has a population of between 80-100,000 Sámi. To date the most successful nation in the contest is Norway.

The contest is split into two categories the Song Contest or the Joik contest. Finland’s winners are:

Joik Contest

2006 – Anna-Reetta Niemelä – “Golleeatnama mánná“

Song Contest

1994 – Inger Marie Gaino Nilut and Niko Valkeapää – “Duinna gávnnadit” (Joint victory with Norway)

1995 – Niko Valkeapää – “Vádjolus“

2005 – Poppoo – “Giella ii leat jáddan“

2009 – SomBy – “Ii iđida”

2014 – Aila-duo – “Naharij kandâ“

2021 – Lávre & Hilda – “Jođi” (Joint victory with Norway)
