TORRANCE, Calif., July 22—William L. O'Donovan, a vice president for sales of the Cela nese Corporation of America from 1951 to 1961, died here yesterday after a stroke. He was 64 years old.

Mr. O'Donovan had been city editor of The White Plains (N.Y.) Daily Reporter, now The Reporter‐Dispatch, before join ing the Celanese war industries division in 1941. Later sales manager, he retired as vice president, and served as a con sultant until several years ago.

Born in Far Rockaway, Queens, Mr. O'Donovan attend ed Columbia University. He had been a major with the Seventh Regiment, New York National Guard.

Surviving are his widow, the former Cicely Clark; a son, Wil liam C. of Williamsburg, Va.; three daughters, Mrs. Valerie Curran of Edina, Minn.; Mrs. Molly Gorman of San Marino, Calif., and Mrs. Cecely Gargaro of Los Angeles; two brothers; a sister and eight grandchil dren.
