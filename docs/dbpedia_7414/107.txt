Tommy Tutone

Tommy Tutone is an American rock band known for their iconic hit song “867-5309/Jenny”. The band was formed in 1978 in California by lead vocalist Tommy Heath and guitarist Jim Keller. Their music style combines elements of power pop, rock, and new wave, which garnered them a loyal following in the late 1970s and early 1980s.

Tommy Tutone released their self-titled debut album in 1980, which featured their breakthrough single “867-5309/Jenny”. The song became a massive hit, reaching number 4 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and achieving platinum certification. The catchy melody and memorable chorus made the track an instant classic, solidifying Tommy Tutone’s place in rock music history.

The success of “867-5309/Jenny” propelled Tommy Tutone into the limelight, and they continued to release music throughout the 1980s. Their follow-up album, “Tommy Tutone 2” (1981), spawned several singles, including “Angel Say No” and “Which Man Are You”. The band’s energetic live performances and infectious hooks endeared them to fans across the United States and beyond.

In the UK, Tommy Tutone also found success with their music, with several of their songs charting on the UK Singles Chart. Their blend of rock and power pop resonated with British audiences, further cementing their reputation as a talented and versatile band.

Tommy Tutone’s discography includes a number of studio albums and compilations, showcasing their evolution as musicians and songwriters. Their music has endured over the years, with “867-5309/Jenny” remaining a radio staple and a beloved classic among fans of 1980s rock music.
