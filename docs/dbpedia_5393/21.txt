Pula – the largest resort city and port on the Adriatic Sea in the west of Croatia, in the south of the Istrian Peninsula. It is an economic and cultural center of Istria. The population of the city is 57,500 people (2011). It has a railroad station and the only international airport on the peninsula. Shipbuilding, cement, tobacco, leather, footwear and woodworking enterprises; important cargo port, naval base are located here. Pula’s harbor is considered one of the best and safest natural harbors in the world. In the Aquarium of the city, in the five halls of the old Austro-Hungarian fortress, the flora and fauna of the Northern Adriatic are represented.

According to legend, the foundation of Pula is associated with the myth of the inhabitants of Colchis, pursuing the Argonauts, who stole the Golden Fleece.
