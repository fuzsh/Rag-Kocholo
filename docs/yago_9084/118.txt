Tim Berners Lee is a British computer scientist who is widely credited with inventing the World Wide Web in 1989. He was born on June 8, 1955 in England and founded the World Wide Web Foundation in 2009 to improve the availability of the internet. His invention of the World Wide Web has revolutionized the way people communicate and access information.

About

British computer scientist who is widely credited with inventing the World Wide Web in 1989. He founded the World Wide Web Foundation in 2009, which works to improve the availability of the internet.

Before Fame

He got his degree in Physics from Queen's College, Oxford.

Trivia

He was knighted in 2004 by Queen Elizabeth II for his work.

Family Life

Tim's parents were named Mary Lee Woods and Conway Berners-Lee. He was married to Nancy Carlson from 1990 to 2011. Berners-Lee later remarried Rosemary Leith. He has two children, Ben and Alice.
