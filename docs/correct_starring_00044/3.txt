Who is Woody Harrelson?

This role quickly turned into a breakout success, endearing him to audiences with his portrayal of the charmingly naive bartender.

This role not only provided Harrelson with widespread recognition but also laid a solid foundation for his future career in Hollywood. Before his television breakthrough, Harrelson attended Hanover College in Indiana, where he began to cultivate his acting skills in theater, demonstrating early on his affinity for the stage—a passion that would significantly influence his acting style and choices in later years.

Over the years, he has built an impressive film career with notable performances in movies such as Natural Born Killers, The Hunger Games series, Zombieland, and Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri. Here’s the list of Woody Harrelson movies and TV shows.

1. Natural Born Killers (1994)

In Oliver Stone’s “Natural Born Killers,” Woody Harrelson played Mickey Knox, a psychotic serial killer on a cross-country killing spree. His portrayal of Mickey was both chilling and captivating, showcasing Harrelson’s ability to delve into dark and complex characters. The film stirred significant controversy upon its release due to its graphic content and commentary on media violence. Despite—or perhaps because of—this controversy, “Natural Born Killers” became a critical piece in discussions about violence in media. The film grossed over $50 million in the U.S., and Harrelson’s performance was praised for its intensity and depth, marking a significant departure from his previous comedic roles.

2. The People vs. Larry Flynt (1996)

In “The People vs. Larry Flynt,” Harrelson starred as Larry Flynt, the infamous publisher of “Hustler Magazine.” His portrayal explored Flynt’s legal battles to protect the First Amendment right to free speech. Harrelson’s performance earned him an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor, highlighting his transition from a television actor to a respected film star. The film itself garnered critical acclaim and sparked discussions on freedom of expression, grossing around $20 million domestically. Harrelson’s role in this film is often cited as one of the defining moments of his career, showcasing his ability to handle both the dramatic gravitas and the nuanced humor of the character.

3. No Country for Old Men (2007)

In the Coen Brothers’ critically acclaimed film “No Country for Old Men,” Woody Harrelson played Carson Wells, a seasoned and pragmatic bounty hunter. The film, an adaptation of Cormac McCarthy’s novel, explores themes of fate, violence, and morality in the American West. Harrelson’s character, though not the main protagonist, is integral to the narrative’s tension and progression. His portrayal of Carson Wells, a confident and somewhat cocky bounty hunter, provides a counterpoint to the film’s primary antagonist, Anton Chigurh, played by Javier Bardem.

The film was a commercial and critical triumph, grossing over $171 million worldwide against a budget of $25 million. “No Country for Old Men” received numerous accolades, including four Academy Awards: Best Picture, Best Director, Best Adapted Screenplay, and Best Supporting Actor for Bardem. Harrelson’s performance, particularly his pivotal confrontation scene with Chigurh, added depth to the film’s exploration of fate and inevitability. The film’s success reinforced Harrelson’s reputation as a versatile actor capable of delivering compelling performances in ensemble casts.

4. Zombieland (2009)

“Zombieland” marked a significant milestone in Woody Harrelson’s career, showcasing his talent in blending horror with comedy. In the film, Harrelson played Tallahassee, a fearless and eccentric zombie apocalypse survivor with a singular obsession: finding Twinkies. His performance was both humorous and endearing, providing much of the film’s comedic relief while also embodying the resilience and absurdity of survival in a post-apocalyptic world.

“Zombieland” was a box office hit, grossing over $102 million worldwide on a modest budget of $23.6 million. The film’s unique approach to the zombie genre, combining elements of road trip comedy and horror, resonated with audiences and critics alike. Harrelson’s portrayal of Tallahassee became iconic, and his chemistry with co-stars Jesse Eisenberg, Emma Stone, and Abigail Breslin was a key factor in the film’s success.

The film’s popularity led to a sequel, “Zombieland: Double Tap,” released ten years later in 2019. The sequel also performed well, grossing $122 million globally, proving the enduring appeal of the characters and the world they inhabit. Harrelson’s return as Tallahassee was met with enthusiasm, and his performance once again balanced humor with the grit required to survive in a zombie-infested landscape.

5. Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri (2017)

In “Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri,” Woody Harrelson delivered one of his most critically acclaimed performances as Chief Bill Willoughby. The film, directed by Martin McDonagh, tells the story of a grieving mother, played by Frances McDormand, who challenges the local authorities to solve her daughter’s murder by placing provocative billboards on the outskirts of town. Harrelson’s character, Chief Willoughby, is portrayed as a well-intentioned and compassionate police officer struggling with a terminal illness and the pressure of the unsolved case.

The film was both a critical and commercial success, grossing over $160 million worldwide against a production budget of $12 million. It received widespread acclaim for its sharp script, dark humor, and powerful performances. Harrelson’s portrayal of Chief Willoughby was particularly praised for its depth and nuance, adding emotional weight to the narrative and providing a counterbalance to McDormand’s fiery character.

6. True Detective (2014)

“True Detective” was a landmark television series that premiered on HBO in January 2014. The first season, created by Nic Pizzolatto, was an anthology crime drama that captivated audiences with its dark, philosophical storytelling and complex characters. Woody Harrelson played Detective Marty Hart, a deeply flawed but dedicated investigator. Alongside him, Matthew McConaughey portrayed the enigmatic and brooding Detective Rust Cohle.

The show was praised for its narrative depth, exploring themes of existentialism, morality, and the human condition. Harrelson’s portrayal of Marty Hart was particularly noted for its emotional complexity and realism. His character’s personal struggles and professional challenges added a rich layer to the series, making it not just a procedural drama but a profound character study.

Viewership and Cultural Impact: “True Detective” debuted to impressive ratings, with the premiere episode drawing approximately 2.3 million viewers. As the season progressed, viewership steadily increased, with the finale reaching an audience of 3.5 million viewers. The show’s unique storytelling format, which included a nonlinear narrative and detailed character development, received critical acclaim and reinvigorated interest in anthology series formats.

Legacy: The success of “True Detective” led to subsequent seasons, each featuring a new cast and storyline, maintaining the anthology format that the first season popularized. Harrelson’s and McConaughey’s performances were pivotal in setting a high standard for the series, and their dynamic on-screen partnership remains one of the most celebrated aspects of the show’s history.

2019 Hosting:

Harrelson’s most recent hosting gig in 2019 was tied to his role in “Zombieland: Double Tap.” The episode featured a mix of political satire and pop culture references. One of the standout sketches was a parody of the Democratic presidential debates, where Harrelson portrayed Joe Biden, drawing laughs for his humorous take on the politician. This episode saw a notable spike in viewership, reflecting Harrelson’s enduring popularity and ability to draw audiences.

Viewership and Popularity: Whenever Harrelson hosts SNL, the show typically experiences a boost in viewership. This trend reflects his status as a beloved figure in entertainment, whose presence on the show is eagerly anticipated by fans. The popularity of his episodes is a testament to his enduring appeal and the audience’s appreciation of his comedic style.

Woody Harrelson Awards and Nominations

Throughout his career, Woody Harrelson has accumulated a wealth of accolades, solidifying his reputation as a versatile and talented actor. His ability to seamlessly transition between comedy and drama has earned him praise and numerous awards and nominations, underscoring his notable impact on both film and television.

Academy Award Nominations

Woody Harrelson has received three Academy Award nominations, a testament to his remarkable performances and his ability to take on complex and challenging roles:

Best Actor for “The People vs. Larry Flynt” (1996): Harrelson’s portrayal of the controversial publisher Larry Flynt earned him his first Oscar nomination. The film, directed by Milos Forman, was lauded for its bold storytelling and Harrelson’s nuanced performance. This role marked a turning point in his career, showcasing his ability to handle dramatic material with depth and sensitivity.

Best Supporting Actor for “The Messenger” (2009): In “The Messenger,” Harrelson played Captain Tony Stone, a war-weary officer tasked with delivering news of soldiers’ deaths to their families. His powerful and empathetic performance earned him a nomination for Best Supporting Actor. The film was praised for its raw and honest depiction of the emotional toll of war, and Harrelson’s performance was a central pillar of its critical success.

Best Supporting Actor for “Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri” (2017): Harrelson’s role as Chief Bill Willoughby in this dark comedy-drama was widely acclaimed. The film, directed by Martin McDonagh, received multiple accolades, and Harrelson’s portrayal of a small-town police chief dealing with personal and professional challenges was both poignant and impactful. His nomination for Best Supporting Actor highlighted his ability to imbue characters with a deep sense of humanity.

Golden Globe Nominations

Harrelson has also been recognized by the Hollywood Foreign Press Association with several Golden Globe nominations, reflecting his versatility across various genres:

Best Actor in a Motion Picture – Drama for “The People vs. Larry Flynt” (1997): This nomination mirrored his Oscar nod for the same role, further cementing his performance as one of the year’s best.

Best Actor in a Supporting Role in a Motion Picture for “The Messenger” (2010): Again, echoing his Academy Award nomination, this Golden Globe nod acknowledged his powerful supporting performance.

Best Actor in a Supporting Role in a Motion Picture for “Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri” (2018): Harrelson’s performance in this critically acclaimed film continued to earn him accolades and recognition from the Golden Globes.

Emmy Awards

Harrelson’s television work has been equally celebrated, with multiple Emmy nominations highlighting his ability to excel in long-form storytelling:

Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series for “Cheers” (1987, 1988, 1990, 1991, 1992): Harrelson’s role as Woody Boyd on “Cheers” was beloved by audiences and critics alike. He won the Emmy for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series in 1989, marking a high point in his early career.

Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series for “True Detective” (2014): His role as Detective Martin Hart in HBO’s “True Detective” was a departure from his earlier comedic work, showcasing his dramatic range. This performance earned him an Emmy nomination and was pivotal in the show’s critical and commercial success.

Outstanding Guest Actor in a Comedy Series for “Saturday Night Live” (2019, 2020): Harrelson’s guest appearances on “Saturday Night Live” have also been recognized with Emmy nominations, reflecting his enduring talent and versatility in live television and sketch comedy.

Additional Accolades

Beyond these major awards, Harrelson has been honored by various film festivals and critics’ organizations. For instance:

Screen Actors Guild Awards: Harrelson has received multiple SAG Award nominations, including for his ensemble work in “No Country for Old Men” (2007) and “Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri” (2017). His performances have consistently contributed to the success of these films, highlighting his ability to collaborate effectively within an ensemble cast.

BAFTA Awards: He has also been nominated for BAFTA awards, including Best Supporting Actor for “Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri,” further affirming his international appeal and recognition.

Conclusion

Woody Harrelson is a versatile and talented actor with a long list of successful movies and TV shows. From his iconic role as Woody Boyd in Cheers to his intense performance in True Detective, he has shown his range and skill. His memorable roles in films like White Men Can’t Jump, Natural Born Killers, Zombieland, and The Hunger Games series have solidified his place in Hollywood. With three Academy Award nominations and a commitment to being an environmental activist, Woody Harrelson continues to be a beloved and respected figure in the entertainment industry.
