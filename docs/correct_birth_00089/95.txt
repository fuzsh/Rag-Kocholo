Bryan Adams was born on November 5, 1959 in Kingston, Ontario (Canada). He is a multi-awarded rock star, composer and photographer. He wrote and sang several successful rocksongs and ballads. His greatest hits are surely "Summer of '69" and his film songs "Everything I Do (I Do It For You)" (Robin Hood), "Have You Ever Really Loved A Woman" (Don Juan DeMarco) and the complete Soundtrack album for the Disney movie "Spirit". His cooperations with other artists, such as Sting and Rod Stewart, Tina Turner, Barbra Streisand, Ronan Keating or Spice Girl Mel C, have been very successful, too.
