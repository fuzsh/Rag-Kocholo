THIS week’s episode of CSI, Grave Danger, directed by Quentin Tarantino (pictured), was pulled by Five on grounds of sensitivity, featuring, as it does, a character who is buried alive (it will be shown next week). But it was significant also as the first step of a full-scale shift to the small screen for the writer-director, who will also be making a mini-series. He has decided that this is the best way to accommodate his increasingly voluminous storytelling style. “Most people aren’t down with four-hour movies,” he explained. “But TV has caught up with my aspirations.”
