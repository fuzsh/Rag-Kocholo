Angel Sandy Martínez (b: October 8, 1970) is a retired MLB catcher. Previously, Martínez played for the Toronto Blue Jays (1995-1997), Chicago Cubs (1998-1999), Florida Marlins (2000), Montreal Expos (2001), Cleveland Indians (2004), and Boston Red Sox (2004).

Martinez was signed by the Toronto Blue Jays as an amateur free agent in 1990 and made his MLB debut on June 24, 1995. He was traded to the Chicago Cubs on December 11, 1997, for minor leaguer Trevor Schaffer. On May 6, 1998, Martinez was catching when Kerry Wood tied the major league record for strikeouts in a 9 inning game.

He became a free agent after the 1999 season and signed with the Florida Marlins on December 6, 1999. A free agent at the end of the 2000 season, Martinez signed with the Montreal Expos on November 17, 2000. Martinez played one game for the Expos in 2001.
