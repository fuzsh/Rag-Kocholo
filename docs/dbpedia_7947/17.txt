Early Life and Background

Chris Sarandon's journey to stardom began with humble roots. Let's explore some intriguing facts about his early life and background.

Born on July 24, 1942, in Beckley, West Virginia, Chris Sarandon grew up in a small town.

His parents, Christopher and Cliffie Sarandon, were of Greek descent, adding a rich cultural heritage to his upbringing.

Chris attended Woodrow Wilson High School, where he first developed an interest in acting.

He earned a bachelor's degree in speech at West Virginia University, further honing his skills in performance.

Sarandon later pursued a master's degree in theater from The Catholic University of America in Washington, D.C.

Career Beginnings

Chris Sarandon's career took off with a mix of theater, television, and film roles. Here are some key moments from his early career.

His first major role was in the soap opera "The Guiding Light," where he played Dr. Tom Halverson.

Sarandon made his Broadway debut in 1968 with the play "The Rothschilds."

He appeared in several off-Broadway productions, showcasing his versatility as an actor.

His breakthrough film role came in 1975 when he starred as Leon Shermer in "Dog Day Afternoon."

This performance earned him an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor.

Iconic Roles

Chris Sarandon is best known for a few iconic roles that have left a lasting impact on audiences. Let's dive into some of these memorable performances.

He played the suave vampire Jerry Dandrige in the 1985 horror classic "Fright Night."

Sarandon voiced the character of Jack Skellington in Tim Burton's "The Nightmare Before Christmas."

In "The Princess Bride," he portrayed the villainous Prince Humperdinck, a role that remains beloved by fans.

He starred as Detective Mike Norris in the 1988 horror film "Child's Play."

Sarandon appeared in the 1994 film "The Resurrected," based on H.P. Lovecraft's novella.

Personal Life

Chris Sarandon's personal life has been as eventful as his career. Here are some interesting facts about his relationships and family.

He married actress Susan Sarandon in 1967, and they remained together until their divorce in 1979.

The couple met while attending The Catholic University of America.

Chris and Susan Sarandon have one daughter, Eva Amurri, who is also an actress.

He married his second wife, Lisa Ann Cooper, in 1980, and they have three children together.

Sarandon's third marriage is to actress Joanna Gleason, whom he wed in 1994.

Awards and Recognition

Throughout his career, Chris Sarandon has received numerous accolades for his work. Here are some highlights of his achievements.

He received an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor for his role in "Dog Day Afternoon."

Sarandon won a Saturn Award for Best Actor for his performance in "Fright Night."

He was nominated for a Daytime Emmy Award for his role in "The Guiding Light."

Sarandon received a Drama Desk Award nomination for his performance in the play "Nickel and Dimed."

He was honored with a Lifetime Achievement Award at the 2016 Rhode Island International Film Festival.
