Srinivasan Doraiswamy "Srinivas" Bio

Srinivasan Doraiswamy, commonly known as Srinivas, is a renowned Indian playback singer who has carved a niche for himself in the music industry. Born on June 14, 1969, in Thiruvannamalai, Tamil Nadu, Srinivas developed a deep passion for music from a very young age. With his mellifluous voice and versatility, he has won the hearts of millions of music lovers across the globe. Srinivas started his musical journey by participating in various college competitions and local music shows during his school days. His talent soon caught the attention of acclaimed composer A. R. Rahman, who gave him his first break in the Tamil film "Minsara Kanavu" (1997). The melodious duet "Vennilavae Vennilavae" sung by Srinivas and singer Hariharan became an instant hit, catapulting Srinivas to fame. This marked the beginning of his extraordinary career in playback singing. Over the years, Srinivas has worked with several prominent music directors in the Indian film industry, including Ilaiyaraaja, Vishal-Shekhar, and Yuvan Shankar Raja. He has lent his voice to a wide range of genres, including romantic ballads, peppy numbers, and soulful tracks, showcasing his versatility and impeccable vocal prowess. Srinivas has sung in various languages such as Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, and Hindi, establishing himself as a pan-Indian singer. Srinivas' mesmerizing voice has earned him numerous accolades and recognition throughout his career . He has been honored with several prestigious awards, including multiple Filmfare Awards, Tamil Nadu State Film Awards, and National Film Awards. With his rich discipline, dedication, and passion for music, Srinivas continues to enthrall music lovers with his soulful renderings and remains an inspiration to aspiring singers in India and beyond.
