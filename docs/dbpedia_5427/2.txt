Timothy Dalton Bio

Timothy Dalton is an accomplished actor born on March 21, 1946, in Colwyn Bay, Denbighshire, Wales. He grew up in Manchester, England, where his father was a hotel manager. Dalton was educated at a boarding school in Bury where he developed an interest in acting, and later studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in London. He began his acting career in theatre before transitioning to film and television. Dalton made his film debut in 1971 in the movie "The Lion in Winter," playing King Philip II alongside Peter O'Toole and Katherine Hepburn. He went on to appear in several other films including "The Spy Who Loved Me," "Flash Gordon," and "Brenda Starr." However, his most famous role was that of James Bond, which he played in two films, "The Living Daylights" and "License to Kill." Despite his success as James Bond, Dalton continued to showcase his versatility as an actor by appearing in a range of roles in other films and television shows. He played a villain in the movie "Hot Fuzz," a police officer in the television series "Chuck," and the voice of Mr. Pricklepants in "Toy Story 3." Dalton has won critical acclaim for his performances in stage productions, including a Tony Award for his work in "The Glass Menagerie." In addition to his acting career , Dalton is also involved in charity work. He supports several organizations, including the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children and the Royal National Lifeboat Institution. Dalton is admired for his talent, versatility , and philanthropic endeavors, and continues to be a respected figure in the entertainment industry.
