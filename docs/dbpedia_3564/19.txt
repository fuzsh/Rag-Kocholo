Name: Sant’Antonio di Gallura

Province: Olbia-Tempio

Population: Approximately 2,000 residents

Elevation: 553 meters above sea level

Region: Sardinia

Closest Town and Distance: Olbia, approximately 30 kilometers away

Sant’Antonio di Gallura is a charming hilltop village nestled in the Olbia-Tempio province of Sardinia, Italy. With its population of around 2,000 residents, it offers visitors a glimpse into traditional Sardinian life against a stunning mountain backdrop.

Historical Sites: The village is home to the beautiful Parish Church of Sant’Antonio Abate, a Romanesque-style church dating back to the 12th century. Visitors can admire its intricate architecture and religious artworks.

Cuisine: Sant’Antonio di Gallura is known for its delicious Sardinian cuisine, featuring local specialties such as porceddu (roast suckling pig), pane carasau (crispy flatbread), and mirto liqueur made from myrtle berries. Visitors can savor these authentic dishes in the village’s cozy trattorias.

Nature Trails: Surrounding the village are scenic hiking trails that lead through pristine countryside and offer panoramic views of the Gallura region. Nature lovers can explore the diverse flora and fauna of Sardinia while enjoying the peace and serenity of the outdoors.

Festivals: Throughout the year, Sant’Antonio di Gallura hosts a variety of traditional festivals and events that celebrate the local culture and traditions. One of the highlights is the Feast of Sant’Antonio Abate, a religious procession followed by lively festivities in the village square.

Craftsmanship: The village is known for its skilled artisans who produce traditional Sardinian handicrafts such as intricate filigree jewelry, handwoven textiles, and ceramic pottery. Visitors can browse the local shops and galleries to find unique souvenirs to take home.

Sant’Antonio di Gallura offers a peaceful retreat in the heart of Sardinia, where visitors can immerse themselves in the island’s rich history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether exploring historic sites, savoring local flavors, or wandering through scenic landscapes, the village provides a delightful escape from the hustle and bustle of modern life.
