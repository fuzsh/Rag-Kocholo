Marty Feldman was an English actor and comedian.

Biography[]

Born in London, England, Feldman was a fairground worker and musician before becoming a comedian with Morris, Marty and Mitch who made their television debut in 1955. He was a comedy writer on a number of shows, including The Frost Report and At Last the 1948 Show, for which he helped cowrite "The Four Yorkshiremen Sketch."

Feldman had a number of film roles, including Young Frankenstein, The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes' Smarter Brother and the self directed The Last Remake of Beau Geste.

He passed away in 1982.

Singing[]

Feldman sang in two of his classic comedies, teasing Dr. Frankenstein with "I Ain't Got Nobody" in Young Frankenstein and joining "The Kangaroo Hop" in The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes' Smarter Brother.
