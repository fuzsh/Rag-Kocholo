Sheila Sim was on the way to becoming one of Britain’s leading actresses when she gave it up in return for a permanent role as wife to Richard Attenborough, mother to their three children and partner in a plethora of altruistic schemes.

The couple had met at Rada during the war and married in 1945. At that point Sim was more famous than “Dickie” after her acclaimed performance opposite Eric Portman in the cult 1944 Powell and Pressburger film A Canterbury Tale. She went on to appear with Attenborough in the original West End production of The Mousetrap in 1952. After the birth of her third child, Charlotte, in 1959, she never acted again.
