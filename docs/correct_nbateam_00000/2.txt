MEMPHIS, Tenn. (WMC) - NBA champion and Memphis Grizzlies legend Marc Gasol announced his retirement from basketball after 20 professional seasons, including 13 in the NBA.

Gasol spent the first 11 seasons of his NBA career with the Memphis Grizzlies before being traded to the Toronto Raptors in the 2018-19 season where he won his first NBA championship.

Gasol finished his NBA career playing one season for the Los Angeles Lakers in 2020-2021.

Gasol spent the rest of his career in Spain playing for Basquet Girona, a basketball club he has owned since 2014.

The big man averaged 14 points, 7.4 rebounds, and 3.4 assists per game in his career. Gasol was a three-time NBA all-star, two-time all-NBA player, and a Defensive Player of the Year winner, the first in Memphis Grizzlies history.

The Memphis Grizzlies acquired Marc Gasol from the Los Angeles Lakers in 2008 for his brother Pau Gasol.

Since the trade, Gasol put together an outstanding career in Memphis, leading the Grizzlies to their first Conference Finals appearance in franchise history in 2013, and holding multiple franchise record including blocked shots, rebounds, and field goals made.
