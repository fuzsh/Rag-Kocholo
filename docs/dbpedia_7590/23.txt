LaGrange, GA, USA

Latitude and longitude coordinates are: 33.037880, -85.030060.

LaGrange is a city and the seat of Troup County, located 47 miles north of Columbus and 67 miles southwest of Atlanta, in northwestern Georgia, United States. It is the main city of the LaGrange, Georgia Micropolitan Statistical Area. The city is home to LaGrange College which was founded in 1831. It is the oldest private college in Georgia. There are several buildings in the city that are listed on the National Register of Historic Places including The Troup County Courthouse, Annex, and Jail. These three buildings were built in 1939. Other points of interest in LaGrange are Hills & Dales Estate, Callaway Memorial Tower, Biblical History Center, Lafayette Fountain, Yellowjacket Creek Recreation Area, Sunny Point Recreation Area, Yellow Jacket Beach, Bisham Manor, Hogg Mine, and so on. LaGrange-Callaway Airport, Interstate 85, and Interstate 185 serve the area. The city is also home to West Georgia Technical College and Point University.
