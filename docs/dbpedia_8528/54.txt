Craig Oliver (born 15 May 1969) was Downing Street Director of Communications from 28 February 2011 to 13 July 2016, succeeding Andy Coulson and preceding Katie Perrior. Born in Basford, Northamptonshire, he was raised in Scotland before becoming a reporter for STV in 1992 and eventually making his way to the BBC in 2006. He served as Director of Politics and Communications for Prime Minister David Cameron from 2011 to 2016 (during which time he served as the main political strategist for the anti-Brexit "Britain Stronger in Europe" campaign), after which he was knighted and became a strategic consultant.
