Jill Goldsmith is a lawyer who became a writer and producer for television, with credits on Emmy-winning series NYPD Blue, The Practice, Ally McBeal, Law & Order, and Boston Legal. She was a 2008 finalist for the Humanitas Prize. She has also been a featured speaker at numerous law schools, conferences, and writing programs. She has appeared as a panelist on C-Span Close Up, CBS News, and CNN. She received her J.D. from Washington University School of Law. She also served an internship with U.S. Senator Paul Simon on the Senate Judiciary Committee. After her internship and prior to writing for television, Jill spent seven years as a Public Defender in the Juvenile and Felony Trial Divisions of the Cook County Public Defender's Office in Chicago, Illinois. She is also a contributing author to the book, Lawyers in Your Living Room! Law on Television, published in 2009. She wrote the chapter entitled, Writing for Television: From Courtroom to Writer's Room.
