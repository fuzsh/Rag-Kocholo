M. G. Ramachandran was a highly influential actor and filmmaker in Tamil cinema. He played a key role in popularizing the "masala film" genre and brought a unique style of action and melodrama to the industry.

M. G. Ramachandran leveraged his immense popularity as an actor to enter politics. He founded the political party AIADMK and went on to become the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu, where he implemented various welfare schemes for the people.

M. G. Ramachandran's leadership in Tamil Nadu was marked by his strong focus on social welfare and populism. He implemented several schemes aimed at uplifting the marginalized sections of society and remains a revered figure in the state's political history.

M. G. Ramachandran had a profound impact on Tamil Nadu's cultural and social landscape. He used his films and political platform to address societal issues and promote Tamil pride, leading to a significant shift in the region's identity and consciousness.

M. G. Ramachandran was instrumental in establishing several healthcare initiatives in Tamil Nadu, including the popularization of the "Amma Unavagam" (Mother's canteen) scheme that provided subsidized meals to the poor. His efforts significantly improved healthcare access for the underprivileged.

Ramachandran was known for his unique style of wearing dark sunglasses, often indoors as well as outdoors, which became his signature look.

He was a talented actor and starred in several films in his career, earning him the title of "Puratchi Thalaivar" (Revolutionary Leader) among his fans.

Ramachandran was a skilled playback singer and lent his voice to several popular songs in his films, showcasing his versatility in the entertainment industry.

He was a philanthropist at heart and founded numerous charitable organizations aimed at helping the underprivileged and promoting education and healthcare in society.
