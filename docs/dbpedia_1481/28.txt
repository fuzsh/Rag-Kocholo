Sylvester Gilbert, a Representative from Connecticut; born in Hebron, Tolland County, Conn., October 20, 1755; pursued classical studies, and was graduated from Dartmouth College, Hanover, N.H., in 1775; studied law; was admitted to the bar in November 1777 and commenced practice in Hebron; member of the State house of representatives 1780-1812; State's attorney for Tolland County 1786-1807; chief judge of the county court and judge of the probate court 1807-1818; principal of a law school 1810-1818; member of the State senate in 1815 and 1816; elected as a Republican to the Fifteenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Uriel Holmes and served from November 16, 1818, to March 3, 1819; resumed the practice of law in Hebron; again judge of the county court 1820-1825; again a member of the State house of representatives in 1826; died in Hebron, Conn., January 2, 1846; interment in Old Cemetery. [1]

U.S. Congressman. He graduated from Dartmouth College New Hampshire, in 1775, studied law, was admitted to the bar in November 1777 and commenced the practice of law in Hebron, Connecticut. He was a member of the Connecticut State House of Representatives, (1780-1812) Connecticut State attorney for Tolland County, (1786-1807), a member of the Connecticut State Senate, (1815-16) and chief judge of the county court (1807-18). In 1818, he was elected as a Republican to the Fifteenth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Uriel Holmes, serving until 1819. After leaving Congress, he resumed the practice of law, was judge of Tolland County, (1820-25) and again a member of the Connecticut State House of Representatives, in 1826. [2]
