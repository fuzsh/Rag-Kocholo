Billy Joel, born in 1949 in New York, is an iconic American singer-songwriter and pianist. He gained fame in the 1970s and 1980s with hits like 'Piano Man,' 'Uptown Girl,' and 'We Didn't Start the Fire.' His contributions to music have earned him multiple Grammy Awards and an induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

Joel's lyrics often delve into the complexities of relationships, exploring the ineffable nature of love, admiration for partners, and the mystery of attraction. In recent work, he continues to address themes like fear of emotional intimacy and the journey of rebuilding trust, resonating with audiences through his heartfelt and relatable storytelling.
