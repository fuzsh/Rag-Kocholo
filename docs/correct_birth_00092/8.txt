Biography

Highly successful singer-songwriter who is one of the best-selling artists in music history

Billy Joel was featured in Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, 1999.

William Martin Joel who is better known as Billy Joel was born on 9 February 1949 in Bronx, New York City, New York, United States.[1] He is the child of Helmuth Julius Joel and Rosalind Nyman. In the 1950 census, he is listed as the son of Howard Joel in New York, Bronx, New York, United States.[2] He and Christie Brinkley were married and they are the parents of Alexa Joel.

A globally-recognized pianist, singer, and songwriter, Billy is a 6-time Grammy award winner and one of the best-selling artists in music history. Billy's nickname is "The Piano Man" which is his signature song. In 1977, Billy released his album entitled "The Stranger." Over 10 million copies of the album were sold and it contains multiple hit singles, including "Just the Way You Are", "Movin' Out (Anthony's Song)", "Only the Good Die Young", "She's Always a Woman", and "Scenes from an Italian Restaurant."

Billy continues to tour and he regularly performs at Madison Square Garden in New York City.
