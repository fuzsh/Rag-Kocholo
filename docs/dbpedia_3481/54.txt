FM Tango

FM Tango was a radio station that began operation in Buenos Aires in 1990.

Beginning in 1991 they persuaded all of the major record companies to produce a series of compact discs. Now, and it's not often you can say this, the track selections on the FM Tango discs were really excellent.

The design of the covers was also truly excellent - far better than anything the Argentinians have managed since. Someone had the great idea of using the caricatures of Hermengildo Sabat.

With good tracks and great design the discs could only succeed and the rest, as they say, is history. But it really is history. In 1997 the city government took away the radio station's license and the station was closed down.

Most of these discs are now deleted. Today they are collectors items. However some of the very best ones are still available, and, of the others, most have equally worthwhile replacements available today.

Some of the more popular ones have resurfaced in a number of guises: first of all by the Argentine record store Musimundo under their own label, and also by BMG, first under their RCA Club and later Solo Tango labels. Of course, BMG have only re-issued their own discs and even then only a partial selection. Consequently this great collection is already fading into the memory.

Help us re-construct the catalogue

So far, with the help of other collectors, I've found 71 albums across four labels, but I may be missing more. I'm still lacking track listings and cover artwork for some of the discs, whilst for others I have only very small scans of the covers taken from websites. If you can, please help us complete the catalogue.
