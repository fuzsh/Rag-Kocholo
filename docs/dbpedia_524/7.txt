Needing a new exhibition layout, Wirral and Wigan Finescale Railway Modellers purchased a South Coast set ‘OO’ gauge project. TIM ROGERS explains how they upgraded and improved it to go on tour. Photography, Mike Wild.

Scale: 4mm:1ft | Gauge: 'OO', 16.5mm | Size: 17ft x 17ft | Period: 1968-1974 | Control: Analogue

Above: The station is busy as a 2-HAL unit departs from the bay platform following arrival of a 4-VEP+2-EPB combination. In the reception sidings a Class 08 is preparing to shunt a new cut of coal wagons.

RATHER THAN being an exact model, Newhaven Harbour is based on the infrastructure of the Newhaven and Seaford Branch in East Sussex and is intended to provide a flavour of the general location.

It reflects operations on the BR Southern Region between 1968 and 1974 at which time, Newhaven was served by 27 miles of track and three stations - Harbour, Marine and Town. Passenger traffic was handled by a variety of Electric Multiple Units (EMUs) – 2-HAL, 2-BIL, 4-VEP, 4-CEP were common while it even saw 5-BEL ‘Brighton Belle’ Pullman sets on special workings.
