Jo Van Fleet, the Academy Award-winning actress who made a career playing mothers on stage and in such films as "East of Eden" and "Cool Hand Luke," died Monday in Jamaica Hospital.

While she was said to be 81 at the time of her death, some reference books gave her age as 76.

Van Fleet was a close friend of the Studio Arena Theatre's founding artistic director, Neal Du Brock. She paid a number of visits to Buffalo and in 1970 starred in Paul Zindel's "The Effect of Gamma Rays on Man-in-the-Moon Marigolds." The following year, she starred in Neil Simon's "The Gingerbread Lady" for the Studio Arena.

In her first screen appearance, Van Fleet won the 1955 Oscar for best supporting actress as the mother of James Dean's character in "East of Eden." Two years later, she won a Tony as best supporting actress for her role as Jessie Mae Watts in Horton Foote's "Trip to Bountiful."

She also played the mother of Susan Hayward's character in the film "I'll Cry Tomorrow" (1955), Paul Newman's character in the film "Cool Hand Luke" (1967) and Anthony Perkins' character in "Look Homeward, Angel" on Broadway.
