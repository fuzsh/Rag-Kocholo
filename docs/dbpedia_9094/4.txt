Curiel de Duero

Curiel de Duero is a municipality of Spain, in the province of Valladolid, autonomous community of Castile and Leon. It has an area of ​​18,75 km ² with a population of 134 inhabitants and a densidad of 7,15 hab / km ². It belongs to the Campo de Peñafiel region. Together with the municipalities of San Llorente, Corrales de Duero, Valdearcos de la Vega, and Bocos de Duero form a grouping of villages called Valle del Cuco. Routes limited to the north of Curiel, historically belonged to the Community of town and land of Curiel, but is not included in the Cuco Valley. Curiel was the head of the Community of Villa and Land of Curiel within the territory called Extremadura Castellana. It has also been known as Curiel de los Ajos for the quality of its garlic. See Curiel village and land community. The economy is based on the cultivation of rainfed cereal, vineyards and sheep farming. It has several wineries of the Ribera del Duero Denomination.
