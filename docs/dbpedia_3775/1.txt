Robert Black Robert Black, of Bedminster Twp., died on Saturday, February 20, 2010 in Grand View Hospital, West Rockhill Twp. He was 84. Born in Philadelphia, he was the son of the late Robert P. and Elizabeth (Cochrane) Black. A U.S. Marine veteran of WWII, he served in Guam and the South Pacific. He was employed as a pressman for the Philadelphia Inquirer at the age of 17, then following his years of military service returned to work there until his retirement. Mr. Black operated Sweetbriar Stables in Bedminster where he bred, raised and ran thoroughbred horses in the area. He was a member of the Pressman’s Union in Philadelphia and a lifetime member of the Philadelphia VFW Post # 538. He is survived by a sister, Doris B. Reith of Bedminster, nieces, nephews, great nieces and nephews and one great great nephew. In addition to his parents, he was preceded in death by a sister, Gladys C. Black. Memorial Services will be held on Saturday, February 27, 2010 at 11 AM in the Irish Meetinghouse at Deep Run Presbyterian Church, 16 Irish Meetinghouse Road, Perkasie. Interment will be private at the convenience of the family in Forest Hills Memorial Park, Philadelphia. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made in his memory to the charity of one’s choosing. Arrangements are by the Bernard Suess Funeral Home, Perkasie. www.suessfuneralhome.net
