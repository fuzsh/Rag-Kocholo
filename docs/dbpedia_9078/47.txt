Johnta Austin is an American rapper and songwriter, born on June 28, 1980, in Atlanta, Georgia. He rose to fame in 2012 with the release of his EP titled Love. As a songwriter, the Georgia singer-songwriter became known for the 1998 Tyrese's hit Sweet Lady. But he became widely recognized after winning Grammy awards for the songs, We Belong Together and Be Without You by Mariah Carey and Mary J. Blige, respectively, in 2005 and 2006.

He has collaborated with producers such as Bryan-Michael Cox and Jermaine Dupri and singers such as Janet Jackson, Toni Braxton, Jessica Simpson, and Chris Brown. In 2019, 25 years into his career, he independently issued his first solo album titled Love, Sex, & Religion with involvement from two of his longtime studio partners, Troy Taylor and Bryan Michael-Cox.
