LOS ANGELES, Dec. 26 (AP) —Robert Adrian Scott, a film writer‐producer who served a year in prison for refusing to testify before the House Un American Activities Committee, died yesterday at his home in suburban Sherman Oaks. He was 61 years old and recently had undergone surgery for cancer.

Mr. Scott, who was born in Arlington, N. J., and was grad uated from Amherst College, came to California in 1939. His film credits included “Murder, My Sweet,” “The Boy With the Green Hair” and “Crossfire.”

On his release from prison in 1951, he became executive producer for a London film studio. He returned to this country in 1968, to write tele vision scripts and produce films for Universal‐International Studios.

After being divorced twice, Mr. Scott in 1955 married Joan LaCour, a film and television writer, who survives him.
