Slint is an American rock band formed in Louisville, Kentucky, in 1986. Known for their influential role in the post-rock and math rock genres, they gained critical acclaim with their 1991 album 'Spiderland'. Their music is often characterized by complex song structures and dynamic changes in tempo and volume.

Slint's lyrics frequently explore themes of emotional farewells, cherished love memories, and inner strength resilience. Their evocative storytelling and introspective approach have resonated deeply with listeners, making their work timeless despite their relatively brief active period.
