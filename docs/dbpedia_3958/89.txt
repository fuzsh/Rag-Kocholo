Fordwich is a small town located in the county of Kent, England. It is situated on the River Stour and is known for being the smallest town in the country. Despite its size, Fordwich has a rich history and was once an important port for the transportation of goods. Today, the town is a popular tourist destination and is home to a number of historic buildings and landmarks.

One of the most notable landmarks in Fordwich is the Church of St. Mary the Virgin. This beautiful church dates back to the 12th century and is known for its stunning architecture and intricate stained glass windows. Visitors can also explore the nearby Fordwich Town Hall, which was built in the 16th century and is now used as a community center. The town is also home to a number of quaint shops and cafes, making it the perfect place to spend a lazy afternoon.

In addition to its historic landmarks, Fordwich is also known for its natural beauty. The town is surrounded by lush green countryside and is a popular destination for walkers and hikers.
