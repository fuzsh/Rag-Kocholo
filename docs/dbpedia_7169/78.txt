Pierre-Gilles de Gennes was a French physicist born in Paris on October 24, 1932, and he passed away in Orsay on May 18, 2007. He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1991 for his discovery that methods used to study ordering phenomena in simple systems could be generalized to more complex forms of matter, particularly in liquid crystals and polymers.
