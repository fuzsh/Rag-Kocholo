Vice Adm.William V. Davis Jr. of the Navy, retired, a pioneer Navy aviator, died Saturday of cancer at the University of South Alabama Hospital in Mobile.

Admiral Davis, who was a resident of Fairhope, Ala., was 79 years old.

He was born in Savannah, Ga., and was graduated from the Naval Academy in 1924. After duty on the battleship Idaho, he entered flight training.

He was thd navigator aboard the Woolaroc, a monoplane that won a $25,000 prize in a 1927 race from Oakland, Calif., to Honolulu. The flight won him the Distinguished Flying Cross.

Among his commands were the U.S.S. Tulagi, at Okinawa in World War II, and, after the war, the carrier Franklin D. Roosevelt. In wartime, he was also a deputy chief of staff in the Pacific, and chief of staff, headquarters, Strategic Air Force, Pacific.

In 1958, he became deputy commander in chief of the Atlantic Fleet, a post he held until retiring in 1960. Later he became a naval military adviser for Lockheed Aircraft Corporation. He was also executive director of the U.S.S. Alabama Battleship Commission.

In 1949, the National Air Council recognized Admiral Davis's work in the development of helicopters and in high-altitude jet flight. He is survived by his wife, Margaret, of Fairhope, Ala.; a son, William V. David 3d of Atlanta; three daughters, Judith Kuncas of Export, Pa.; Margaret Martin of Mobile, Ala.; and Winifred B. Davis, of Savannah, and 11 grandchildren.

The funeral service is to be held today at the Fort Barancas Naval Air Station, Pensacola, Fla.
