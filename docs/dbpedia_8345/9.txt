Historic U.S. Customhouse in New Bedford, Massachusetts. The Greek Revival style building was constructed between 1834 and 1836.

Listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1970 (NRHP No. 70000735). It was designated a National Historic Landmark on December 30, 1970. The National Park Service Statement of Significance stated:

“Constructed in 1834-36, this oblong granite building, with a hipped roof, is a powerful example of a small public building in the Greek Revival style. It is symbolic of the era when New Bedford was a major port.”

The custom house became part of the New Bedford Whaling National Historical Park in 1996.

Historic American Buildings Survey (HABS No. MA-682 as the Custom House).
