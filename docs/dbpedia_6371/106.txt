The Bangalore Metro, locally known as Namma Metro, is a rapid transit system serving the city of Bangalore, India. With an operational length of 73.75 kilometers, it stands as the second-longest operational metro network in India.

The network currently consists of two phases, with a third phase under construction. Phase 1 is spread across 42.3 km and consists of 51 stations. Phase 2 is expected to add 72 km and 61 stations to the network.

The Bangalore Metro is a popular mode of transportation for commuters and tourists alike, with over 5 lakh people using it daily. The trains run at a frequency of 5–15 minutes and reach speeds of just under 80 km/h.

The trains, each consisting of 6 coaches, traverse the system both at ground level and through elevated and underground sections. This makes the Bangalore Metro a unique and versatile transportation system.

Bangalore Metro/Namma Metro Lines

The Bangalore Metro, also known as Namma Metro, is currently operating with two route lines: Purple and Green. Four more route lines are under construction and will be operational in the coming months.

Purple Line

The Purple Line was the first operational Namma Metro line. Initiated as part of Phase 1 of the Bangalore route, it encompasses 37 stations and 4 interchanges. The Purple Line started with the first underground metro station in Bangalore.

Green Line

The Green Line is the second operational line on Bangalore Metro routes. It runs on 29 stations with a length of 31 km.

Yellow Line

The upcoming Yellow Line will link Rashtreeya Vidyalaya Road to Bommasandra and will cover a distance of over 18 km. It is scheduled to operate on 16 stations and will soon establish a connection to Hosur.

Blue Line

The Blue Line will be the first Bangalore airport metro linking Kasturi Nagar to the airport. This line will cover a distance of 58.19 km with 30 stations.

Pink Line

The Pink Line will be connecting Nagawara to Gottigere, which is in the south of Namma Bangalore. It is planned to feature 18 stations, including some that will be situated underground.

Orange Line

The Orange Line will be developed in Phase 3 of the Bangalore metro line map. It will connect two lines – Phase 2A and Phase 3A at a single station. This new line in Bangalore is expected to be functional by 2028-29.

Bangalore Metro App: Purchase QR Tickets, Find Stations, and Book Auto/Taxi

The Namma Metro app allows you to purchase QR tickets, find stations, and book auto/taxi services. It is currently available for Android users only.

Find Bangalore Metro stations and get directions

Book auto/taxi services to and from Bangalore Metro stations

Benefits:

Easy to find Bangalore Metro stations and get directions

How to download the Namma Metro app:

Access the Google Play Store on your Android device.

Search for “Namma Metro” and tap the first result.

Tap the “Install” button.

After installing the app, click on the “Open” button to initiate its launch.

How to purchase QR tickets using the Namma Metro app:

Open the Namma Metro app and tap the “Buy Ticket” button.

Select the stations you want to travel between.

Choose the quantity of tickets you wish to buy.

Click the “Pay Now” button and choose your desired payment method.

Once your payment is processed, you will receive a QR ticket on your screen.

How to find Bangalore Metro stations and get directions using the Namma Metro app:

Open the Namma Metro app and tap the “Stations” button.

Tap the “Find Stations” button.

Enter the name of the station you are looking for or tap on the map to find stations near you.

Tap on a station to get directions.

How to book auto/taxi services using the Namma Metro app:

Open the Namma Metro app and tap the “Auto/Taxi” button.

Enter your pick-up and drop-off locations.

Select the type of vehicle you want to book.

Tap the “Book Now” button and confirm your booking.

Bangalore Metro Project Details

The Namma Metro project is currently in an expansion phase and is being undertaken by the Bangalore Metro Rail Corporation Limited (BMRCL). While Phase 1 of the Bengaluru Metro and certain segments of Phase 2 are already operational, the remaining portions are expected to be completed by 2025.

The Karnataka Government has approved the construction of Phase 3, which will cover 45 km. The estimated approved cost for the project is ₹16,000 crore, and it is expected to be completed by 2028.

Here are some additional details about the Bangalore Metro project:

The Namma Metro network currently has 73.75 km of operational lines and 51 stations.

Phase 2 of the project will add 72 km and 61 stations to the network.

Phase 3 of the project will add 45 km and 47 stations to the network.

Once all three phases of the project are completed, the Namma Metro network will have 190.75 km of lines and 167 stations.

The Namma Metro project is a major infrastructure project that is transforming the city of Bangalore. It is providing a convenient and affordable mode of transportation for commuters, and it is also helping to reduce congestion and pollution in the city.

Funding for Bangalore Metro

The Bangalore Metro is funded by a combination of sources, including:

State and central government grants: The Government of Karnataka and the Government of India provide grants to the Bangalore Metro Rail Corporation Limited (BMRCL), the company that operates the metro.

Loans from multilateral and commercial lenders: The BMRCL has taken loans from multilateral lenders such as the Asian Development Bank and the Japan International Cooperation Agency, as well as from commercial lenders such as REC Limited.

Revenue from operations: The BMRCL generates revenue from fares, advertising, and other sources.

Bangalore Metro Revenue

The Bangalore Metro achieved profitability for the first time in the fiscal year 2022-23. It earned an estimated Rs 594.02 crore in revenue, with maintenance expenditure accounting for Rs 486.61 crore. This resulted in a profit of Rs 40 crore.

The BMRCL is aiming to close at a profit of Rs 100 crore in the fiscal year 2023-24.

FAQ:

Q1. What are the Bangalore Metro timings?

Ans –The Bangalore Metro train timings are 5 AM to 11 PM on Monday to Friday. On Sundays, the timings are from 6 AM to 11 PM.

Q2. Is Metro operational in Bangalore?

Ans – Yes, Metro is operational in Bangalore with two lines (Green and Purple) and 51 stations.

Q3. Is it safe to travel by metro in Bangalore?

Ans – Yes, it is safe to travel by Metro in Bangalore as there are multiple security checks and assistance personnel at every station.

Q4. Is the Green Line Metro operational in Bangalore?

Ans – Yes, the Green Line Metro is operational in Bangalore with 29 stations from Nagasandra to Silk Institute.

Q5. Is the Purple Line metro running today in Bangalore?

Ans – Yes, the Purple Line metro is running in Bangalore on 37 stations connecting Whitefield to Challaghatta.

Q6. What time does Bangalore Metro start on Sunday?

Ans – On Sunday, the metro starting time in Bangalore is 6 AM and it runs till 11 PM.

Q7. Is Metro Card compulsory in Bangalore?

Ans – Metro card is not compulsory in Bangalore Metro as passengers can buy QR tickets through the Namma Metro app and can be tapped at the QR-enabled Automatic Fare Collection gates.

Q8. What is not allowed in Bangalore Metro?

Ans – Among the items that are prohibited in the metro include sharp objects, guns and firearms, tools, explosive materials, flammable items, disabling chemicals and pets.

Q9. Can we carry alcohol in Bangalore Metro?

Ans – No, alcohol is not allowed in Bangalore Metro.

Q10. Does Bangalore Metro accept cash?

Ans – Yes, passengers can top up and purchase cards or tickets through cash. UPI and cards are also accepted.

Q11. How can I travel in Bangalore Metro?

Ans – Passengers have to board a train from the station after going through multiple checkpoints. A ticket or metro card is required for travelling that you purchase from the station or through the Namma Metro app for the destination.

Q12. How much is a 30-day metro pass in Bangalore?

Ans – There is only a 2-day and 3-day pass available at the time in Bangalore metro. The 2-day pass costs Rs.200 while the 3-day pass costs Rs.400.
