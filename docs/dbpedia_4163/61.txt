Adrian Paunescu was Romania’s highest-profile intellectual for the past 30 years. After a promising literary debut in the 1960s he served as the court poet and chief propagandist for the communist ruler Nicolae Ceausescu.

Paunescu’s chief claim to fame was his role in exposing thousands of young Romanians to the Ceausescu personality cult between 1973 and 1985. Later his talents in public relations were exploited by those in business and politics who were shaping the transition from communism to democracy around their agenda of limited change.
