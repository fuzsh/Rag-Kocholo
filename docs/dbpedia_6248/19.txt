Leicester is a city in the East Midlands of England, and is the county town for Leicestershire. The city lies on the River Soar. The population of Leicester is about 330,000, and about 500,000 people lived in the wider Leicester urban area, making Leicester the tenth largest city in the United Kingdom.

Leicestershire is a major commercial and manufacturing centre, providing jobs for 437,000 people.

Leicester is located on the Midland Main Line and close to the M1 motorway. The city has a large ethnic minority population, particularly of South Asian origin, a product of immigration to the United Kingdom since the Second World War. There are many Hindu, Sikh and Muslim places of worship and the Melton Road district serves as a focus, containing large numbers of Asian restaurants and other small businesses.

Leicester is a centre for higher education, with both the University of Leicester and De Montfort University being based in the city.
