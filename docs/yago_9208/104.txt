Directed by Emilio Fernández and shot by the Mexican Golden Age's most important Canadian, Alex Phillips, The Net is deceptively straightforward. Presented as just another love triangle, it is in fact a fascinatingly strange film, one which eschews dialogue in favor of faces, bodies, and impulses. The story is undeniably simple, but its telling is spectacular, building to a fevered, melodramatic climax with hardly any conversation at all.
