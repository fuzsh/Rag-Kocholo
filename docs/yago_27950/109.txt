Robin Williams improvised so much of his performance on Mrs Doubtfire that the production team shot two million feet of film, the director has said.

The late comedian played a recently divorced father who poses as an elderly housekeeper to stay close to his children in the 1993 film. It remains one of his best-known roles.

Speaking on the 30th anniversary of the film, Chris Columbus, 65, the director, told Business Insider: “Early on in the process, he went to me, ‘Hey boss, the way I like to work, if you’re up for it, is I’ll give you three or four scripted takes, and then let’s play.’
