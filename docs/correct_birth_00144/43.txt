Nelly Furtado, a Canadian singer-songwriter, was born on December 2, 1978, in Victoria, British Columbia. She gained international fame with her debut album 'Whoa, Nelly!' in 2000, which included the hit single 'I'm Like a Bird'. Furtado's diverse musical style blends pop, folk, and Latin influences, making her a versatile and influential artist in the music industry.

Furtado's lyrics often explore profound emotional and social themes. She delves into the pain and growth associated with the end of relationships, the complexities of cultural identity, and the journey of personal growth through suffering. Additionally, her work frequently addresses the tension between individual authenticity and societal expectations, as well as the spiritual aspects of love and surrender.
