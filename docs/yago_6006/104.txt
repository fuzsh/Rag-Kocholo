Dani Levy

Dani Levy is a Swiss director, screenwriter, and actor, born on November 17, 1957, in Basel, Switzerland. Renowned for his unique style and diverse body of work, he has established himself as one of the leading figures in German cinema.

Levy began his career in theater, initially working as an actor and director. However, he shifted his focus to filmmaking in the 1980s, directing short films and later feature films.

A breakthrough in his career came with the film "Robinson's Garden" (1997), which garnered international success and established Levy as a talented director. His work often blends comedy and drama, frequently exploring themes such as identity, family, and Jewish life in Germany.

Some of his notable films include "Alles auf Zucker!" (2004), which received numerous awards including the German Film Award for Best Film, and "Die Welt der Wunderlichs" (2016).

In addition to his film work, Dani Levy is also active as a theater director and writer. Through his creative endeavors and his commitment to social and cultural issues, he has emerged as a significant voice in the German film industry.
