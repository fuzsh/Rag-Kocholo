Mike Bryan Biography

Mike Bryan was born and raised in sunny California and played his collegiate tennis at Stanford University in 1997 and 1998. During his time at Stanford he led the Cardinals to back-to-back NCAA Team Championships and won the NCAA doubles title with his twin brother, Bob Bryan. After the 1998 season he and his brother turned pro and became one of the top doubles tandems in the world. Since turning pro they have won 100 doubles titles, including two French Open championships in 2003 and 2013, three Wimbledon titles in 2006, 2011, and 2013, five U.S. Open titles in 2005, 2008, 2010, 2012 and 2014 and six Australian Open championships in 2006, 2007, 2009, 2010, 2011 and 2013. Their other notable accomplishments include winning the Tour Finals in 2003, 2004, 2009 and 2014, winning a gold medal in the 2012 London Olympic Games and a bronze medal at the 2008 Beijing Olympics. He has also captured the 2003 French Open, 2012 Wimbledon, and 2002 US Open titles in mixed doubles and an Olympic bronze medal in mixed doubles in 2012. To date, Bryan has won over $12 million in prize money while competing with his twin brother on the doubles circuit, and been ranked as the No. 1 tandem in the world since 2003.
