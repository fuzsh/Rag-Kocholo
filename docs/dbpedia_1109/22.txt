NYE IN THE CITY

New Year's Eve sparkles in the CBD this year, with Fed Square having organised a multicultural, family-friendly and alcohol-free celebration to welcome 2015. The entertainment schedule includes African beats by Lamine Sonko and the African Intelligence, acrobatics from the Ethiopian Circus Trio and a great view of the midnight fireworks. UV lights will also be installed, so wear white if you'd like to glow.

Wednesday, 8pm, Federation Square, Flinders Street, city, free, 9655 1900, fedsquare.com

MUSICAL

Based on a hit indie Irish film, Once is a musical set in Dublin all about love and music. Featuring music that won a slew of awards, including Academy, Grammy and Tony awards, the musical follows an Irish busker who meets a Czech immigrant and learns to love again. Starring Tom Parsons and Madeleine Jones, and featuring the hit song Falling Slowly, the production runs into the New Year.

Today, 2pm and 8pm, tomorrow, 1pm, Princess Theatre, 163 Spring Street, city, $90-$140, 136 100, ticketmaster.com.au

TALK

If the walls of Heide could talk, they'd tell tales of the intellectuals, artists and bohemians who have gathered at the "gallery to live in" over its 80-year existence. Today's Heide Past and Present history talk sheds light on the museum's fascinating metamorphosis from private property of John and Sunday Reed to public, contemporary art gallery.

Today, 2pm, Heide Museum of Modern Art, 7 Templestowe Road, Bulleen, $12-$16, 9850 1500, heide.com.au
