Raymond Felton Biography

Raymond Felton began his career in basketball in high school where he led the team to two state championships with a career record of 104-9. As a standout star, Felton went onto play in college where he was voted Carolina Player of the Year in his freshman season and eventually became a Bob Cousy Award winner, an NCAA Champion, and a member of the First Team All-ACC squad in 2005. The point guard was picked as the 5th overall selection in the 2005 NBA Draft. He showed how good he was early on with a breakout game where he scored 31 points and averaged 11.9 points per game in his rookie season earning his NBA All-Rookie Second Team honors. Since then he has played for four other teams and had similar success including with the Knicks where his shooting has been instrumental in winning key games, including against the defending league champions at the time.
