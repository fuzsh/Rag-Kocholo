Monta Ellis scored 15 of his 30 points in the fourth quarter, Tyson Chandler had 11 points and 10 rebounds, and the Dallas Mavericks won for the eighth time in 10 games, beating the Raptors, 106-102, Friday night in Toronto.

Chandler Parsons scored 16 points, Dirk Nowitzki had 15, and Al-Farouq Aminu 14 as the Mavericks snapped Toronto's winning streak at six games.

Mavericks coach Rick Carlisle won his 300th game with Dallas. He's the third coach in team history to reach the mark, joining Don Nelson (339) and Dick Motta (329).

Kyle Lowry scored 25 points, Amir Johnson had 20, and Lou Williams 16 for the Raptors, who saw All-Star DeMar DeRozan leave in the third with a strained left groin.

Patrick Patterson had 10 points and 12 rebounds as the Raptors dropped to 9-2 at home. Toronto's only other home defeat this season was a 100-93 loss to Chicago on Nov. 13.

Grizzlies 112, Trail Blazers 99 — Marc Gasol had 26 points and nine assists as visiting Memphis snapped a nine-game Portland winning streak.

Mike Conley added 21 points and 10 rebounds for the Grizzlies, who have lost just two of 16 games this season for the NBA's best record. Memphis is undefeated against conference foes this season.

Thunder 105, Knicks 78 — Russell Westbrook scored 32 points in his first game back from a broken right hand, and host Oklahoma City routed New York.

Westbrook was injured in the second game of the season.

Timberwolves 120, Lakers 119 — Rookie guard Zach LaVine scored 18 of his team-high 28 points off the bench in the second quarter, and Mo Williams had 25 points and 11 assists to lead Minnesota over host Los Angeles. Kobe Bryant missed a 3-pointer as time expired that helped seal the win for Minnesota.

Spurs 112, Kings 104 — Tony Parker had 27 points and host San Antonio held on to beat Sacramento — its second straight victory without Gregg Popovich, who underwent an undisclosed minor medical procedure recently.

Clippers 102, Rockets 85 — Blake Griffin had 30 points and 10 rebounds and Jamal Crawford added 21 points to help visiting Los Angeles coast past injury-depleted Houston.

Pacers 98, Magic 83 — Rodney Stuckey scored a season-high 24 points and David West had 18 points in his first game of the season to lead host Indiana.

Hawks 100, Pelicans 91 — Jeff Teague scored 26 points, DeMarre Carroll added 19, and Atlanta held off visiting New Orleans.
