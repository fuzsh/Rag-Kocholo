In the Name of the Father is a biographical drama movie directed by Jim Sheridan, written for the screen by Sheridan and Terry George, based on the autobiographical book "Proved Innocent" by Gerry Conlon. Starring Daniel Day-Lewis, Pete Postlethwaite, Alison Crosbie, Emma Thompson, Anthony Brophy, and Frankie McCafferty, the film tells the true story of the Guildford Four. It was released on 27 December 1993 in Ireland, and nominated for 7 Oscars including "Best Picture". In the Name of the Father was shot in Dublin, County Dublin, Ireland, and London, England, UK. The jail scenes took place at Kilmainham Jail, Dublin. Filming also took place at Ardmore Studios.

Where was In the Name of the Father Filmed?

In the Name of the Father was filmed in 100 Huskisson St, Ardmore Studios, Dublin, Kilmainham Gaol, Liverpool, London and Upper Thames St.
