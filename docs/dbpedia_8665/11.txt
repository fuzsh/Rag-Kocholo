At first glance, The Great Cat Massacre by Robert Darnton seems to be about a senseless killing of cats; however we quickly learn that it examines how class conflicts plays a role in this story. During the 18th century, workers of the lower class were brutally mistreated. First of all, there were tension between the lower and middle class. In addition, Darnton describes how the bourgeois would mistreat the artisans as an example of the tension between each social class. Furthermore, the abusive of lower class led them to rise to rebellion. This story helps us understand how the culture of Europe was during the 18th century.
