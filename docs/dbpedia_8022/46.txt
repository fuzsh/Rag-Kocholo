The 1, 3, 5-triazine is also called s-triazine. It is an organic chemical compound and appears as clear, colorless to light yellow liquid. It is biodegradable and could be insoluble in water. The product is available in MEA triazine and MMA triazine types. As a scavenger, the 1, 3, 5-triazine is majorly utilized for sour gases including mercaptan and H2S in liquid or gaseous hydrocarbon streams. It produces thiazides when it reacts with H2S or mercaptan. Furthermore, in the Gattermann reaction, the 1,3,5-triazine is used as a replacement for HCN to produce aromatic aldehydes. The product finds its use in various applications such as medical, biological energy, agriculture, chemical, and oil & gas.

The rising demand for 1, 3, 5-triazine in the medical application will drive the market growth. The product exhibit, antimicrobial, antimalarial, anti-viral, anti-cancer properties. These properties surge the product demand for the treatment of bacterial infection and infected diseases. The spreading of infected diseases such as cold, flu, and tuberculosis coupled with advancement in the product will surge the demand for 1, 3, 5-triazine in the medical application.

However, the direct inhalation of 1, 3, 5-triazine might cause eye irritation, skin allergy, and eye damage. These factors restrict its adoption in various applications and are expected to hamper the market growth.
