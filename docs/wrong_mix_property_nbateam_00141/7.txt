BIOGRAPHY

Gerald Green was born on January 26th, 1986, in Houston, Texas. He is a shooting guard for the Houston Rockets in the NBA.

Green has a cousin in the Los Angeles Lakers, Danny Green. He has a brother, Garlon, who is a professional basketball player for Hapoel Tel Aviv in Israel.

He did not play high school basketball until his sophomore year when he played junior varsity for J. Frank Dobie High School. Green was named an All-American and was the high scorer with 24 points in the 2005 McDonald's All-American Game, which features two teams composed of the best high school basketball players in the United States. Green also won the McDonald's All-American Slam Dunk Contest that year.

Green was one of the last players to enter the NBA directly from high school. He was drafted by the Boston Celtics with the 18th overall pick in the 2005 NBA draft. Green won the 2007 NBA Slam Dunk Contest. On March 16th, 2007, he scored a career-high 25 points against the Dallas Mavericks. In July 2007, Green was traded to the Minnesota Timberwolves. In 2008, Green was traded to the Houston Rockets and was soon released. On July 1st, 2008, Green signed with the Dallas Mavericks.

On December 13th, 2011, Green signed with the Los Angeles Lakers, and on December 22nd, he was waived. A few days later, Green signed with the LA D-Fenders of the NBA Development League. He played at the NBA D-League All-Star game and was awarded the NBA D-League All-Star Game Most Valuable Player. Throughout the season, he averaged 19.1 points, 2.6 rebounds and 1.6 assists per game. He recorded a season-high of 35 points against the Erie BayHawks.

On February 27th, 2012, Green signed a second 10-day contract with the New Jersey Nets, then another 10-day contract on March 8th. On March 18th, he was signed for the rest of the season. Green averaged 12.9 points, 1.1 assists, and 3.5 rebounds in 31 games played. On July 12th, 2012, Green was signed with the Indiana Pacers. Then Green was traded to the Phoenix Suns. In 2015, he signed with the Miami Heat, and in a game against the Knicks, Green scored a season-high 25 points. In 2016, Green returned to the Boston Celtics and scored a season-high 19 points in a 113-103 win against the Grizzlies. In 2017, Green signed with the Milwaukee Bucks and was later waived in October.

In December 2017, Green returned with the Houston Rockets for the second time. On January 3rd, he scored a season-high 27 points in a 116-98 win over the Orlando Magic. Green became the first player in the franchise history to make seven or more 3-pointers in consecutive games after he made seven against Orlando. On March 30th, Green hit a buzzer-beating three-pointer to snatch a win against the Phoenix Suns. On July 10th, 2018, Green re-signed with the Rockets. In 2019, Green re-signed again.
