This print showcases the esteemed physicist Robert Andrews Millikan, an influential figure in 20th-century science. Born in 1868 and passing away in 1953, Millikan's groundbreaking contributions to physics earned him the prestigious Nobel Prize for Physics in 1923. His most notable achievement was his determination of the charge of the electron, a fundamental discovery that revolutionized our understanding of atomic structure. In addition to this remarkable feat, Millikan coined the term "cosmic rays" in 1925, further solidifying his legacy as a visionary scientist. This photograph captures him at a pivotal moment during his illustrious career when he made these significant breakthroughs.
