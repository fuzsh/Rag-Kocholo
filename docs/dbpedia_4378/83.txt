Edward Taylor is Professor of Agricultural and Resource Economics (ARE) and Director of the Center on Rural Economies of the Americas and Pacific Rim (REAP) at the University of California, Davis, where he teaches courses on international development economics and econometric methods. He is also co-editor of theAmerican Journal of Agricultural Economics and founder of the alternative textbook initiative, RebelText.org. Taylor has written extensively on the economy-wide impacts of agricultural and development policies and on immigration. He co-authored Worlds in Motion: Understanding International Migration at the End of the Millennium (Oxford University Press) and was appointed by the U.S. Commission on Immigration Reform to serve on the Binational Study of Mexico-U.S. Migration.
