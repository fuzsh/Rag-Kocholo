Tim Henman, Britain’s former top tennis player who made more than $11.6m (£7.1m) in career prize money, is hoping to net another fortune in property.

Born in Oxford, Henman started playing tennis at only two-and-a-half on his family’s court. He left school at 16 after gaining 10 GCSEs. In 1998, Henman became the first British player since Roger Taylor in 1973 to get to the semi-finals of the men’s singles at Wimbledon. He never reached the finals of any grand slam, but made six semi-finals, including four at Wimbledon.

In all, he won 15 ATP (Association of Tennis Professionals) titles — 11 in singles and four in doubles — and, at his best, was ranked number four in the world.
