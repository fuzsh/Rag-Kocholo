Judge Irma Elsa Gonzalez was appointed to the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of California in 1992, becoming the first Latina of Mexican heritage to be appointed to the Federal Bench. She served as Chief Judge of the District Court, as a Judge of the Superior Court in San Diego County, and as a U.S. Magistrate Judge in the Southern District of California. Judge Gonzalez is a founding Member of Latinas in the Law, a master of the Enright Inn of Court, on the Board of the Association of Business Trial Lawyers and a member of the San Diego La Raza Lawyers. She has received many accolades including the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Hispanic National Bar Association.
