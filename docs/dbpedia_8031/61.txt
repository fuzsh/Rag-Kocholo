Despite his success, Mal Peet did not write his first novel until he was 52. “I always intended to do it,” he said, “but there are so many good reasons for not writing and I’m pretty much expert at them all.” Keeper, published in 2003, about a goalkeeper trained by a ghost in the Amazon rainforest, won him the Branford Boase award for debut novels for children. It set Peet on a path that led to five idiosyncratic novels for teenagers as well as his satire for adults, The Murdstone Trilogy.
