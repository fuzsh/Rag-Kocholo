To say that the LAPD cop-turned-bestselling author Joseph Wambaugh commands respect from contemporary crime writers is to understate the case. The likes of Michael Connelly, Jonathan Kellerman and George Pelecanos line up to pay homage to him; James Ellroy refers to Wambaugh simply as “Master”. He has been credited with inventing the “psychological police procedural”, and without his books shows such as NYPD Blue and The Wire wouldn’t exist. The creator of the latter, David Simon, says that Wambaugh, whose 15th novel, Hollywood Hills, is out this month, “remains a working master of the genre he created”.
