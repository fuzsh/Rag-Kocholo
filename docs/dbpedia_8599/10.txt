Biography

Georg Johannes Rickhey was born August 25, 1898

He was a German engineer and the general director of Mittelwerk GmbH in Dora-Mittelbau.

Rickhey joined the Nazi Party in October 1931 as member number 664,050. From 1940 he guided Gauamt Technik in Essen and was promoted in 1942 to the leader of NSDAP Gau Essen and was also an authorized officer in an Essen mining company.

After he was arrested in 1945, he was taken by the U.S. Army to live at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Ohio where he worked under the terms of Operation Paperclip. He was subsequently indicted as part of the Dachau Trials of 1947 under accusations that he had worked closely with the SS and Gestapo and witnessed executions. He was acquitted due to a lack of evidence and returned to the US.

According to Wikipedia, he died in 1966.
