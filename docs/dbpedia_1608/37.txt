The distinctive neon sign on the tail end of "The Southern Aurora" at Junee, NSW.

The Southern Aurora was an overnight express passenger train that operated between Australia's two largest cities, Sydney and Melbourne. First-class throughout, including the dining facilities, the Southern Aurora featured all-sleeper accommodation.

The train first ran on 16 April 1962 after the opening of the North East standard gauge line from Melbourne to Albury, eliminating the break-of-gauge between the capital cities.

After the demise of sleeper trains in the early 1990s, many of the carriages passed to various preservation groups around the country.

Today, The Southern Aurora can still be seen. plying routes across most of Australia's standard gauge railway network, not as a point to point express, but rather as a rail cruise experience linking its passengers with various experiences along its route.
