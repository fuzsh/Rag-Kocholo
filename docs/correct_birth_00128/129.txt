Olivia Newton-John is an English singer, songwriter, and actress. She was born on Sept. 26, 1948, in Cambridge, England.

Newton-John has sold over 100 million records worldwide, making her one of the best-selling music artists of all time.

Her most iconic songs include "Physical," "Hopelessly Devoted to You," and "You're the One That I Want" from the Grease soundtrack.

Olivia Newton-John has won four Grammy Awards throughout her career.

She is also recognized for her philanthropic efforts, particularly in raising awareness and funds for cancer research and treatment.

Newton-John battled breast cancer and was an advocate for cancer awareness and holistic health approaches.

In 2020, she received a damehood from Queen Elizabeth II for her services to charity, cancer research, and entertainment.
