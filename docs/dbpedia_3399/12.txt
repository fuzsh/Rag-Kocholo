He was born Aug. 13, 1933 in Mertingen, Germany to the late Josef and Josefa Wiedemann. He moved to the U.S. when he was in his 20s.

He was a veteran of the U.S. Army and the National Guard. Then he drove a truck for W.J. Casey Trucking and Rigging. He was a trained cabinetmaker and enjoyed building furniture and other items for his home.

His first wife, Elisabeth, died in 1987 and his second wife, Ingrid, died recently.

He is survived by his children, Barbara, Joseph, Annemarie and John; his daughter-in-law Beverly; and his two granddaughters, Elisabeth and Bridget.

Services: Gathering in celebration of his life Friday, April 9, 4-8 p.m. at his home. Arrangements by the Community Funeral Home, Passaic.
