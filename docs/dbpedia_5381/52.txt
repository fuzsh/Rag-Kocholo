The Mirror Has Two Faces is an American romantic drama movie directed by Barbra Streisand, written by Richard LaGravenese, and based on the 1958 French film "Le Miroir à deux faces" by André Cayatte. Starring Streisand, Jeff Bridges, Pierce Brosnan, George Segal, Brenda Vaccaro, Lauren Bacall, and Mimi Rogers, the film was released on November 15, 1996, and was nominated for 2 Academy Awards (Best Supporting Actress and Best Original Song). The Mirror Has Two Faces was mainly shot in New York City, New York, United States. Filming locations included Columbia University and JFK International Airport.
