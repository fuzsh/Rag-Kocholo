1. Nobel Prize Awarded Women in Literature Since 1901 Ajda BASTAN Assist.Prof.Dr, Department of Translation and Interpreting, Cumhuriyet University-Turkey email:abastan@cumhuriyet.edu.tr

2. • Nobel Prizes are seen as the most prestigious awards on the planet in their distinctive fields. This study introduces the fourteen Nobel Prize women laureates in literature between 1901 and 2016. Out of the 113 winners of the Nobel Prize for Literature, only 14 are women between the years 1901 and 2016. These women are from various societies and wrote in very unique styles.

3. Selma Lagerlöf (1858-1940) -Sweden • The Nobel Prize in Literature 1909 was awarded to Selma Lagerlöf "in appreciation of the lofty idealism, vivid imagination and spiritual perception that characterize her writings". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 51. Lagerlöf’s field is prose, and she wrote in the Swedish language.

4. Grazia Deledda (1871-1936)- Italy • The Nobel Prize in Literature 1926 was awarded to Grazia Deledda "for her idealistically inspired writings which with plastic clarity picture the life on her native island and with depth and sympathy deal with human problems in general". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 55. Deledda’s field is prose, and she wrote in the Italian language.

5. Sigrid Undset (1882-1949)-Norway • The Nobel Prize in Literature 1928 was awarded to Sigrid Undset "principally for her powerful descriptions of Northern life during the Middle Ages". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 46. Undset’s field is prose, and she wrote in the Norwegian language.

6. Pearl Buck (1892-1973) -The United States • The Nobel Prize in Literature 1938 was awarded to Pearl Buck "for her rich and truly epic descriptions of peasant life in China and for her biographical masterpieces". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 46. Her field is prose, and she wrote in the English language.

7. Gabriela Mistral (1889-1957)-Chile • The Nobel Prize in Literature 1945 was awarded to Gabriela Mistral "for her lyric poetry which, inspired by powerful emotions, has made her name a symbol of the idealistic aspirations of the entire Latin American world". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 56. Mistral’s field is poetry, and she wrote in the Spanish language.

8. Nelly Sachs (1891-1970)- Sweden (Born in Germany) • The Nobel Prize in Literature 1966 was divided equally between Shmuel Yosef Agnon "for his profoundly characteristic narrative art with motifs from the life of the Jewish people" and Nelly Sachs "for her outstanding lyrical and dramatic writing, which interprets Israel's destiny with touching strength". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 75. Sachs’s field is poetry and drama, and she wrote in the German language.

9. Nadine Gordimer (1923-2014)-South Africa The Nobel Prize in Literature 1991 was awarded to Nadine Gordimer "who through her magnificent epic writing has - in the words of Alfred Nobel - been of very great benefit to humanity". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 68. Nadine Gordimer's field is prose, and she wrote in the English language.

10. Toni Morrison (1931- -The United States • The Nobel Prize in Literature 1993 was awarded to Toni Morrison "who in novels characterized by visionary force and poetic import, gives life to an essential aspect of American reality". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 62. Toni Morrison's field is prose, and she writes in the English language.

11. Wislawa Szymborska (1923-2012)-Poland • The Nobel Prize in Literature 1996 was awarded to Wislawa Szymborska "for poetry that with ironic precision allows the historical and biological context to come to light in fragments of human reality". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 73. Wislawa Szymborska's field is poetry, and she wrote in the Polish language.

12. Elfriede Jelinek (1946- Austria • The Nobel Prize in Literature 2004 was awarded to Elfriede Jelinek "for her musical flow of voices and counter-voices in novels and plays that with extraordinary linguistic zeal reveal the absurdity of society's clichés and their subjugating power". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 58. Elfriede Jelinek’s field is drama, poetry and prose, and she writes in the German language.

13. Doris Lessing (1919-2013)-The United Kingdom • The Nobel Prize in Literature 2007 was awarded to Doris Lessing "that epicist of the female experience, who with scepticism, fire and visionary power has subjected a divided civilisation to scrutiny". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 88. Doris Lessing's field is prose, and she wrote in the English language.

14. Herta Müller (1953- Germany • The Nobel Prize in Literature 2009 was awarded to Herta Müller "who, with the concentration of poetry and the frankness of prose, depicts the landscape of the dispossessed". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 56. Herta Müller's field is prose, and she writes in the German language.

15. Alice Munro (1931- Canada • The Nobel Prize in Literature 2013 was awarded to Alice Munro "master of the contemporary short story". She won this prestigious prize at the age of 82. Alice Munro’s field is prose, and she writes in the English language.
