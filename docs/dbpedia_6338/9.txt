Béla Goldoványi was a Hungarian sprinter born on December 20, 1925, in Budapest. He specialized in sprint races and competed in the 1940s and 1950s, participating in three Summer Olympics. He achieved notable success, winning a bronze medal in the 4x100 meters relay at the 1952 Olympics and an individual gold at the 1954 European Championships. Throughout his career, Goldoványi also won 18 national championships across different sprint events, including multiple titles in the 100 meters and 200 meters.
