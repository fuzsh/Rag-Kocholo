Marvin J. Mareck, age 60, passed away from a long battle with immune deficiency disease on Monday, August 25, 2008 at his home in Beaver Dam, WI.

Friends may gather on Thursday, August 28, 2008 from 5:00 PM until 8:00 PM at the Murray Funeral Home in Beaver Dam, WI and on Friday, August 29, 2008 from 10:00 AM until 11:00 AM at St. Katharine Drexel Spring Street Church in Beaver Dam. A Mass of Christian Burial will follow on Friday at the Church at 11:00 AM with Fr. Jose Gonzalez officiating. Burial will be on Saturday, August 30, 2008 at 11:30 AM at Mt. Olivet Catholic Cemetery in Waterloo, IA with Fr. Dennis Juhl officiating.

Marvin John Mareck was born on October 18, 1947 to Stephen and Frances (Stangler) Mareck in St. Cloud, MN. He was a 1965 graduate of Albany High School and later graduated with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Business Management from St. Cloud State University. Marv proudly served in the US Army Reserves from 1966 to 1972 during the Vietnam War. On October 20, 1973 he was united in marriage with Kathleen Ann Kahler in Des Moines, IA. Marv worked as a manager in a retail chain before joining John Deere in January of 1979. He worked as a Supply Engineer until his retirement in February of 2008. Marvin was an avid sports fan, rooting on the Badgers, Packers and Brewers. Most of all, Marv loved his family and friends. They were the most important thing in his life and he treasured the time he spent with them.

Survivors are his wife of 34 years, Kathy (Kahler) Mareck, Beaver Dam, daughter, Trisha (David) Burbie, South Pasadena, CA, mother and step-father, Fran and Joe Blommel, Albany, MN, brothers and sisters, Dennis (Marcy) Mareck, Albany, MN, Dave (Darlene) Mareck, Sauk Centre, MN, Kathy (Ralph) Schmitz, Sauk Rapids, MN, Sharon (Ron) Spanier, St. Cloud, MN and Steve (Trese) Mareck, St. Cloud, MN. He is further survived by his brother”s-in-law, Steve (Mary) Kahler, Marion, IA and Bill (Janet) Kahler, Cedar Falls, IA, nieces, nephews, other relatives and friends. Marv was preceded in death by his father, a daughter, Ann and a son Michael.

The family suggests that memorials be made in Marvin”s name to the Immune Deficiency Foundation or to Hillside Hospice.

The Murray Funeral Home in Beaver Dam is caring for the family. Online condolences may be made on our website at www.MurrayFH.com.
