Rochester Post-Bulletin, Rochester MN, November 05, 1996:

Rites Wednesday for Leo S. Kieffer, 87

ST. CHARLES -- A Mass of Christian burial will be offered for Leo S. Kieffer at 1 p.m. Wednesday at St. Charles Borromeo Catholic Church, with the Rev. William Kulas officiating. Burial will be in Calvary Cemetery in St. Charles.

Mr. Kieffer, 87, of Chippewa Falls, wis., formerly of St. Charles, died Sunday morning (Nov. 3, 1996) at Lakeside Nursing Home in Chippewa Falls.

He was born Sept. 23, 1909, in Elba Township, Winona County. On Feb. 27, 1933, he married Evelyn Siebenaler at St. Aloysius Catholic Church in Elba and they moved to St. Charles in 1947. His wife died Jan. 25, 1978. He moved to Cornell, Wis., in 1990. Mr. Kieffer sheared sheep and was a wool buyer for over 50 years and also was a beekeeper for many years. He was a member of St. Charles Borromeo Catholic Church and Holy Cross Catholic Church in Cornell, was fraternal secretary for St. Aloysius Society of Catholic Aid Association for over 25 years, and was a member of the Elba Sportsman's Club.

Survivors include a son, Bernard of Sacramento, Calif.; two daughters, Rose (Mrs. Leonard) Pieper of Cornell, Wis., and Jane (Mrs. Robert) Schultz of North Richland Hills, Texas; 15 grandchildren; 22 great-grandchildren; and a brother, Paul of Altura. An infant son and five brothers preceded him in death.

Friends may call from 4 p.m. to 8 p.m. today and 11 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. Wednesday at Hoff Funeral Home-St. Charles Chapel. The rosary will be recited at 7:30 p.m. today at the funeral home.
