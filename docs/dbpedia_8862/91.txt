The climate of Cangas de Onís (Spain)

Cangas de Onís is a small village and a municipality located in the northwestern part of Spain in the Asturias region. It is an old village which was already inhabited during Roman times. In the 8th century AD the city was the capital of the Asturias Kingdom for several years. One of the oldest Catholic churches in Spain, the Santa Cruz de Cangas de Onís dates back to this period. One of the best known structures in the village is the Roman bridge. Cangas de Onís is located in the vicinity of Picos de Europa which is part of the Cantabrian mountain range. Cangas de Onís has a moderate maritime climate strongly influenced by both the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Biscay. Because of these influences extremes in weather are uncommon in this region. Only in the vicinity of Picos de Europa very high or very low temperatures may occur. Most precipitation falls during the winter, summers are fairly dry.
