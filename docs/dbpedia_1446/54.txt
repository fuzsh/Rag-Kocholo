Irving L. Ausman Jr., 67, of Clarence, a U.S. Army veteran and former employee of T.J. Lipton Co., died Saturday (Nov. 27, 1993) in Veterans Hospital, Buffalo, after a long illness.

Ausman worked as a maintenance man for more than 20 years at Lipton's Albion plant before retiring about 10 years ago.

The Army veteran was an avid pheasant hunter.

Survivors include four sons, Irving III of Cheektowaga, James of Springville, Clint of Medina and Timothy; two daughters, Cindra of Brockport and Dawn of Buffalo; two brothers, Robert and Harvey; a sister, Betty Miller of Texas; 10 grandchildren; and a great-grandchild.

A memorial service will be held at 11 a.m. Tuesday in Merrill-Grinnell Funeral Home, 12 Ingersoll St. [hrbck]
