Inzamam-ul-Haq – profile, career and stats

Inzamam-ul-Haq is a former Pakistani batter who also captained the side.

Inzamam is regarded as one of the finest middle-order batters to grace the game.

Inzamam was in the squad of Pakistan which won the 1992 Cricket World Cup under the leadership of Imran Khan.

Inzamam holds the record for most runs as a Pakistani batter and seventh overall in ODI cricket with his stellar 11,701 runs. Currently, Inzamam-ul-Haq is Pakistan’s third-highest run-scorer in Test cricket with 8,829 in 198 innings.

Inzamam is the only Pakistani batter to score 20,000 international runs.

In 2003, after dominating the crease for a decade, Inzamam was appointed captain of the Pakistan cricket team but was removed in 2007 due to Pakistan’s early exit from the Cricket World Cup.

Inzamam is often remembered by the nickname of ‘Sultan of Multan’

What is the nickname of Inzamam-ul-Haq?

When did Inzamam-ul-Haq play his first international match?

Inzamam played his first ODI against West Indies in 1991 and in 1992 he played his first Test against England.

When did Inzamam-ul-Haq play his last international match?

In 2007, Inzamam played his last ODI and Test against Zimbabwe and South Africa respectively.

Did Inzamam ever play T20I cricket?

Inzamam-ul-Haq played just one T20I and it was against England in 2006. He scored just 11 runs in the game.

How many Tests did Inzamam ul Haq play and how many runs did he score in them?

Inzamam-ul-Haq played 120 Tests and scored 8,830 runs in them at an average of 49.60 with 25 hundred and 46 fifties.

How many ODIs did Inzamam ul Haq play and how many runs did he score in them?

Inzamam-ul-Haq played 378 ODIs and scored 11,739 runs in them at an average of 39.53 with 10 hundreds and 83 fifties.
