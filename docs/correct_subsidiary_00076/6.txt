Dramatic as the Pasha Bulker was, grounded on Newcastle's Nobbys Beach, the salvage business is no longer the lucrative proposition it used to be.

A younger world fleet and improved regulation of crew standards saw the number of maritime accidents worldwide fall steadily over the past two decades, despite the increase in sea trade. Australian waters were 7 per cent safer in 2005 than in 2000, a survey by Norwegian standards body Det norske Veritas showed.
