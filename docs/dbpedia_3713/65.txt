10 Questions for the Dalai Lama is a 2006 documentary film that chronicles a unique interview with the spiritual leader of Tibet, The Dalai Lama. The movie is an hour and a half long, directed by Rick Ray, and features The Dalai Lama, Tenzin Bagdro, and Bob Dole. The film follows Rick Ray on a pilgrimage throughout India as he seeks an audience with The Dalai Lama. It is his desire to ask The Dalai Lama 10 very special questions that have been on his mind about life, religion, and human nature. Once Ray finally gains access to The Dalai Lama, he only has one hour to ply him with questions.

The documentary provides a view into The Dalai Lama's life, thinking and philosophy. It also documents his incredible inner peace and how he is able to maintain it despite the trials and tribulations of life.

The movie covers a wide array of issues, including the subjects of suffering, war, forgiveness, spirituality, compassion, and happiness. During the interview, The Dalai Lama provides insight into how he personally copes with suffering, and shares his views on how a truly compassionate society can be created.

As the interview progresses, it becomes clear that The Dalai Lama has seen and experienced great suffering in his life but has always been able to maintain a sense of inner peace. This is the crux of his teachings and his message to the world - that we can all find happiness and inner peace in our lives by embracing compassion and kindness towards others.

In addition to the interview with The Dalai Lama, the movie features a number of other direct interviews with individuals who come from very diverse backgrounds. Each person is asked a series of questions about their thoughts, feelings, and notions of happiness, spirituality, and the nature of existence. From these dialogues, one gets a sense of just how universal and relevant The Dalai Lama's message and principles are.

10 Questions for the Dalai Lama is a must-watch for anyone seeking poignant answers to the deeper questions of life. The film is incredibly inspiring, enlightening, and thought-provoking. The viewer comes away with a deep appreciation of the power of compassion, inner peace, and the ability to live a truly fulfilling life.

Overall, this documentary offers a fascinating insight into one of the world's most significant spiritual leaders, and leaves the viewer with plenty of food for thought.
