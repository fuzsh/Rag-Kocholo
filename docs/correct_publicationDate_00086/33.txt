“Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde” is a classic novella by Robert Louis Stevenson that delves into the duality of human nature through the story of a respected scientist, Dr. Henry Jekyll, and his dark alter ego, Mr. Edward Hyde. The narrative unfolds through the eyes of Mr. Utterson, a lawyer and friend to Dr. Jekyll, as he uncovers the disturbing connection between the upright doctor and the vile Hyde, whose cruel deeds instill fear across London. Stevenson’s tale explores the struggle between good and evil, the complexities of identity, and the inner conflict that ensues when forbidden desires break free. This iconic work has had a profound impact on literature and popular culture that remains to this day.
