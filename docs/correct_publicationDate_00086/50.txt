Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde is an 1886 Gothic novella by the late Scottish author Robert Louis Stevenson. It follows Gabriel John Utterson, a London-based legal practitioner who investigates a series of strange occurrences between his old friend, Dr. Henry Jekyll, and a murderous criminal named Edward Hyde.

Victims[]

Sir Danvers Carew, MP - Beaten to death by Mr. Hyde by stomping on him and bludgeoning him with his cane.

Dr. Hastie Lanyon - Succumbed to a heart attack after seeing Jekyll and Hyde's transformation process.

Dr. Henry "Harry" Jekyll/Mr. Edward Hyde - Committed suicide by drinking a phial of poison while in his Jekyll persona to prevent his Hyde persona from hurting anyone else.
