Sir Paul Stephenson is the second Metropolitan Police Commissioner in two and a half years whose career has ended in abrupt resignation.

When he stepped in after his predecessor Sir Ian Blair was forced out by Boris Johnson, the Mayor of London, Sir Paul was seen as a safe pair of hands. He was viewed as a man who could provide continuity at a time when the country faced a serious terrorist threat as well as the challenge of policing the Olympics.

Sir Paul was less interested in theories about policing and instead insisted the job of the police was to lock up bad people and make people feel safe.
