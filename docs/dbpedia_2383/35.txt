Head of Hypnos

Raeder #: 65

Collection: Rome, National Museum

Inventory #: 124 680

Provenance: Found 1928 in the cryptoportico that leads from the entrance to the Piazza d'Oro to the so-called Tempe dale (R. Paribeni, NSc 1932, 124f. Nr. 3, Figure 4).

Material: Marble

Dimensions: Height: 0.23 m

Condition: n/a

Discussion: With total misapprehension of the portrayed theme (Hypnos himself sinking into sleep) and the individual stylistic forms, J. Papadopoulos (in: A. Giuliano) once more has traced the head to a bronze prototype of the 4th century B.C. P. Zanker, on the contrary, has convincingly and thoroughly established it to be a Roman invention.

References: S. Aurigemma, Villa Adriana (1961) 162 Figure 165; Helbig 4 III Nr. 2274; S. Aurigemma, Le Terme di Diocleziano e il Mus. Naz. Rom.6 (1970) 100 Nr. 276: Zanker 155f. Plate 85; A. Giuliano, Mus. Naz. Rom. Le Sculture I 1 (1979) 201 Nr. 125.

Plate #: 5

Raeder page(s): 78-79

Full Citation: Joachim Raeder, Die statuarische Ausstattung der Villa Hadriana bei Tivoli (Peter Lang: Frankfurt am Main, Bern: 1963) 31.
