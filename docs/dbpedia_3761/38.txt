The London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE) is a public research university based in London, England, and a member institution of the federal University of London. It was founded in 1895 by Fabian Society members Sidney Webb, Beatrice Webb, Graham Wallas, and George Bernard Shaw for the betterment of society. LSE is located in Westminster, Central London, near the boundary between Covent Garden and Holborn. 150 nationalities are represented among its student body and the school has one of the highest percentages of international students (70%) in the world. Its alumni and staff include 55 past or present heads of state or government and 18 Nobel laureates. Out of all European universities, LSE has educated the most billionaires according to a 2014 global census of U.S dollar billionaires.
