2. DEFINITION The Chanak Crisis was an Anglo-Turkish dispute that brought the two countries to the brink of war in 1922.

3. Question 1 What were the causes of the crisis?

4. Answer 1 Britain and Greece backed the Turkish sultan against Turkish nationalists wanting to overthrow the Treaty of Sevres. The subsequent nationalist victory over the Greeks made south-eastern Europe vulnerable to Turkish invasions.

5. Question 2 Who was Mustafa Kemal Ataturk?

6. Answer 2 A Turkish officer who led the nationalists against the sultan as Mustafa Kemal Ataturk, founded the Turkish republic and was president of Turkey, 1923-1938.

7. Question 3 What prevented all-out war between Britain and Turkey?

8. Answer 3 It was only 4 years after the end of the First World War. Few people in Britain or the Dominions wanted war.

9. Question 4 How was the crisis resolved?

10. Answer 4 British forces were outnumbered and surrounded by the Turks at the port of Chanak. Both sides eventually agreed to a truce.
