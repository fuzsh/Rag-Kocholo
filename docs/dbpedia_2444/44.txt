Two years after Hitler’s troops marched into Paris, an RAF wing commander carried out one of the most daring missions of the Second World War and brought hope to the occupied city.

The full story of Ken Gatward’s heroic low-level raid has come to light with the sale of the Distinguished Flying Cross he was awarded on his safe return to England.

Gatward and his navigator Flight Sergeant George Fern flew their Bristol Beaufighter at window height down the Champs Élysée, dropped a French Tricolour on the Arc de Triomphe, then strafed the headquarters of the Gestapo, the Nazi secret police. The pair could see black-uniformed SS officers running for their lives as 20mm canon shells struck the building.
