Patricia D'Arbanville (born May 25, 1951) is an American actress known for her appearance in Andy Warhol projects. She gained recognition for her roles in films such as Flesh (1968), L'Amour (1973), and Bilitis (1977). D'Arbanville has had a successful career in both film and television, starring in various projects in the United States and France. In 1987, she received a Drama-Logue Award for Best Actress for her stage performance in Italian American Reconciliation (1987). Notably, she portrayed Lt. Virginia Cooper on the Fox series New York Undercover. D'Arbanville's personal life includes a romantic relationship with singer-songwriter Cat Stevens, who wrote the hit song 'Lady D'Arbanville' about her.
