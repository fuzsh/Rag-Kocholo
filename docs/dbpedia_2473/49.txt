Jo Van Fleet was born on December 29, 1915 in Oakland, California. She established herself as a notable dramatic actress on Broadway over several years, winning a Tony Award in 1954 for her skill in a difficult role, playing an unsympathetic, even abusive character, in Horton Foote's "The Trip to Bountiful" with Lillian Gish and Eva Marie Saint. Her first film role was playing the estranged mother of James Dean's character in Jenseits von Eden (1955). This debut performance earned Van Fleet an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress. Her subsequent film work was steady through 1960, then very sporadic, and include such films as Die tätowierte Rose (1955), Und morgen werd' ich weinen (1955), Heißer Süden (1956), Zwei rechnen ab (1957) and Der Unbeugsame (1967).

In 1958, Van Fleet was nominated for a Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actress in a Play for her performance in "Look Homeward, Angel" on Broadway. Other films include Wilder Strom (1960), Rodgers and Hammerstein's Cinderella (1965) and Lass mich küssen deinen Schmetterling (1968). Her television work include Alfred Hitchcock präsentiert (1955), Bonanza (1959), Thriller (1960) and Verrückter wilder Westen (1965).

Jo Van Fleet died at age 80 of undisclosed causes on June 10, 1996 in Queens, New York City.
