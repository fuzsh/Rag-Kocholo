Nino Gogoladze

Executive Board

PFP, 2013, Chicago, Illinois National Programme Coordinator, OSCE High Commissioner on National Minorities In her current position, Nino Gogoladze is a National Programme Coordinator for the OSCE High Commissioner on National Minorities (OSCE/HCNM) in Tbilisi, Georgia. She previously worked as a Project Coordinator at the UN Population Fund (UNFPA), a Programme Coordinator at the Heinrich Boell Foundation, and as a National Anti-Trafficking Officer for the OSCE Mission to Georgia. Nino represents the OSCE HCNM in Georgia and oversees the work of the office seeking to prevent ethnic conflict in the country and integration of national minorities to promote diverse and secure society. She follows closely the political situation in Georgia and the region and evaluates its possible impact on inter-ethnic relations. As a program coordinator Nino is responsible for project implementation, monitoring, and analysis of the impact of ongoing course of the OSCE HCNM activities in the country. She is also a liaison for Governmental bodies, international organizations and NGOS present in the country. Nino holds LLM degree from London School of Economics and Political Science; and MA in International Relations and European Studies from the Central European University, Budapest, Hungary. Nino has participated in a number of leadership programmes, including the Atlantic Council, and the Aspen Institute Romania. She is also publishing on the national minority participation issues in Georgia.
