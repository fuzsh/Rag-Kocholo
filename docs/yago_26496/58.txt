Princess Marie of Hohenzollern-Sigmaringen (Marie Luise Alexandra Karoline; 17 November 1845 – 26 November 1912), later Countess of Flanders, was a princess of Hohenzollern-Sigmaringen, later simply of Hohenzollern. She married Prince Philippe, Count of Flanders, second son of King Leopold I of Belgium, and she was the mother of King Albert I.

Marriage[edit]

On 25 April 1867 at Saint Hedwig's Cathedral in Berlin, she married Prince Philippe, Count of Flanders, son of King Leopold I of Belgium and Louise of Orléans.

Issue[edit]

Prince Baudouin of Belgium (3 June 1869 – 23 January 1891) died unmarried.

Princess Henriette of Belgium (30 November 1870 – 28 March 1948) married Prince Emmanuel, Duke of Vendôme and had issue.

Princess Joséphine Marie of Belgium (30 November 1870 — 18 January 1871) she died ageed one month old.

Princess Joséphine Caroline of Belgium (18 October 1872 – 6 January 1958) she married Prince Karl Anton of Hohenzollern and had issue.

Albert I of Belgium (8 April 1875 – 17 February 1934) married Duchess Elisabeth in Bavaria and had issue.

Titles and styles[edit]

17 November 1845 – 25 April 1867 Her Serene Highness Princess Marie of Hohenzollern-Sigmaringen.

25 April 1867 – 17 November 1905 Her Royal Highness The Countess of Flanders.

17 November 1905 – 26 November 1912 Her Royal Highness The Dowager Countess of Flanders.
