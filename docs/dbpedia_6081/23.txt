In the lower left corner of the canvas is a signature identifying it as by Bartolomeo Bassante – a disciple of José de Ribera – even though most contemporary critics do not entirely agree with this attribution. From the collections of Queen Isabella Farnese, it was mistakenly attributed to the Genoese Giovanni Benedetto Castiglione in her inventory of 1746. The signature was acknowledged and attributed to Bassante in the subsequent inventory of Queen Isabella’s testament in 1766. Pérez Sánchez (1970) approved this historical attribution, although the most recent studies by Porzio (2014), Spinosa (2021) and Farina (2022) agree in assigning it to the painter Antonio de Bellis. Nonetheless, the third scholar believes it to be the result of a collaboration with an artist close to Agostino Beltrano. Thus, the signature would be an apocryphal 18th-century addition.
