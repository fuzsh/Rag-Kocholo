Oracle Corp moved forward in its bid to reshape the business software market on November 02 by agreeing to acquire document management software maker Stellent Inc, in a deal valued at $440 million in cash.

Redwood Shores, California-based Oracle said in a statement it was making a cash tender offer of $13.50 per Stellent share.

Stellent makes enterprise content management software used to maintain the electronic filing systems of organisations. It counts 4,700 customers world-wide -- including Procter & Gamble, Merrill Lynch, The Home Depot, IING, Vodafone, and Bayer Corp.
