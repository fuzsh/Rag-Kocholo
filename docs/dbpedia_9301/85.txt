Babyface, whose real name is Kenneth Brian Edmonds, performed "The Star-Spangled Banner" on Sunday night at Gainbridge Fieldhouse in downtown Indianapolis, Indiana to commence the 2024 NBA All-Star Game.

Edmonds, 64, is a highly influential American singer, songwriter, and producer in the R&B and pop music genres. He gained prominence in the late 1980s and 1990s for his smooth vocal style, heartfelt lyrics, and ability to craft hit songs for himself and other artists.

Babyface has worked with several A-list artists

Throughout his career, Babyface has written and produced numerous chart-topping singles and albums for artists such as Whitney Houston, Boyz II Men, Mariah Carey, Toni Braxton, and many others. He has won 13 Grammy Awards for his work, including awards for Song of the Year and Producer of the Year.
