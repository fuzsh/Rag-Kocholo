Lieutenant John Vesey Knox, Northamptonshire Regiment. He was the eldest son of Edmund Francis Vesey Knox, KC, MP, and Anne Elizabeth Knox (nee Lloyd) of Paddington, London and Shimnah, Newcastle, County Down, Ireland. A nephew of Brigadier-General A W F Knox, CB, and of Brigadier-General H H S Knox, DSO. He married Hilda Florence Reynolds in Jul-Sep 1913 in Plymouth, Devon.

He was educated at Wixenford, Wokingham, Berkshire and Rugby School (Steel's), Warwickshire. Gentleman Cadet at the Royal Military College, Sandhurst (Feb-Dec 1911). Commissioned into 1st Battalion The Northamptonshire Regiment as a 2nd Lieutenant. He resigned his commission and took up an appointment in Feb 1914 as a clerk in the Principal Probate Registry at Somerset House, whilst living at 76 Elm Park Gardens. When WW1 started he volunteered for service on 5 Aug 1914 and was commissioned 2nd Lieutenant in the 18th (County of London) Battalion, London Regiment (London Irish Rifles). Having filed for divorce from his wife Hilda, for adultery, on 6 Oct 1914, he was attached to the 2nd London Divisional Cyclist Company being promoted to Lieutenant on 25 Sep 1915. He compiled the Company War Diary, whilst in action in France and Flanders, until the end of April 1916. He transferred to the Royal Flying Corps in 1 May 1916. He was awarded a Royal Aero Club Aviators Certificate (No.3700) on 17 Oct 1916 after taking his test on a Maurice Farman biplane at the Military Flying School, Brooklands, Surrey. e was attached to No 56 Squadron, Royal Flying Corps at Colney, Hertfordshire but suffered a fractured jaw on 21 Nov 1916, whilst flying a Curtis Aircraft B624, when it crashed on top of a tree after a 200 foot spinning nose dive. Admitted to the Royal Flying Corps Hospital at 37 Bryaston Square, London, he was returned to No. 42 (Training) Squadron for light duty on 5 Jul 1917. He was attached to No 85 Squadron at Norwich for light duty and flying in Sep 1917 and then to No. 19 (Training) Squadron at Hounslow in Nov 1917. he was appointed as an Instructor at No 9 Flying School, Norwich. He was accidentally killed on 4 Jan 1918 when a passenger in a RE8 A3891 flown by 2nd Lieutenant P D Parker. The aircraft suffered from an engine failure, fell into a spin, crashed, and caught fire, killing both inside it. His body was returned to Northern Ireland and was buried in St Coleman's Churchyard, Newcastle, County Down, Northern Ireland.

He is also commemorated on the Wixenford School WW1 Roll of Honour in the School Chapel, Ludgrove Preparatory School, Ludgrove Path, Ludgrove, Wokingham, Berkshire (see: https://www.iwm.org.uk/memorials/item/memorial/18747), on the Rugby School WW1 Memorial in the Rugby School Chapel, Lawrence Sheriff Street, Rugby, Warwickshire, England (see: https://www.warmemorialsonline.org.uk/memorial/136533) and on the Newcastle War Memorial in Central Promenade, Newcastle, County Down, Northern Ireland (see: http://www.irishwarmemorials.ie/Memorials-Detail?memoId=197\).
