Mammootty

Mammootty, whose real name is Muhammad Kutty Ismail Paniparambil, is an Indian film actor and producer who primarily works in Malayalam cinema. He was born on September 7, 1951, in Chempu, Kerala, India. Mammootty made his acting debut in 1971 with the Malayalam film Anubhavangal Paalichakal, directed by K.S. Sethumadhavan.

He gained popularity for his performances in films like New Delhi, Oru Vadakkan Veeragatha, Mathilukal, and Drishyam. Mammootty is considered one of the finest actors in Indian cinema and has won several awards, including three National Film Awards, seven Kerala State Film Awards, and numerous Filmfare Awards.

Apart from his acting career, Mammootty is also a philanthropist and is actively involved in social work through his charity organization named Mammootty Charitable Trust. He is also a lawyer and has practiced law before he entered the film industry.

In addition to Malayalam cinema, Mammootty has also acted in several films in other Indian languages like Tamil, Telugu, and Hindi. He is regarded as one of the greatest actors in Indian cinema and has a huge fan following in India and abroad.

Mammootty is a popular Malayalam Actor. He has appeared in many South Indian regional films and Bollywood movies. He has been honoured with National Film Award for best actor and Padma Sree by Govt. of India.
