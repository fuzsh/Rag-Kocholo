Elisabeth of Wied (1843-1916), consort of King Carol I of Romania. Eldest child of Hermann, Prince of Wied and Princess Marie of Nassau. Married in 1869. Had demonstrated a facility for languages early in life and was an accomplished pianist, but her interests were literary, with a focus on poetry and folklore that developed in part from her exposure to Romanian legends and ballads. Was a prolific writer who went by the pen name of “Carmen Sylva.” Crowned as the first Queen of Romania in 1881, the year in which Romania was declared a kingdom.
