Serbia and Montenegro participated twice in the Contest after debuting at the 2004 Contest in Istanbul with the song "Lane moje" by Željko Joksimović, which finished as runner-up to "Wild Dances" by Ruslana of Ukraine.

They appeared again the following year finishing in seventh place. Both entries were performed in Serbian and Montenegrin respectively and following their withdrawal prior to the 2006 Contest in Athens, they still appeared to present their votes.

Following independence in 2007, they have continued to compete separately as Serbia and Montenegro.

The country made only one appearance at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2005 in Hasselt, finishing in 13th place. Following independence, it has continued to enter as Serbia and Montenegro.
