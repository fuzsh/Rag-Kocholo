Bob McMullan and Sean Innis explore the demands and support surrounding government ministers. They are responsible for running important portfolio areas, even though they’re unlikely to have professional expertise in that area. Despite all this responsibility, and power, ministers arrive in the job with no training.

Australia’s leading institutions, including government, business, NGOs and media, are among the least-trusted in the world at a time when the Australian economy has experienced 25 years of economic growth. What can the Australian Public Service do to help bridge the trust divide?
