P. W. BOTHA, the eighth Prime Minister and the first State President of South Africa, was a contradictory figure. Although he admitted that the apartheid state “must adapt or die” and introduced constitutional reforms that brought Coloured and Asian representation into Parliament and the Cabinet, he also ignored the demands of Africans and supervised the brutal crackdown on violent anti-apartheid unrest that had threatened to render South Africa ungovernable. He famously wagged his finger in 1985 during his “crossing the Rubicon” speech, declaring to the opponents as well as to the friends of apartheid South Africa: “Don’t push us too far.”
