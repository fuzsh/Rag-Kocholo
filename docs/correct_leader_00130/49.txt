Leaders of the new South Africa paid at best a half-hearted tribute today to P.W. Botha, the country’s last hardline president of the apartheid era.

Mr Botha led South Africa’s white-minority government from 1978 to 1989, first as Prime Minister then as President. He died late last night from a stroke at his home in the seaside resort of Wilderness in the Western Cape, aged 90.

Nelson Mandela, who served about a decade of his 27-year prison term under Mr Botha’s rule, today issued a slightly guarded but nonetheless generous tribute to the former leader.
