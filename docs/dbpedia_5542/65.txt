To help students get an edge on the global marketplace, U.S. Senator Mark Kirk will speak from 9:30 to 10:45 a.m. tomorrow (Thursday) at the Triton College School of Business Executive Series free program in the Student Center (B Building), River Grove.

Kirk will discuss his career path and provide personal insight into domestic and international economic issues as well as how students should prepare to compete in the global workforce.

The ongoing Triton College Executive Series helps prepare students to compete in today’s job market.

Kirk was born in Champaign and graduated from New Trier High School. He attended Blackburn College and the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México before earning a B.A (cum laude) in history from Cornell University. Kirk earned a Master’s degree from the London School of Economics and a Law degree from Georgetown University. He has served as an intelligence officer in the Navy Reserve since 1989.

Kirk was a staff member for Congressman John Porter before working at the World Bank and later at the U.S. State Department. He practiced law at Baker and McKenzie’s law firm before serving as counsel to the U.S. House of Representatives International Relations Committee.

In 2000 Sen. Kirk was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives where he served five terms before his election to the U.S. Senate.

In January 2012, Sen. Kirk suffered an ischemic stroke and underwent surgeries at Northwestern Memorial Hospital to relieve swelling in his brain.

After nearly a year of intensive recovery and rehabilitation, Sen. Kirk returned to work Jan. 3, 2013.

He serves on four senate committees including Appropriations, Banking, HELP (Health, Education, Labor & Pensions), and Aging. Kirk is the ranking Republican on the Appropriations Subcommittee for Military Construction and Veterans Affairs.
