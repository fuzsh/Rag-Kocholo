Carillon Historical Park is a sprawling 65-acre park and museum located in Dayton, Ohio. The park is home to a variety of historic buildings and exhibits that delve into the history of technology and the evolution of Dayton and its residents from 1796 to the present day. As a visitor, you can explore the different sections of the park, each dedicated to a specific theme such as settlement, transportation, invention, and industry.

Attractions at Carillon Historical Park

Among the many attractions at Carillon Historical Park is the Carillon Park Railroad, a charming miniature railway that adds a touch of whimsy to the park. The Kettering Family Education Center is another highlight, offering changing exhibits that keep the experience fresh for repeat visitors. The center also features a video about the park and houses gift and snack shops where you can pick up a souvenir or grab a quick bite.

The John W. Berry Sr. Wright Brothers Aviation Center

The John W. Berry Sr. Wright Brothers Aviation Center is a key attraction at Carillon Historical Park. It houses the 1905 Wright Flyer III, recognized as the world's first practical airplane. This exhibit offers a unique opportunity to delve into the history of aviation and appreciate the pioneering work of the Wright Brothers.
