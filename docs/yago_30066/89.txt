Maria Theresa of Naples and Sicily (Maria Teresa Carolina Giuseppina; 6 June 1772 – 13 April 1807) was the first Empress of Austria and last Holy Roman Empress as the spouse of Francis II. She was born a Princess of Naples as the eldest daughter of King Ferdinand I of the Two Sicilies and Queen Maria Carolina and was named after her maternal grandmother Empress Maria Theresa.

Issue[edit]

Archduchess Maria Ludovika of Austria 12 December 1791 – 17 December 1847) married Napoleon and had issue.

Ferdinand I of Austria (19 April 1793 – 29 June 1875) married Maria Anna of Savoy but had no issue.

Archduchess Maria Leopoldina of Austria (22 January 1797 – 11 December 1826) married Pedro I of Brazil and had issue.

Archduchess Clementina of Austria (1 March 1798 – 3 September 1881) married Leopold, Prince of Salerno and had issue.

Archduke Joseph Franz of Austria (9 April 1799 – 30 June 1807) died in infancy.

Archduchess Marie Caroline of Austria (8 April 1801 – 22 May 1832) married Crown Prince Frederick Augustus of Saxony but had no issue.

Archduke Franz Karl of Austria (17 December 1802 – 8 March 1878) married Princess Sophie of Bavaria and had issue.

Archduchess Maria Anna of Austria (8 June 1804 – 28 December 1858) never married.
