Research and Exhibits at the Espera Archaeological Museum

The Espera Archaeological Museum is dedicated to sharing research and findings from the archaeological sites of Carissa Aurelia and Esperilla. It also features exhibits on the Iberian and Roman necropolises found within the municipality. Visitors can learn about these historical sites and the civilizations that once inhabited them through the museum's extensive collections.

Collections at the Espera Archaeological Museum

The Espera Archaeological Museum houses a variety of artifacts from the Esperilla site, including a series of sculptures dating from the 4th to 2nd centuries BC. The museum also features pieces from the Carissa Aurelia site, prehistoric materials, orientalizing elements from the 6th to 5th centuries BC, and Roman pottery from the 3rd to 1st centuries BC. These collections offer visitors a glimpse into the past and the diverse cultures that have shaped the region.
