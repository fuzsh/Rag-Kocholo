Mwali, also known by its French name as Mohéli, is the smallest island of the Comoros archipelago. It is situated to the northwest of Madagascar and due east of Mozambique in the Indian Ocean. It covers an area of 290 square kilometers (112 square miles) and has a population of around 38,000. Mwali has three official languages—Comorian, French and Arabic—but the great majority of its inhabitants are Comorians. The capital of the Island is Fomboni.
