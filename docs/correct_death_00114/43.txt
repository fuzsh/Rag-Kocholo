Biography

Charles Mingus Jr is Notable.

Charles Mingus was an American jazz double bassist, pianist, composer and bandleader, considered to be one of the greatest jazz musicians and composers in history.[1]

He was born in Nogales, Arizona in 1922, the son of Charles Mingus, Sr. and Harriet Sophia Phillips.[2] His mother died about six months after his birth in 1922,[3] and his father remarried the following year, to Mamie Newton Carson,[4] who raised him and his two older sisters. His ancestry is said to include German, African American, and Native American, as well as Chinese forebears.[1]

He was married four times. His wives were Jeanne Gross, Lucille (Celia) Germanis, Judy Starkey, and Susan Graham Ungaro.[1]
