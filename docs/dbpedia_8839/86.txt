Biography

* 2018 All-Missouri Valley Football Conference Second Team

* 2017 MVFC Commissioner's Academic Excellence Award

* 2015, 2016, 2018 MVFC Honor Roll

2018 SEASON (JUNIOR): All-Missouri Valley Football Conference second team fullback...Played in 14 of 15 games with six starts...Missed the national championship game due to an NCAA suspension...Finished the year with eight receptions for 46 yards and one touchdown, a 2-yard strike at South Dakota...Carried five times for 31 yards...Blocked for the No. 1 rushing attack in the MVFC averaging 286.2 yards per game (7th in FCS) and 6.4 yards per carry...Also provided protection on the PAT and field goal unit that went 83-for-85 on PAT kicks and 9-for-15 on field goals...Made the MVFC Honor Roll.

2017 SEASON (SOPHOMORE): Played in 14 of 15 games including starts against Mississippi Valley State and Wofford...Missed the Eastern Washington game in Week 2 with a foot injury...Caught four passes for 32 yards but did not have a carry...Key blocker for NDSU rushing attack that led the Missouri Valley Football Conference and ranked fourth nationally with 272.2 yards per game...Earned the MVFC Commissioner's Academic Excellence Award.

2016 SEASON (FRESHMAN): Played in 8 of 14 games, missing six games in the middle of the season with a broken foot...Made two starts against Iowa and South Dakota...Named to the MVFC Honor Roll.

2015 SEASON (REDSHIRT): Sat out the season as a redshirt in the Bison program...Named to the MVFC Honor Roll.

HIGH SCHOOL: Four-year starter who played linebacker and running back...Cavalier teams went 44-3 in his career under coach Sandy Laxdal with 9-man state titles in 2013 and 2014...North Dakota 9-man senior Athlete of the Year...Two-time all-state first team offense...All-state second team defense as a sophomore...Rushed for 2,081 yards and 41 TDs his final season...Totaled 177 tackles including 114 solo stops, 26 tackles for loss and 13 sacks on defense...Averaged 8.8 yards per carry over his career with 453 carries for 4,016 yards, 72 touchdowns and 51 PATs...Set Cavalier career records for scoring (534), solo tackles (307) and tackles for loss (72) and season records for scoring (280), solo tackles (114) and assisted tackles (89)...Also an all-state basketball player with more than 1,500 career points.

PERSONAL: Majoring in general agriculture...Son of Charles and Lori Robbins of Cavalier, N.D...Lori was NDSU's first women's basketball All-American in 1982 and was inducted into the Bison Athletic Hall of Fame in 1997...Has two older brothers...Brady played basketball at Mayville State and Spencer played football at Minnesota State Moorhead.
