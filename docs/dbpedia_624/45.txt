Name: Zhejiangosaurus lishuiensis

Name Meaning: Zhejiang Reptile

First Described: 2007

Described By: Lü et al.

Classification: Dinosauria, Ornithischia, Genasauria, Thyreophora, Eurypoda, Ankylosauria

Zhejiangosaurus is a ankylosaur from the Chaochuan Formation in Zhejiang, China, living ab out 100 to 93.9 million years ago, in the Cenomanian age of the Late Cretaceous. It is only known from a fragmentary partial skeleton, and it is a dubious taxon, thought to have been a Nodosaurid and then an Ankylosaurine at times.
