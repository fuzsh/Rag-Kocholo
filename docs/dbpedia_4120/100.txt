Fatih Terim, the Turkey coach, could remain in his post for another two years, according to Hasan Dogan, the Turkish football federation president.

It has been widely reported that Terim wanted a return to club management after Wednesday’s Euro 2008 semi-final loss to Germany. However, after the side’s success in Austria and Switzerland, the country’s federation are keen to keep the 54-year-old.

“We talked yesterday on the plane returning from Basle,” Dogan said. “At this moment in time Terim is at the helm of the national side and, God willing, he will be so until 2010.”
