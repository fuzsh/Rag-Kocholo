This was reported by the head of the National Union of Artists of Ukraine Kostyantyn Chernyavskyi to the publication "Local history".

It is known that the occupiers from Russia destroyed the museum on March 21 with a direct hit by an aerial bomb.

Although the originals of Kuindzhi's paintings were not in the building, the works of other famous Ukrainian artists were stored there, including Ivan Aivazovsky, Mykola Hlushchenko, Tetiana Yablonska, Mykhailo Deregus, Andrii Kotska, Mykola Bendryk, Leonid Gadi. And also modern painters - Vasyl Korenchuk, Oleksandr Bondarenko, Lyudmila Masalska, Oleg Kovalev, Volodymyr Kharakoz, Serhiy Barannyk.

The Kuindzhi Museum was opened in 2010 in the Geocintov Manor, which was built in 1902. The exposition of the institution contained about 2000 exhibits, including works of painting, decorative and applied arts, as well as graphics and sculpture.
