Collier Memorial State Park is a picturesque park located in southern Oregon, just 30 miles north of Klamath Falls. This beautiful park is nestled in the heart of the Cascade Mountains and offers visitors a tranquil and serene escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

Establishment and History

Collier Memorial State Park was established in 1945 as a memorial to the Collier brothers, Peter and Benjamin, who were instrumental in the development of the timber industry in the region. The park encompasses over 500 acres of pristine wilderness and is home to a variety of flora and fauna.

Activities and Attractions

There is no shortage of things to do and see at Collier Memorial State Park. Nature enthusiasts will delight in the abundance of hiking trails that wind through the park, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests. The park is also home to the Collier Logging Museum, which showcases the rich history of the timber industry in the area.

For those looking to cool off on a hot summer day, the park features a swimming area on the Williamson River. Visitors can take a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear waters or simply relax on the sandy beach.

Camping is a popular activity at Collier Memorial State Park, with several well-maintained campsites available for both tents and RVs. Each campsite is equipped with a picnic table and fire ring, perfect for enjoying a meal under the stars.

Wildlife and Nature

Collier Memorial State Park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. The park is home to a diverse range of animal species, including deer, elk, beavers, and a variety of bird species. Birdwatchers will be delighted by the opportunity to spot rare and migratory birds in their natural habitat.

The park is also known for its impressive old-growth ponderosa pine trees, some of which are over 400 years old. These majestic trees provide a stunning backdrop for picnics, hikes, and photography.

Visitor Information

Collier Memorial State Park is open year-round, although some facilities may have limited availability during the winter months. The park has a day-use fee, which can be paid at the entrance or purchased in advance online. Pets are welcome in the park but must be kept on a leash at all times.

For those looking to extend their stay, there are several nearby attractions worth exploring. Crater Lake National Park, one of the most iconic natural wonders in the United States, is just a short drive away. The city of Klamath Falls also offers a range of amenities, including restaurants, shops, and cultural attractions.
